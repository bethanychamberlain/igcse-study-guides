Movement In and Out of Cells

Pin this to your wall

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Diffusion Net movement of particles from high to low concentration. No energy needed. Osmosis (water-only, needs membrane)
Osmosis Net movement of water only through a partially permeable membrane, from dilute to concentrated. Diffusion (any particle, no membrane needed)
Active transport Movement from low to high concentration. Needs energy from respiration. Diffusion (goes the other way, no energy)
Concentration gradient The difference in concentration between two areas. Particles move "down" the gradient (high→low) in diffusion. "Against the gradient" = active transport
Partially permeable membrane A membrane with tiny holes — lets small molecules through (water), blocks big ones (sugar, protein). "Selectively permeable" (same thing, different name)
Turgid Plant cell swollen with water — firm and healthy. Cell wall stops it bursting. Lysis (animal cell bursting — no wall)
Plasmolysed Plant cell that's lost so much water the membrane pulls away from the cell wall. Crenated (same idea but in animal cells — shrivelled)
Flaccid Plant cell that's lost some water — soft and floppy, but not plasmolysed yet. Turgid (opposite — full of water, firm)

The Big Three — Side by Side

Diffusion Osmosis Active Transport
Direction High → Low Dilute → Conc. Low → High
Energy? No No Yes
What moves? Any particle Water only Any particle
Membrane? Not required Required Required
Go-to example O₂ into blood Water into roots Minerals into roots
Type Passive Passive Active
Memory Trick
Diffusion = Downhill (high to low, easy, free). Active transport = Against the flow (low to high, costs energy). Osmosis = Only water, Only through a membrane.

Say It in One Sentence

Diffusion Particles naturally spread from where there are lots to where there are few.
Osmosis Water sneaks through a membrane from the watery side to the less-watery side.
Active transport Cells burn energy to force particles uphill, from where there are few to where there are lots.
Turgid A plant cell so full of water it's firm like an inflated balloon.
Plasmolysed A plant cell that lost so much water the insides shrank away from the wall.

What Happens to Cells in Different Solutions

Pure Water (dilute) Concentrated Solution
Water moves... Into the cell Out of the cell
Animal cell Swells → bursts (lysis) Shrinks (crenated)
Plant cell Swells → turgid (wall stops burst) Shrinks → flaccid → plasmolysed
Exam Trap
Plant cells don't burst in water because of their cell wall. If they ask "Why don't plant cells burst?" the answer is always the cell wall.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"from high to low concentration" Diffusion (or osmosis if it says water + membrane)
"from low to high concentration" Active transport
"using energy" or "requires energy" Active transport
"water molecules" + "membrane" Osmosis
"why do root hair cells have mitochondria?" For active transport (energy from respiration)
"why don't plant cells burst?" Cell wall prevents it
"gas exchange in the lungs" Diffusion of O₂ and CO₂
