Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Cell surface membrane Thin layer around the cytoplasm. Controls what enters and exits the cell. Cell wall (thick, rigid, cellulose — plant cells only)
Cytoplasm Jelly-like substance where chemical reactions happen. Contains water, dissolved substances, and organelles. —
Nucleus Contains DNA (genes on chromosomes). Controls the cell's activities and characteristics. Nucleolus (a structure inside the nucleus — different thing)
Chloroplast Contains chlorophyll. Site of photosynthesis. Only in plant cells (green parts). Mitochondria (respiration, not photosynthesis)
Mitochondria Site of aerobic respiration. Releases energy for the cell. Has folded inner membrane. Chloroplast (makes food from light — mitochondria release energy from food)
Cellulose cell wall Rigid outer layer in plant cells. Provides support. Freely permeable (lets everything through). Cell membrane (thin, selective, present in ALL cells)
Vacuole In plants: large, permanent, filled with cell sap, provides turgor. In animals: small and temporary. Vesicle (tiny transport bubble, not the large plant vacuole)
Ribosome Tiny structure on rough ER. Site of protein synthesis. —
Tissue A group of cells with similar structure and function working together. Organ (made of DIFFERENT tissues working together)
Magnification How many times bigger the image is compared to the real object. Formula: M = Measured ÷ Actual. Resolution (ability to see detail — different from making it bigger)

Memory Trick — Plant Cells Have 3 Extras
Plant cells have everything animal cells have, PLUS three things: Cell wall, Chloroplasts, large permanent vaCuole. Three C's for plants.

Specialised Cells — Structure Linked to Function

Cell Job Key Adaptation
Red blood cell Carry oxygen No nucleus (more haemoglobin), biconcave disc (large surface area)
Ciliated cell Move mucus Tiny hairs (cilia) beat to push mucus along the airways
Root hair cell Absorb water/minerals Long thin extension (huge surface area for absorption)
Xylem vessel Transport water No cytoplasm, no end walls (continuous tube), lignin-thickened walls
Palisade cell Photosynthesis Packed with chloroplasts, near top of leaf where light is strongest
Exam Trap — Red Blood Cells Have No Nucleus
Don't write "all cells have a nucleus." Red blood cells are the exception — they have no nucleus to make room for more haemoglobin so they carry more oxygen.

Say It in One Sentence

Cell membrane The bouncer at the door — it decides what gets into the cell and what stays out.
Nucleus The boss of the cell — it holds the DNA instructions that tell the cell what to do.
Mitochondria The cell's batteries — they release energy through aerobic respiration.
Chloroplast Solar panels for plants — they trap light energy to make glucose by photosynthesis.
Levels of organisation Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ System → Organism (smallest to largest).
Division of labour Different cells do different jobs, so each part becomes really efficient at its specific task.

Memory Tricks

Starch vs Glycogen — Who Stores What?
Plants store Starch. Animals store Glycogen. Think: PS → AG. Or: "Plants are Starchy, Animals are Glyco-powered."
MAM Triangle for Magnification
Put M (measured size) on top, A (actual size) and M (magnification) on the bottom. Cover what you want to find — the other two show the calculation. Always convert to the same units first (1 mm = 1000 µm).
Why Does This Cell Need Lots of Mitochondria?
If an exam question asks "Why does [cell] have many mitochondria?", the answer is always: it has a high rate of metabolic activity and needs a lot of energy from respiration. Works for muscle cells, liver cells, sperm cells.
Shape Tells You Which Cell
Irregular shape = animal cell (no cell wall). Regular box shape = plant cell (cell wall holds the shape). This works on exam diagrams every time.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"State the function of the cell membrane" Controls what enters and exits the cell; separates cell contents from surroundings
"State the function of mitochondria" Site of aerobic respiration — releases energy for the cell
"Name a structure found in plant cells but not animal cells" Cell wall, chloroplasts, or large permanent vacuole (any one)
"How is the red blood cell adapted?" No nucleus = more haemoglobin; biconcave = large surface area; flexible = fits through capillaries
"How is the root hair cell adapted?" Long thin extension = large surface area for absorbing water and minerals
"Put these in order: organ, cell, tissue, organism" Cell → Tissue → Organ → Organ System → Organism
"Calculate the magnification" (gives measured + actual size) M = Measured ÷ Actual. Convert to same units first (1 mm = 1000 µm)
"Why does a liver cell have many mitochondria?" High metabolic rate → needs lots of energy from aerobic respiration
