Reproduction

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Gamete A sex cell (sperm or ovum) that is haploid — has half the normal number of chromosomes. Zygote (that's a fertilised egg — diploid)
Fertilisation The fusion of a male gamete nucleus with a female gamete nucleus to form a zygote. Pollination (that's just pollen landing on the stigma — no fusion yet)
Pollination Transfer of pollen from an anther to a stigma. Fertilisation (pollination comes FIRST; fertilisation is the actual fusion of nuclei)
Ovule Structure inside the ovary of a flower that contains the female gamete. Becomes the seed after fertilisation. Ovary (the ovary contains the ovule; the ovary becomes the fruit)
Germination The development of a seed into a new young plant. Needs water, oxygen, and suitable temperature. Pollination or fertilisation (germination happens to seeds, not flowers)
Placenta Disc-shaped organ connecting fetus to uterus wall via the umbilical cord. Exchanges nutrients, oxygen, and waste. —
Implantation Embedding of the embryo into the uterus lining, about 6 days after fertilisation. Fertilisation (fertilisation happens in the oviduct; implantation happens in the uterus)
Ovulation Release of a mature ovum from the ovary on Day 14 of the menstrual cycle, triggered by a surge of LH. Menstruation (that's the shedding of the uterus lining — Days 1–5)
Oestrogen Hormone from the ovary that repairs and thickens the uterus lining during Days 5–14. Progesterone (that maintains the lining AFTER ovulation)
Progesterone Hormone from the corpus luteum that maintains the uterus lining and inhibits FSH. Drop in progesterone triggers menstruation. Oestrogen (oestrogen builds up the lining; progesterone maintains it)

Sexual vs Asexual Reproduction

Sexual Asexual
Parents Two (male + female) One
Gametes? Yes No
Fertilisation? Yes No
Offspring Genetically different Genetically identical (clones)
Cell division Meiosis (to make gametes) Mitosis
Advantage Variation → adaptation Fast, only one parent needed
Disadvantage Slower, needs two parents No variation → all vulnerable to same disease

Insect-Pollinated vs Wind-Pollinated Flowers

Insect-Pollinated Wind-Pollinated
Petals Large, bright, scented Small, dull, no scent
Pollen Sticky, large grains, small amounts Light, smooth grains, huge amounts
Anthers Stiff, inside flower Dangling outside flower
Stigma Flat, inside flower Long, feathery, hanging outside
Nectar? Yes No

Say It in One Sentence

After fertilisation The ovule becomes a seed and the ovary becomes a fruit — learn this pair, it's guaranteed marks.
Pollen tube After pollination, a tube grows down through the style so the male gamete can reach and fuse with the female gamete in the ovule.
Sperm features Tail for swimming, many mitochondria for energy, acrosome with enzymes to penetrate the egg.
Placenta rule Maternal and fetal blood NEVER mix — materials cross by diffusion across a thin membrane.
Menstrual cycle 28-day cycle: menstruation (Days 1–5), repair (5–14), ovulation (Day 14), maintenance (14–28).
HIV vs AIDS HIV is the virus; AIDS is the condition that develops later when the immune system fails — they are not the same thing.
Oxytocin Hormone from the pituitary gland that triggers uterus contractions during labour.

Memory Tricks

Germination Needs WOT
Water, Oxygen, Temperature. Light is NOT needed for germination — that's a classic wrong answer.
Ovule → Seed, Ovary → Fruit
Think: the small thing (ovule) becomes the small thing (seed). The big thing (ovary) becomes the big thing (fruit). Small → small, big → big.
Oestrogen = Builds, Progesterone = Protects
Oestrogen = On (builds it up). Progesterone = Preserves (keeps the lining maintained). When progesterone drops, the lining sheds.
LH = Launch
LH = Launches the egg. A surge of LH on Day 14 triggers ovulation (the egg is "launched" from the ovary).

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"What is the difference between pollination and fertilisation?" Pollination = pollen lands on stigma. Fertilisation = fusion of male and female gamete nuclei. Pollination comes first.
"Why do sperm have many mitochondria?" To release energy (by respiration) for swimming / movement of the tail
"State the conditions needed for germination" Water, oxygen, suitable temperature. NOT light.
"What happens to the ovule / ovary after fertilisation?" Ovule → seed. Ovary → fruit. Petals, stamens, stigma, style wither.
"Why should a pregnant woman not smoke?" Nicotine crosses the placenta → reduces oxygen in blood → less oxygen for fetus → lower birth weight
"Do maternal and fetal blood mix?" No. Materials cross by diffusion across the placenta membrane.
"Where does fertilisation occur in humans?" In the oviduct (Fallopian tube), usually the first third.
"Explain why clones are vulnerable to disease" All genetically identical → no variation → if one is affected, all will be. Example: Irish Potato Famine.
