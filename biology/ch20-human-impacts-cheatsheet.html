Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Deforestation The rapid destruction of woodland, usually to create farmland, get timber, or access mineral resources. —
Monoculture Growing a single crop over a large area. Efficient but reduces biodiversity and makes crops vulnerable to disease. —
Eutrophication The process where excess nutrients (nitrates/phosphates) enter water, causing algal blooms, oxygen depletion, and death of aquatic organisms. "Fertiliser poisons fish" (it's indirect — the chain is: fertiliser → algae → bacteria → no oxygen → fish die)
BOD Biological oxygen demand — the mass of oxygen consumed by microorganisms in a water sample. High BOD = polluted water. —
Bioaccumulation Concentration of a pesticide increases at each trophic level in a food chain because it is stored in tissues, not broken down. —
Greenhouse effect CO₂ and methane trap infrared radiation near the Earth's surface, warming the planet. Acid rain (that's caused by SO₂ and nitrogen oxides, not CO₂)
Acid rain Rain made acidic by SO₂ and nitrogen oxides from burning fossil fuels dissolving in rainwater. Greenhouse effect (different gases, different problem)
Sustainable development Using resources at a rate that allows them to be replaced, so future generations can also use them. —
Conservation Protecting and managing ecosystems and species to maintain biodiversity. Preservation (keeping something completely unchanged — conservation allows managed use)
Endangered species A species at risk of extinction due to habitat loss, over-hunting, pollution, introduced species, or climate change. —

Greenhouse Effect vs Acid Rain

Greenhouse Effect Acid Rain
Gases CO₂ and methane SO₂ and nitrogen oxides
Source Burning fossil fuels, cattle, rice fields Burning fossil fuels (power stations, cars)
Effect Global warming, sea level rise, extreme weather Acidic lakes (fish die), forest damage, soil mineral loss
Solutions Reduce fossil fuels, alternative energy, replant forests Scrubbers, catalytic converters, add limestone to lakes
Exam Trap
Both are caused by burning fossil fuels, but by DIFFERENT gases. CO₂ = greenhouse effect. SO₂ / nitrogen oxides = acid rain. Don't mix them up in an answer.

Say It in One Sentence

Deforestation Fewer trees = less photosynthesis = more CO₂ in atmosphere + soil erosion + flooding + species extinction.
Intensive farming More food per area, but at the cost of pollution, animal welfare issues, and antibiotic-resistant bacteria.
Eutrophication chain Fertiliser → algal bloom → blocks light → plants die → bacteria multiply → use up oxygen → fish die.
Bioaccumulation Pesticide concentration increases up the food chain because it's stored in body fat, not broken down.
Net mesh size Larger meshes let young fish escape so they can grow and reproduce — key to sustainable fishing.
Hedgerow benefits Windbreaks, nesting sites for pest-eating birds, breeding sites for pollinating insects, and prevent soil erosion.

Memory Tricks

Eutrophication — Learn the Chain
Fertiliser → Algae → Light blocked → Plants die → Bacteria boom → Oxygen drops → Fish die. F-A-L-P-B-O-F. Each step = 1 mark on a 5–6 mark question. Don't just write "fertiliser kills fish" — you need ALL the steps.
Deforestation = Four S's
Soil erosion, Species extinction, Surplus CO₂ (less photosynthesis), Surface water flooding. Four consequences, all starting with S.
Conservation Steps
Sample → Manage → Carry out → Re-sample. "SMCR" — Sample, Manage, Carry out, Resample.
Fish Farming Has Downsides Too
Don't assume fish farming is perfect. Excess food and faeces cause eutrophication below the pens. Pesticides kill wild organisms. Always give both sides in a "discuss" answer.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Describe the process of eutrophication" Excess nitrates → algal bloom → light blocked → plants die → bacteria multiply → oxygen depleted → fish/invertebrates die
"Give three consequences of deforestation" Soil erosion, rising CO₂ (less photosynthesis), species extinction, flooding — pick any three
"What is the greenhouse effect?" CO₂ and methane trap infrared radiation near Earth's surface, causing global warming
"How are acid rain effects reduced?" Scrubbers in power stations, catalytic converters in cars, crushed limestone added to acidified lakes
"Why is bioaccumulation dangerous to top predators?" Pesticide concentration increases at each trophic level — top predators get a lethal dose even if water concentration was tiny
"Define sustainable development" Using resources at a rate that allows them to be replaced, so future generations can use them too
"Give two methods of conserving fish stocks" Larger net mesh sizes (lets young fish escape), fishing quotas (limits on catch), restocking from fish farms
"What does a high BOD indicate?" Polluted water — lots of bacteria consuming oxygen for respiration
