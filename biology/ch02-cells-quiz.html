Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Name three structures that are found in both animal and plant cells.
3 marks
Answer
Cell surface membrane [1], cytoplasm [1], nucleus [1].
These are the only three that appear in BOTH types. Do not write "cell wall" — that is plant cells only.

2
Name three structures found in plant cells but not in animal cells.
3 marks
Answer
Cellulose cell wall [1], large permanent vacuole [1], chloroplasts [1].
You could also accept "starch granules" as a plant-only feature. But the top three to learn are cell wall, vacuole, chloroplasts.

3
State the function of the nucleus.
2 marks
Answer
The nucleus contains the genetic material (DNA / genes on chromosomes) [1] which carries the coded instructions that control the activities and characteristics of the cell [1].
Two marks, two ideas: (1) it contains DNA/genetic material, and (2) this controls what the cell does. Just saying "it controls the cell" only gets one mark — you need to mention genes or DNA for the second.

4
Arrange these terms in order from smallest to largest: organ, cell, organism, organelle, tissue, system.
3 marks
Answer
Organelle → cell → tissue → organ → system → organism [3 marks for all correct; deduct 1 for each swap needed].
The most common mistake is mixing up tissue and organ. Remember: a tissue is one type of cell grouped together; an organ is several different tissues combined.

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY or HOW, not just name things.
5
State the function of mitochondria. Explain why muscle cells contain many mitochondria.
3 marks
Answer
Mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration [1], which releases energy [1]. Muscle cells contain many mitochondria because they need a large amount of energy for contraction / movement [1].
The chain: mitochondria → respiration → energy → needed for contraction. Each link is a mark point. Don't just say "they need energy" — say what the energy is used for.

6
A root hair cell absorbs water and minerals from the soil. Describe two features of the root hair cell that make it well adapted for this function.
4 marks
Answer
1. It has a long, thin root hair extension [1] which increases the surface area for absorption of water and minerals [1].
2. It has a thin cell wall [1] so that water and dissolved minerals can pass through easily / the distance for diffusion is short [1].
For every adaptation question, you need TWO parts: name the feature AND explain how it helps. "It has a large surface area" alone only gets 1 mark. "...which increases absorption" gets the second mark.

7
Red blood cells have no nucleus. Explain how this is an adaptation for their function of transporting oxygen.
3 marks
Answer
Red blood cells have no nucleus [1], which leaves more space / room inside the cell [1] to be filled with haemoglobin, allowing the cell to carry more oxygen [1].
The logic: no nucleus → more space → more haemoglobin → more oxygen carried. You can also mention the biconcave shape (large surface area for gas exchange) as a bonus adaptation if the question asks for more than one.

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning. This is where exam grades are won or lost.
8
A student looks at a liver cell and a palisade cell from a leaf under a microscope. Compare these two cells by stating three similarities and three differences between them. For each difference, explain why the difference exists.
6 marks
Answer
Similarities (any 3): Both have a cell surface membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus, mitochondria [1 each, max 3].

Differences:
• The palisade cell has a cellulose cell wall but the liver cell does not [1] — the cell wall provides structural support since plants cannot move to avoid damage.
• The palisade cell has chloroplasts but the liver cell does not [1] — chloroplasts are needed for photosynthesis because plants make their own food (autotrophic nutrition).
• The palisade cell has a large permanent vacuole but the liver cell does not (only small temporary vacuoles) [1] — the vacuole maintains turgor pressure to keep the plant upright.
The key to full marks is explaining WHY the difference exists, not just listing it. Link the structural difference to the lifestyle difference: plants are autotrophic (make food), animals are heterotrophic (eat food). That's the underlying reason for every plant vs animal difference.

9
Explain what is meant by division of labour in a multicellular organism. Use the example of the digestive system to describe how cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems are organised.
5 marks
Answer
Division of labour means that different cells, tissues and organs are specialised to perform different functions [1], so each part can work efficiently at its own job [1].

Example: Epithelial cells group together to form epithelial tissue which lines the intestine [1]. Different tissues (epithelial, muscle, blood) combine to form an organ such as the small intestine [1]. The small intestine works with other organs (stomach, liver, pancreas) to form the digestive system (organ system), which breaks down and absorbs food [1].
Walk through each level with a concrete example. The examiner wants to see: cells → tissue → organ → organ system, with a named example at each level. The digestive system is the safest example because every student has seen it.

10
A xylem vessel is a specialised plant cell. Describe three adaptations of the xylem vessel and explain how each adaptation helps it carry out its function of transporting water.
6 marks
Answer
1. Xylem vessels have no end walls between cells [1], so many cells join together to form a continuous hollow tube through which water can flow without obstruction [1].

2. Xylem vessels have no cytoplasm [1], which means there is nothing inside the tube to slow down the flow of water [1].

3. The walls are strengthened with a waterproof substance called lignin [1], which prevents the vessel from collapsing under pressure and stops water leaking out sideways, as well as providing structural support for the plant [1].
This is the pattern for every adaptation question: name the feature [1 mark] then explain how it helps the function [1 mark]. Three adaptations, two marks each = 6 marks. If you only describe the feature without explaining why it matters, you lose half the marks.
