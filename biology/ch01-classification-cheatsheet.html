Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Species A group of organisms that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring. Population (same species in one area, not the definition of species itself)
Classification Placing organisms into groups based on their observable characteristics. —
Binomial nomenclature Two-part scientific name: Genus species. Capital G, lowercase s, always in italics. Common names (those vary by country — scientific names are universal)
Dichotomous key An identification tool using yes/no questions that branch into two choices each time. —
Heterotroph An organism that feeds on organic molecules made by other organisms (animals, fungi). Autotroph (makes its own food)
Autotroph An organism that makes its own food, usually by photosynthesis (plants). Heterotroph (eats food from others)
Saprotrophic Feeding by secreting enzymes onto dead matter and absorbing the digested products (fungi). Parasitic (feeds on LIVING organisms)
Excretion Removal of toxic waste products of metabolism (e.g. CO₂, urea). Egestion (removing undigested food — faeces were never inside cells)
Prokaryote Organism with no true nucleus and no membrane-bound organelles (bacteria). Protoctist (HAS a nucleus)
Ectothermic Body temperature depends on the environment (fish, amphibians, reptiles). Endothermic (controls own body temperature — birds, mammals)

Exam Trap — Fungi vs Plants
Fungi are NOT plants. Fungi have no chlorophyll, their cell walls are chitin (not cellulose), and they feed by absorbing digested dead matter. These three differences are what the exam wants.

Bacteria vs Viruses

Bacteria Virus
Living? Yes — shows all 7 characteristics No — only active inside a host
A cell? Yes (cytoplasm, wall, membrane) No — just DNA/RNA + protein coat
Reproduction On its own (binary fission) Only inside a host cell
Treatment Antibiotics Antibiotics do NOT work

Say It in One Sentence

MRS GREN The 7 life characteristics: Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition.
Hierarchy Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species (groups get smaller, organisms get more similar).
Virus Not alive — just a piece of DNA or RNA in a protein coat that hijacks living cells to copy itself.
Arthropods Invertebrates with a hard exoskeleton, jointed limbs, and a segmented body.
Insects 3 body sections, 6 legs, usually wings — don't call a spider an insect (that's an arachnid: 2 sections, 8 legs).
Vertebrates Animals with a backbone: fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals — tell them apart by skin covering.
Saprotrophic Fungi secrete enzymes onto dead matter, then absorb the dissolved nutrients — external digestion.

Memory Tricks

MRS GREN — 7 Characteristics of Life
Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition. Say it out loud until you can rattle off all seven with definitions.
King Philip Came Over For Good Spaghetti
Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species. As you go down: fewer organisms in each group, more features in common.
Skin = Shortcut for Vertebrate Class
Wet scales = Fish. Smooth moist skin = Amphibian. Dry scales = Reptile. Feathers = Bird. Hair/fur = Mammal. Spot the skin, name the class.
Pro-karyote = "Before Nucleus"
"Pro" = before, "karyon" = nucleus. Prokaryotes evolved before cells with nuclei existed. So prokaryotes = NO nucleus. If the cell has a nucleus, it's NOT a prokaryote.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Define species" A group of organisms that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring (both parts = 2 marks)
"List the characteristics of living organisms" MRS GREN: Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition
"Difference between excretion and egestion" Excretion = waste from metabolism (CO₂, urea). Egestion = removing undigested food (faeces)
"Why are fungi not plants?" No chlorophyll, cell wall is chitin not cellulose, feed by saprotrophic nutrition not photosynthesis
"Why are viruses not in any kingdom?" They are not cells, cannot reproduce on their own, show none of the 7 life characteristics outside a host
"Difference between bacteria and viruses" Bacteria are cells and reproduce alone; viruses are not cells and can only reproduce inside a host
"How many legs does an insect have?" 6 legs, 3 body sections. (Arachnids = 8 legs, 2 body sections — spiders are NOT insects)
"Identify this vertebrate" (exam gives description) Check the skin covering first: wet scales, dry scales, moist skin, feathers, or hair/fur
