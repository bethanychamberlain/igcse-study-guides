Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Write the word equation for photosynthesis. Include everything needed for full marks.
2 marks
Answer
carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen [1]
with light energy and chlorophyll shown above/below the arrow [1].
You MUST include "light energy" and "chlorophyll." Without them you only get 1 mark. Do not write "sugar" instead of "glucose."

2
What is the green pigment in plants that absorbs light energy? In which organelle is it found?
2 marks
Answer
The pigment is chlorophyll [1]. It is found in chloroplasts [1].
Don't mix these up. Chlorophyll = the pigment (substance). Chloroplast = the organelle (structure). Two different things.

3
Name two mineral ions that plants need, and state the function of each.
2 marks
Answer
Nitrate ions — needed to make amino acids and proteins [1].
Magnesium ions — needed to make chlorophyll [1].
Name + function = 1 mark each. "Nitrate for growth" is too vague — say "amino acids/proteins."

What solution is used to test for starch in a leaf, and what colour change indicates starch is present?
2 marks
Answer
Iodine solution [1]. Starch is present if the colour changes from brown/orange to blue-black [1].
Don't write "black" alone — "blue-black" is the expected answer. And iodine tests for starch, NOT glucose (that's Benedict's).

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY, not just name things.
5
Name four layers of a leaf, in order from top to bottom, and give one adaptation of each for photosynthesis.
4 marks
Answer
Upper epidermis — transparent, lets light pass through to palisade cells [1].
Palisade mesophyll — packed with many chloroplasts for maximum light absorption [1].
Spongy mesophyll — has air spaces for diffusion of CO₂ and O₂ through the leaf [1].
Lower epidermis — contains stomata which allow CO₂ in and O₂ out [1].
Layer + adaptation = 1 mark each. You can also include waxy cuticle (reduces water loss, transparent) or vein (xylem delivers water, phloem removes glucose) for credit.

6
Explain the role of guard cells in controlling gas exchange in a leaf.
3 marks
Answer
Guard cells surround stomata (pores in the lower epidermis) [1]. When the plant has plenty of water, guard cells become turgid and curve apart, opening the stoma to allow CO₂ in for photosynthesis [1]. When the plant is short of water, guard cells become flaccid and the stoma closes, reducing water loss [1].
Three marks for three ideas: guard cells control stomata, turgid = open, flaccid = closed. Use the words "turgid" and "flaccid" — they score marks.

7
Define the term limiting factor. Name the three main limiting factors of photosynthesis.
3 marks
Answer
A limiting factor is the factor that is in shortest supply and therefore restricts the rate of the overall process [1]. The three main limiting factors are: light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, and temperature [1 for all three named; 1 if only one or two named].
The definition must include "shortest supply" or "nearest to its minimum value." Just saying "a factor that affects the rate" is too vague.

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning.
8
A plant with variegated (green and white) leaves was destarched and then left in bright light for 8 hours. The leaf was tested for starch. (a) Describe how you would test the leaf for starch. [3] (b) What results would you expect, and what do they show? [3]
6 marks
Answer
(a) Starch test procedure:
Boil the leaf in water to kill the cells and break down cell walls [1]. Warm the leaf in ethanol to remove the green chlorophyll [1]. Dip in warm water to soften, then place on a white tile and add iodine solution [1].

(b) Expected results:
The green parts of the leaf will turn blue-black (starch present) [1]. The white parts will turn brown/orange (no starch) [1]. This shows that chlorophyll is needed for photosynthesis — only the parts with chlorophyll could produce glucose and store it as starch [1].
Part (a): three steps for three marks. Part (b): describe both results AND give the conclusion. "Chlorophyll is needed for photosynthesis" is the key conclusion the examiner wants.

9
A greenhouse grower wants to maximise the yield of tomato plants. Explain three things the grower could do, and for each one, explain the biological reason why it would increase the yield.
6 marks
Answer
1. Increase CO₂ concentration (e.g. burn paraffin or release from cylinders) [1] — CO₂ is a substrate for photosynthesis, so more CO₂ increases the rate of photosynthesis and more glucose is produced for growth [1].

2. Add artificial lighting [1] — light provides the energy for photosynthesis; increasing intensity or duration means more photosynthesis can occur [1].

3. Control temperature with heaters [1] — temperature affects the rate of enzyme-controlled reactions in photosynthesis; keeping it at the optimum ensures the fastest rate without denaturing enzymes [1].
Each point needs: what to do (1 mark) + why it works biologically (1 mark). "Add more light to help the plant" is too vague — link it to the rate of photosynthesis specifically.

10
Two groups of seedlings were grown in solutions that were deficient in two different ions. Group M was grown without nitrate ions. Group N was grown without magnesium ions. Group O received all the ions that plants require. (a) Describe and explain the expected appearance of each group. [4] (b) Explain why Group O was included. [2]
6 marks
Answer
(a) Group M (no nitrate): Reduced/stunted growth because nitrate is needed to make amino acids and proteins for growth [1]. Leaves turn yellow, especially lower leaves [1].

Group N (no magnesium): Leaves turn pale green or yellow (chlorosis) because magnesium is needed to make chlorophyll [1]. Growth is severely reduced because without chlorophyll, less photosynthesis can occur [1].

(b) Group O (control): Group O is the control experiment [1]. It provides a comparison to show that any differences in growth in M and N are due to the missing mineral ion and not some other factor [1].
Part (a): for each group, describe the symptom AND explain WHY (link to what the mineral does). Part (b): the word "control" is worth a mark, and explaining its purpose (fair comparison) is the second mark.
