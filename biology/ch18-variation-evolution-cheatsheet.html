Variation and Evolution

Pin this to your wall

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Continuous variation A range of values with intermediates (e.g. height, body mass). Caused by genes AND environment. Bell curve graph. Discontinuous (clear-cut categories, no intermediates)
Discontinuous variation Clear-cut categories with no intermediates (e.g. blood group, sex). Caused by genes only. Bar chart graph. Continuous (a range with in-between values)
Mutation A change in the base sequence of DNA (gene mutation) or in chromosome number (chromosome mutation). Variation (mutation is one CAUSE of variation, not variation itself)
Mutagen A factor that increases the rate of mutation. E.g. UV light, gamma rays, chemicals in tobacco smoke. Carcinogen (a mutagen that specifically causes cancer)
Adaptation The way an organism is suited to survive in its environment. —
Natural selection Organisms with the best-suited characteristics are more likely to survive, reproduce, and pass on their genes. Artificial selection (that's when humans choose which organisms breed)
Artificial selection When humans choose organisms with desirable traits and breed them together (selective breeding). Genetic engineering (that's directly changing DNA, not traditional breeding)
Evolution The change in a species over time through natural selection. —
Fitness How well-suited an organism is to survive and reproduce in its environment. Physical fitness (in biology, fitness = reproductive success)

Continuous vs Discontinuous Variation

Continuous Discontinuous
Intermediates? Yes — full range No — distinct categories
Caused by Genes AND environment Genes only
Graph type Smooth bell curve Bar chart
Can you measure it? Yes (quantitative) No (qualitative — categories)
Examples Height, body mass, hand span Blood group, sex, eye colour

Natural vs Artificial Selection

Natural Selection Artificial Selection
Agent The natural environment Humans
Speed Slow (many generations) Much quicker
Purpose Survival in the wild Benefit to humans
Effect on variation Maintains variation Reduces variation (danger!)

Say It in One Sentence

Phenotype equation Phenotype = Genotype + Environment — your traits come from BOTH your genes AND your surroundings.
Gene mutation A change in the base sequence of DNA that may produce a different protein (or no protein at all).
Crossing over Homologous chromosomes swap segments during meiosis, creating new allele combinations.
Darwin's finches Finches on different Galapagos islands had different beaks adapted to different foods — evidence for natural selection.
Antibiotic resistance Bacteria with a resistance mutation survive antibiotics, reproduce, and pass it on — natural selection happening now.
New species Two populations separated → different selection pressures → accumulate different adaptations → can no longer interbreed = new species.

Memory Tricks

Natural Selection — 5-Step Chain
Over-produce → Vary → Struggle → Survive → Pass on. Each step = 1 mark on a 5-mark question. Memorise: "OVSSP."
Continuous = Can Measure
If you can put a number on it (height = 165 cm), it's continuous. If you can only put a label on it (blood group = A), it's discontinuous.
Darwin vs Lamarck
Lamarck = "You stretched your neck, so your babies will have long necks" (WRONG). Darwin = "Some giraffes randomly had longer necks, and those survived better" (RIGHT). Lamarck thinks the environment causes changes; Darwin says it selects from existing ones.
Artificial Selection Reduces Variation
Think of it like a funnel: each generation you pick only the "best" few, so the gene pool gets narrower and narrower. Narrow gene pool = vulnerable to disease (Irish Potato Famine again).

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Explain how natural selection leads to evolution" 5 steps: overproduction, variation, struggle for existence, survival of the fittest, advantageous traits passed on
"Why do identical twins raised apart look slightly different?" Same genotype but different environments — phenotype = genotype + environment
"How does antibiotic resistance develop?" Random mutation → resistant bacteria survive antibiotic → reproduce → resistance spreads through population
"What is a mutagen? Give an example." A factor that increases mutation rate. Examples: UV light, gamma rays, tar in cigarette smoke
"Describe how to selectively breed sheep for thicker wool" Choose sheep with thickest wool → breed them → select best offspring → repeat over many generations
"Is height continuous or discontinuous?" Continuous — range of values, can be measured, influenced by genes AND environment
"Give a disadvantage of artificial selection" Reduces genetic variation — all organisms similar, so one disease could wipe them all out
"Name three sources of genetic variation" Crossing over, independent assortment, random fertilisation (all happen during meiosis / sexual reproduction)
