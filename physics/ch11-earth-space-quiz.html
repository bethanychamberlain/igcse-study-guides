Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
What causes day and night on Earth?
1 mark
Answer
The Earth rotates (spins) on its axis once every 24 hours [1]. Half the Earth faces the Sun (day) and half faces away (night).
The key word is "rotates" or "spins on its axis." Do NOT say "the Earth orbits the Sun" — that takes a year and causes the seasons, not day/night.

What is a light year?
1 mark
Answer
A light year is the distance that light travels in one year [1].
You MUST say "distance." If you say "time" you get zero. A light year is a unit of distance, equal to about 9.5 x 1012 km.

Why can we see the Moon from Earth?
1 mark
Answer
The Moon reflects sunlight [1]. It does not produce its own light.
Same applies to planets — we only see them because they reflect the Sun's light.

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY, not just name things.
5
Explain why it is summer in the Northern Hemisphere in June. Give two reasons why temperatures are higher.
4 marks
Answer
In June, the Northern Hemisphere is tilted towards the Sun because of the 23.5° tilt of the Earth's axis [1].

Reason 1: The Sun's rays hit the surface more directly (less spread out), so more energy is concentrated on each square metre [1].
Reason 2: There are more hours of daylight, so the surface receives energy for longer each day [1].

It is NOT because the Earth is closer to the Sun [1].
You MUST mention the tilt. Then give both reasons: more direct sunlight AND longer days. Saying "closer to the Sun" will cost you marks.

6
Describe the life cycle of a star similar in mass to our Sun, from nebula to its final stage.
4 marks
Answer
A cloud of gas and dust called a nebula collapses under gravity [1].
It forms a protostar, which heats up until nuclear fusion begins, becoming a main sequence star [1].
When the hydrogen fuel runs low, the star expands and cools to become a red giant [1].
The outer layers drift away and the core remains as a hot, dense white dwarf, which eventually cools and fades [1].
The sequence: nebula → protostar → main sequence → red giant → white dwarf. Each stage is roughly one mark. For massive stars, the end stages are different (supernova → neutron star/black hole).

7
What is a geostationary orbit? Explain why communications satellites are placed in this type of orbit.
3 marks
Answer
A geostationary orbit has a period of 24 hours, matching the Earth's rotation [1]. The satellite stays above the same point on the Earth's surface at all times [1].

Communications satellites use this orbit so that ground-based dish aerials can point at the satellite in a fixed direction without needing to track its movement [1].
Three marks: period = 24 hours, stays above same point, and the practical reason (fixed dish direction). The orbit is 35,900 km above the equator.

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning.
8
Scientists believe the Universe is expanding and that it started with the Big Bang. State two pieces of evidence that support this theory and explain how each one supports it.
6 marks
Answer
Evidence 1 — Red shift:
Light from distant galaxies is shifted towards the red end of the spectrum [1]. This shows that galaxies are moving away from us [1]. The more distant the galaxy, the greater the red shift, which means the Universe is expanding [1].

Evidence 2 — Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMBR):
Microwave radiation has been detected coming from every direction in space [1]. This is thought to be the remnant radiation from the Big Bang, which has cooled and stretched as the Universe expanded [1]. Its existence is consistent with predictions of the Big Bang theory [1].
This is a classic 4-6 mark question. Name both pieces of evidence, then for each one: state what it is AND explain how it supports the Big Bang. Just naming "red shift" without explaining it = 1 mark instead of 3.

9
Compare the fate of a medium-mass star (like the Sun) with a very massive star after each reaches the red giant/supergiant stage. Describe the final stages for each.
5 marks
Answer
Medium-mass star (like the Sun):
After the red giant stage, the outer layers drift away [1]. The remaining core becomes a white dwarf — a small, hot, dense object that gradually cools and fades [1].

Very massive star:
After the red supergiant stage, the star collapses and then explodes in a supernova — a huge nuclear explosion [1]. The remaining core becomes either a neutron star (extremely dense, made almost entirely of neutrons) [1], or, if the core is massive enough, a black hole (gravity so strong that not even light can escape) [1].
Cover both paths separately. Key terms: white dwarf (medium star), supernova + neutron star OR black hole (massive star). Don't mix up which ending goes with which star.

The table below shows data for two planets orbiting the Sun.

Mars2281.88-23

(a) Explain why Neptune has a longer orbital period than Mars. [2]
(b) Explain why Neptune has a much lower surface temperature than Mars. [2]
(c) State the force that keeps both planets in orbit around the Sun. [1]
5 marks
Answer
(a) Neptune is much further from the Sun, so it has a much larger orbit to complete [1]. Also, planets further from the Sun travel more slowly [1].

(b) Neptune is much further from the Sun [1]. The intensity of the Sun's radiation decreases with distance (it spreads out), so Neptune receives much less energy per square metre [1].

(c) Gravity (gravitational attraction / gravitational pull of the Sun) [1].
For (a): two ideas — bigger orbit AND slower speed. For (b): further away AND less radiation per unit area. For (c): just "gravity" is enough for 1 mark.
