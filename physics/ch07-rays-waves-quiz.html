Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
State the two laws of reflection.
2 marks
Answer
Law 1: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection [1].
Law 2: The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal all lie in the same plane [1].
Many students forget Law 2. Make sure you mention the "normal" — saying "the mirror surface" instead of "the normal" loses you the mark.

List the seven types of electromagnetic wave in order of decreasing wavelength (longest first).
2 marks
Answer
Radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, gamma rays [2 for all correct and in order; 1 for mostly correct with one swap].
Mnemonic: Running Man In Vegas Uses Xtra Gear. This question appears on nearly every physics paper. Learn it cold.

3
State two properties that are common to all electromagnetic waves.
2 marks
Answer
Any two from: They are all transverse waves [1]. They all travel at the speed of light (3 × 108 m/s) in a vacuum [1]. They can all travel through a vacuum [1]. They all transfer energy [1].
Make sure your two properties are genuinely different. "They travel through a vacuum" and "they don't need a medium" count as the same point.

4
What type of lens is used to correct (a) short sight and (b) long sight?
2 marks
Answer
(a) Short sight is corrected with a concave (diverging) lens [1].
(b) Long sight is corrected with a convex (converging) lens [1].
Either the lens shape name (concave/convex) or the function name (diverging/converging) gets the mark. Know both.

Intermediate — Can You Explain and Calculate?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to show working in calculations and explain effects using correct terminology.
5
Describe what happens to a ray of light when it passes from air into a glass block at an angle. In your answer, mention the normal, speed, and direction.
3 marks
Answer
When light enters the glass, it slows down (glass is denser than air) [1]. The ray bends towards the normal [1], so the angle of refraction is smaller than the angle of incidence [1]. This bending is called refraction and is caused by the change in speed.
Three marks: (1) speed decreases, (2) bends towards normal, (3) angle of refraction is smaller. If the ray entered along the normal (at 90 degrees), it would NOT bend — only mention bending when the ray enters at an angle.

6
An object is 10 cm in front of a plane mirror. (a) How far is the image behind the mirror? (b) If the object is moved 3 cm closer to the mirror, how far is the object from its image now? (c) What type of image is formed?
3 marks
Answer
(a) The image is 10 cm behind the mirror (same distance as object is in front) [1].
(b) New object distance = 10 - 3 = 7 cm. Image is 7 cm behind mirror. Distance from object to image = 7 + 7 = 14 cm [1].
(c) The image is virtual (it cannot be formed on a screen) [1].
Part (b): the object is now 7 cm from the mirror, so the image is also 7 cm behind. Total distance = 7 + 7 = 14 cm. Students often forget to add both distances.

7
Explain what is meant by total internal reflection. State the two conditions needed for it to occur.
4 marks
Answer
Total internal reflection (TIR) is when light hitting the inside surface of a material is completely reflected back inside, with no refracted ray passing through the surface [1]. It occurs when the surface acts like a perfect mirror [1].

Condition 1: Light must be travelling from a denser medium to a less dense medium (e.g. glass to air) [1].
Condition 2: The angle of incidence must be greater than the critical angle [1].
Four marks: definition of TIR [1-2], then the two conditions [1 each]. Both conditions are needed — writing only one gets you only one mark for this part.

Advanced — Can You Discuss and Apply?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, calculations with working, and clear reasoning.
8
The refractive index of diamond is 2.42 and the speed of light in a vacuum is 300 000 km/s. (a) Calculate the speed of light in diamond. (b) Calculate the critical angle for diamond. (c) Explain why diamonds sparkle so much.
5 marks
Answer
(a) n = speed in vacuum / speed in medium
speed in diamond = 300 000 / 2.42 = 124 000 km/s (accept 1.24 × 105 km/s) [1].

(b) sin c = 1 / n = 1 / 2.42 = 0.413 [1]
c = sin-1(0.413) = 24° [1].

(c) Diamond has a very small critical angle (24°) [1], which means that light entering the diamond is much more likely to undergo total internal reflection rather than passing out through the surface. The diamond is cut with angled faces so that light is reflected many times inside before escaping, creating the sparkle [1].
Part (a): rearrange the refractive index formula. Part (b): use sin c = 1/n. Part (c): link the small critical angle to lots of TIR and the diamond cut.

9
A convex lens has a focal length of 6 cm. An object is placed 9 cm from the lens. (a) State the three standard rays you would draw on a ray diagram. (b) Describe the image formed (state whether it is real or virtual, upright or inverted, and enlarged or diminished). (c) Name a device that uses a convex lens to form this type of image.
6 marks
Answer
(a) Ray 1: A ray through the centre of the lens passes straight through [1]. Ray 2: A ray parallel to the principal axis passes through the principal focus F after the lens [1]. Ray 3: A ray through F' (on the object side) leaves the lens parallel to the principal axis [1].

(b) The object is between F and 2F (since F = 6 cm and 2F = 12 cm, and the object is at 9 cm). The image is: real, inverted, and enlarged (magnified, larger than the object) [2 — 1 for real and inverted, 1 for enlarged].

(c) A projector (or film projector) [1].
To determine the image type, compare the object distance (9 cm) to F (6 cm) and 2F (12 cm). Object between F and 2F gives a real, inverted, enlarged image beyond 2F. This is the projector setup.

10
A telephone system uses optical fibres to transmit digital signals. (a) State the difference between an analogue signal and a digital signal. (b) Give two advantages of transmitting digital signals rather than analogue signals. (c) Give two advantages of using optical fibres rather than copper cables for long-distance communication.
6 marks
Answer
(a) An analogue signal varies continuously and can take any value [1]. A digital signal has only two values (on/off or 1/0) [1].

(b) Any two from: Digital signals can be regenerated (cleaned up) to remove noise, so quality is maintained [1]. Analogue signals amplify the noise along with the signal [1]. Digital signals can carry more data [1].

(c) Any two from: Optical fibres have less attenuation (signal loss) [1]. They are not affected by electrical interference [1]. They can carry more signals simultaneously [1]. They are thinner and lighter [1]. They are more secure (cannot be tapped) [1].
Part (a) needs BOTH definitions — one mark for each. Parts (b) and (c) need two DIFFERENT advantages each. The key word in (b) is "regenerated" — examiners love this term.
