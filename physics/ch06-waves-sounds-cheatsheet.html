Physics Ch6 — Cheat Sheet

Waves and Sounds

Pin this to your wall

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Transverse wave Oscillations are at right angles to the direction the wave travels. E.g. light, water ripples. Longitudinal (oscillations along the wave direction)
Longitudinal wave Oscillations are in the same direction as the wave travels. Made of compressions and rarefactions. E.g. sound. Transverse (oscillations perpendicular to wave direction)
Frequency (f) Number of waves passing a point per second. Unit: hertz (Hz). Higher frequency = higher pitch. Period (period = 1/frequency — the time for ONE wave)
Wavelength (λ) Distance from one point on a wave to the same point on the next wave. Unit: metres (m). Amplitude (that's up/down distance, not along)
Amplitude Maximum displacement from the rest position. Bigger amplitude = louder sound. Wavelength (wavelength is crest-to-crest, amplitude is crest-to-middle)
Reflection Waves bounce off a surface. Angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Refraction (that's bending due to speed change)
Refraction Waves change direction when they change speed (e.g. entering a different medium). Frequency stays the same. Diffraction (that's spreading through a gap)
Diffraction Waves spread out when passing through a gap or around an obstacle. Most spreading when gap size equals wavelength. Refraction (that's bending due to speed change, not spreading)
Ultrasound Sound with frequency above 20 000 Hz — above the upper limit of human hearing. —
Echo A reflected sound. The sound travels to a surface and back again, so divide total distance by 2. Forgetting to divide by 2 (sound goes THERE and BACK)

Key Formulas — Learn These Cold

What Formula Units
Wave equation v = f × λ m/s, Hz, m
Frequency & period f = 1 / T Hz, s
Echo / sonar distance d = (v × t) / 2 m, m/s, s
Exam Trap — Divide by 2 for Echoes!
The most common mistake in echo questions is forgetting that sound travels there and back. Total distance = speed × time. Then divide by 2 to get the distance to the reflecting surface. If your answer looks ridiculously large, check this.

Transverse vs Longitudinal — Side by Side

Transverse Longitudinal
Oscillation direction At right angles to wave travel Same direction as wave travel
Features Peaks (crests) and troughs Compressions and rarefactions
Examples Light, EM waves, water ripples Sound, seismic P-waves
Needs a medium? EM waves: No. Water: Yes Yes (always)
Exam Trap — Sound Is NOT Transverse
The wavy line on an oscilloscope is a graph of air pressure over time — it is NOT a picture of the wave. Sound waves are always longitudinal: air vibrates back and forth, not up and down.

Three Wave Effects — How to Tell Them Apart

Effect What Happens Cause
Reflection Wave bounces off a surface Hitting a barrier
Refraction Wave changes direction Wave changes speed (new medium)
Diffraction Wave spreads out Passing through a gap or around an edge
Memory Trick
Reflection = bounces (like a mirror). Refraction = bends (think "fracture" = break direction). Diffraction = spreads (through a gap).

Reading an Oscilloscope Trace

What You See What It Means
Waves closer together (more peaks) Higher frequency = higher pitch
Waves further apart (fewer peaks) Lower frequency = lower pitch
Taller waves Greater amplitude = louder
Shorter waves Smaller amplitude = quieter
Two-Check Rule
When comparing two oscilloscope traces, always check TWO things separately: (1) Are peaks the same height? (amplitude/loudness). (2) Are peaks the same distance apart? (frequency/pitch). The exam will often show two traces and ask you to describe the differences.

Speed of Sound — Key Numbers

Medium Approx Speed (m/s)
Air 330
Water 1 400
Concrete / steel 5 000

Rule: sound travels fastest in solids, then liquids, then gases. Human hearing range: 20 Hz to 20 000 Hz.

Say It in One Sentence

Waves Waves transfer energy without transferring matter — the particles vibrate but don't travel with the wave.
Transverse Vibrations at right angles to the wave direction — think of shaking a rope side to side.
Longitudinal Vibrations along the wave direction — push-pull like a slinky, creating compressions and rarefactions.
v = fλ Wave speed = frequency × wavelength — the only formula you need for wave calculations.
Sound in a vacuum Sound cannot travel through empty space because there are no particles to pass on the vibrations.
Diffraction Maximum spreading happens when the gap size equals the wavelength — wider gaps = less spreading.
Ultrasound Frequency above 20 000 Hz — used for echo-sounding, baby scans, cleaning, and flaw detection.

Memory Tricks

T for Transverse = T-Shape
Transverse: oscillations go across (perpendicular), forming a T-shape with the wave direction. Longitudinal: oscillations go aLong the same direction.
Echo = There and Back = Divide by 2
Sound goes TO the wall and comes BACK. Total distance = speed × time. The wall is only HALF that distance away. Write " / 2" in big letters at the top of any echo question.
Refraction: Speed Changes, Frequency Doesn't
When a wave enters a new medium, its speed changes and its wavelength changes, but the frequency stays the same. Think of it like a marching band stepping from pavement onto sand — they slow down and their step length shortens, but they are still marching at the same beat (frequency).
Why Ultrasound Instead of X-rays for Babies?
Two reasons: (1) Ultrasound does not damage living cells. (2) It can distinguish between layers of soft tissue. Say both for full marks.
Sound Cannot Travel Through Space
Three-point answer: (1) Space is a vacuum / has no particles. (2) Sound needs a medium. (3) No particles = nothing to vibrate / pass on compressions.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"A wave has f = 4 Hz and λ = 2 m. Find the speed." Step 1: v = f × λ. Step 2: v = 4 × 2. Step 3: v = 8 m/s
"Find the wavelength if v = 330 m/s and f = 660 Hz" Step 1: λ = v / f. Step 2: λ = 330 / 660. Step 3: λ = 0.5 m
"A person claps and hears the echo 1.6 s later. Cliff distance? (v = 330 m/s)" Step 1: Total d = 330 × 1.6 = 528 m. Step 2: Divide by 2. Step 3: Distance = 264 m
"Echo-sounder pulse returns in 0.1 s. Depth? (v = 1400 m/s)" Step 1: Total d = 1400 × 0.1 = 140 m. Step 2: Divide by 2. Step 3: Depth = 70 m
"Explain why sound cannot travel through space" Space is a vacuum (no particles). Sound needs a medium. Without particles, there is nothing to vibrate.
"What is the difference between transverse and longitudinal waves?" Transverse: oscillations perpendicular to wave direction. Longitudinal: oscillations parallel (same direction).
"The oscilloscope shows taller, more closely spaced waves. Describe the sound." Taller = louder (greater amplitude). More closely spaced = higher pitch (higher frequency).
"Name four uses of ultrasound" (1) Echo-sounding (sea depth). (2) Pre-natal scanning. (3) Cleaning. (4) Detecting flaws in metals.
