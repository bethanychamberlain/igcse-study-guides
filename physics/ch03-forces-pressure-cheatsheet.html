Forces and Pressure

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Moment The turning effect of a force about a pivot. moment = force × perpendicular distance. Unit: N m. Momentum (that's mass × velocity, from Ch2)
Equilibrium When an object is balanced: clockwise moments = anticlockwise moments AND forces up = forces down. —
Centre of gravity The single point where all the weight of an object appears to act. For a uniform object, it is at the centre. —
Elastic A material that returns to its original shape when the force is removed. Plastic (permanently deformed — keeps new shape)
Extension How much longer a spring is compared to its original length: extension = stretched length - original length. Total length (always subtract the unstretched length!)
Pressure (p) Force per unit area: p = F / A. Unit: pascal (Pa) = 1 N/m2. Force (pressure depends on area too — same force on smaller area = higher pressure)
Atmospheric pressure Pressure exerted by the weight of air above. About 100 kPa at sea level. —

All Five Formulas — Learn These Cold

What Formula Units
Moment moment = F × d N m, N, m
Hooke's law F = k × x N, N/m, m
Pressure (solid) p = F / A Pa, N, m2
Pressure (liquid) p = ρgh Pa, kg/m3, N/kg, m
Boyle's law p1V1 = p2V2 Any consistent units
Exam Trap — Which Pressure Formula?
Use p = F / A for solid objects pressing on surfaces. Use p = ρgh for the pressure inside a liquid at a given depth. If the question mentions depth and density, use ρgh.

Three Types of Equilibrium — Side by Side

Type What Happens When Tilted Example
Stable Returns to original position Cone on its base
Unstable Topples further away Cone on its tip
Neutral Stays in new position Ball on flat surface
Stability Rule
An object topples when its centre of gravity goes beyond the edge of its base. To make something more stable: lower the centre of gravity and/or widen the base. These exact words get marks.

Hooke's Law — Key Points on the Graph

Point What Happens
Limit of proportionality Extension stops being proportional to load — the graph stops being a straight line
Elastic limit Beyond this, the spring is permanently deformed — it won't return to its original length
Exam Trap — Extension vs Length
Extension = current length - original length. If a spring starts at 40 mm and stretches to 58 mm, the extension is 18 mm, not 58 mm. Always subtract the original length first.

Say It in One Sentence

Moment The turning effect of a force — bigger force or bigger distance from the pivot means a bigger moment.
Principle of moments For a balanced object: sum of clockwise moments = sum of anticlockwise moments.
Centre of gravity The one point where the weight of the whole object acts — find it for irregular shapes using a plumb line.
Hooke's law Below the elastic limit, double the force = double the extension — the graph is a straight line through the origin.
Pressure Same force on a smaller area = higher pressure — that's why pins are sharp and skis are wide.
Liquid pressure Deeper = higher pressure, denser liquid = higher pressure, and it acts in all directions.
Boyle's law At constant temperature: halve the volume and the pressure doubles (inversely proportional).
How a straw works You reduce pressure inside the straw; the atmosphere pushes the drink up. You don't "pull" the liquid.

Memory Tricks

Choosing Where to Take Moments
Take moments about the point where an unknown force acts. That force's distance becomes zero, so it drops out of the equation. Now you only have one unknown — solve for it.
Maximum vs Minimum Pressure
Same weight, different area. Smallest face = maximum pressure. Biggest face = minimum pressure. The force (weight) never changes — only the area does.
Boyle's Law Sense Check
After solving a Boyle's law problem, check: did pressure go UP? Then volume must have gone DOWN (and vice versa). If both went the same direction, you made an error. Inverse proportion = one up, one down.
Liquid Pressure — "DSA" Rule
Liquid pressure depends on: Depth, denSity, All directions. It does NOT depend on the shape or width of the container.
Moment vs Momentum
Moment = force × distance (turning). Momentum = mass × velocity (moving). They sound similar but are completely different quantities. Moment is in N m, momentum is in kg m/s.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Find the moment of a 40 N force acting 0.3 m from the pivot" Step 1: moment = F × d. Step 2: 40 × 0.3. Step 3: 12 N m
"A plank is balanced. Find the unknown force." Set clockwise moments = anticlockwise moments. Take moments about one support to eliminate one unknown.
"A block (2600 kg, 0.5 × 1.0 × 2.0 m). Maximum pressure?" Step 1: W = 26 000 N. Step 2: Smallest area = 0.5 × 1.0 = 0.5 m2. Step 3: p = 26 000/0.5 = 52 000 Pa
"Pressure at 2 m depth in water (1000 kg/m3)" Step 1: p = ρgh. Step 2: 1000 × 10 × 2. Step 3: 20 000 Pa = 20 kPa
"An air bubble at 3 atm has volume 2 cm3. Volume at 1 atm?" Step 1: p1V1 = p2V2. Step 2: 3 × 2 = 1 × V2. Step 3: V2 = 6 cm3
"How to make an object more stable?" Lower the centre of gravity and/or widen the base.
"Explain how a straw works" Sucking reduces air pressure inside the straw. Atmospheric pressure on the drink surface is now greater, so it pushes the liquid up into the straw.
"Explain Boyle's law using kinetic theory" Halving volume = twice as many particles per unit volume = twice as many collisions per second with walls = double the pressure.
