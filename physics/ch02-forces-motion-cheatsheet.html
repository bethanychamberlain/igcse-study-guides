Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Speed Distance moved per unit time. v = s / t. Unit: m/s. Velocity (speed + direction)
Velocity Speed in a given direction. A vector quantity (has magnitude AND direction). Speed (just magnitude, no direction)
Acceleration Rate of change of velocity: a = Δv / Δt. Unit: m/s2. Speed (acceleration is how fast speed CHANGES, not how fast you go)
Resultant force The single overall force when all forces are combined. Use THIS in F = ma. Any single force (always subtract opposing forces first)
Momentum (p) Mass × velocity. Unit: kg m/s. A vector — direction matters! Force (momentum is a property, force changes momentum)
Terminal velocity Maximum speed of a falling object, when air resistance equals weight (forces balanced). "Stopped" (terminal velocity means constant speed, NOT zero speed)
Inertia An object's resistance to changing its velocity. More mass = more inertia. —
Weight (W) The gravitational force on an object. W = m × g. Unit: newtons (N). Mass (mass is in kg and stays the same; weight is in N and changes with gravity)

All Six Formulas — Learn These Cold

What Formula Units
Speed v = s / t m/s, m, s
Acceleration a = Δv / Δt m/s2, m/s, s
Newton's 2nd law F = m × a N, kg, m/s2
Weight W = m × g N, kg, N/kg
Momentum p = m × v kg m/s, kg, m/s
Force & momentum F = Δp / Δt N, kg m/s, s
Exam Trap — F Is the RESULTANT Force
In F = ma, F is the resultant force, not just any single force. If a question gives driving force AND friction, subtract friction first, then use F = ma. This is the #1 mistake students make.

Distance-Time vs Speed-Time Graphs

Distance-Time Speed-Time
Gradient = Speed Acceleration
Area under = (not used) Distance travelled

Exam Trap — Flat Line on Speed-Time
A flat line on a speed-time graph does NOT mean stopped. It means constant speed. Students confuse this constantly. Only on a distance-time graph does a flat line mean stationary.

Say It in One Sentence

Newton's 1st law An object stays still or keeps moving at constant velocity unless an external force acts on it.
Newton's 2nd law The bigger the resultant force, the bigger the acceleration — and heavier objects accelerate less (F = ma).
Newton's 3rd law Every force has an equal and opposite reaction force — but they act on DIFFERENT objects.
Terminal velocity When air resistance grows to equal weight, forces balance and speed stops changing.
Balanced forces Resultant force is zero — object is either stationary OR moving at constant velocity (NOT necessarily stopped).
Momentum Mass times velocity — total momentum is always conserved in a collision (no external forces).
Free fall All objects near Earth accelerate at g = 10 m/s2 when only gravity acts — gaining 10 m/s every second.

Memory Tricks

Units Tell You the Formula
m/s means "metres per second" = distance / time. m/s2 means "metres per second per second" = change in velocity / time. If you forget the formula, read the units out loud.
F = ma Triangle
Draw a triangle: F on top, m and a on the bottom. Cover what you want: cover F = m × a. Cover m = F / a. Cover a = F / m.
Terminal Velocity Story
A skydiver in 4 stages: (1) jumps — weight > drag — accelerates. (2) goes faster — drag increases. (3) drag = weight — forces balanced — terminal velocity. (4) parachute opens — drag > weight — decelerates — new, slower terminal velocity.
Momentum Direction Trick
Always pick one direction as + (e.g. right). Everything going the other way is -. Multiply mass × velocity (with its sign). Add up the totals. The sign of your answer tells you which way things end up moving.
Speed-Time Area = Distance
Break the area under the graph into rectangles and triangles. Rectangle: base × height. Triangle: ½ × base × height. Use the SCALE numbers on the axes, not the paper length.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"A car has 1500 N driving force and 500 N friction. What is the acceleration? (mass = 1000 kg)" Step 1: Resultant F = 1500 - 500 = 1000 N. Step 2: a = F/m = 1000/1000. Step 3: a = 1 m/s2
"A rocket (200 kg) has thrust 3000 N. What is its acceleration?" Step 1: Weight = 200 × 10 = 2000 N. Step 2: Resultant F = 3000 - 2000 = 1000 N. Step 3: a = 1000/200 = 5 m/s2
"Explain why the sky-diver reaches terminal velocity" As speed increases, air resistance increases. Eventually air resistance = weight, resultant force = 0, so acceleration = 0 and speed is constant.
"What does a flat section on a speed-time graph mean?" Constant speed (zero acceleration). The object IS still moving.
"Find the distance from a speed-time graph" Calculate the area under the line. Split into rectangles + triangles.
"State Newton's third law and give an example" Every action has an equal and opposite reaction, acting on different objects. E.g. runner pushes ground back, ground pushes runner forward.
"Trolley A (2 kg, 3 m/s) hits trolley B (4 kg, at rest). They stick together. Find velocity." Before: 2×3 + 4×0 = 6 kg m/s. After: 6 = 6 × v. v = 1 m/s
"Does mass change on the Moon?" No. Mass is the amount of matter and stays the same. Weight changes because gravity is weaker.
