Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
State the SI base unit and its symbol for each of these quantities: (a) mass, (b) length, (c) time.
3 marks
Answer
(a) kilogram, kg [1]
(b) metre, m [1]
(c) second, s [1]
The SI unit of mass is the kilogram, NOT the gram. This is a favourite trick question. You must write both the name and the symbol for full marks.

2
Convert the following: (a) 2500 m into km, (b) 0.75 kg into g, (c) 350 ms into s.
3 marks
Answer
(a) 2500 / 1000 = 2.5 km [1]
(b) 0.75 x 1000 = 750 g [1]
(c) 350 / 1000 = 0.35 s [1]
Going from a smaller unit to a bigger unit: divide. Going from a bigger unit to a smaller unit: multiply. Show your working even on simple conversions.

3
Write the equation that links density, mass, and volume.
1 mark
Answer
density = mass / volume  (or ρ = m / V) [1]
Writing it in words or in symbols both get the mark. Make sure the mass is on top (numerator) and the volume is on the bottom (denominator).

4
A student measures the time for 20 swings of a pendulum and gets 46 seconds. What is the time for one swing (the period)?
2 marks
Answer
Period = 46 / 20 [1] = 2.3 s [1]
One mark for showing the division, one for the correct answer with unit. Always include "s" (seconds) in your answer. This type of question appears almost every year.

Intermediate — Can You Calculate It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to show working using the three-step method: formula, substitute, calculate + units.
5
A metal block has a mass of 540 g and dimensions 10 cm x 5 cm x 4 cm. Calculate the density of the metal in g/cm3.
3 marks
Answer
Volume = 10 x 5 x 4 = 200 cm3 [1]
Density = mass / volume = 540 / 200 [1]
Density = 2.7 g/cm3 [1]
Three steps: calculate volume first, then write the density formula, then substitute and calculate. This density matches aluminium. Always include units in your final answer.
Rate your answer:
6
A storage tank holds petrol with a density of 750 kg/m3. The tank has a volume of 4 m3. Calculate the mass of petrol in the tank.
3 marks
Answer
Write the formula: m = ρ x V [1]
Substitute: m = 750 x 4 [1]
m = 3000 kg [1]
This tests whether you can rearrange the density formula. Remember: to find mass, multiply density by volume. The units must match: kg/m3 and m3 give you kg.
Rate your answer:
7
A measuring cylinder contains 50 cm3 of water. A small stone is lowered in and the water level rises to 68 cm3. The stone has a mass of 48.6 g. Calculate the density of the stone.
3 marks
Answer
Volume of stone = 68 - 50 = 18 cm3 [1]
Density = mass / volume = 48.6 / 18 [1]
Density = 2.7 g/cm3 [1]
The volume of the stone is found by displacement: new level minus original level. This is the standard practical method for irregular solids. Don't forget the subtraction step.

Advanced — Can You Apply It?

Worth 4-6 marks each. These need multi-step calculations, unit conversions, or describing practical methods. Show every step clearly.
8
A rectangular room measures 5 m x 4 m x 3 m. The density of air is 1.3 kg/m3. (a) Calculate the volume of the room. (b) Calculate the mass of air in the room. (c) Convert your answer to grams.
5 marks
Answer
(a) Volume = 5 x 4 x 3 = 60 m3 [1]

(b) m = ρ x V [1]
m = 1.3 x 60 [1]
m = 78 kg [1]

(c) 78 x 1000 = 78 000 g [1]
Multi-step question: calculate volume first, then use the rearranged density formula to find mass, then convert. Each step earns a mark, so even if you get the final number wrong, show your working for partial credit.

9
The table below shows the densities of four materials. Water has a density of 1.0 g/cm3, and petrol has a density of 0.75 g/cm3.

MaterialDensity (g/cm3)
Wood0.75
Ice0.92
Polythene0.95
Steel7.8
(a) Which materials will float in water? (b) Which materials will float in petrol? (c) Petrol and water do not mix. If water is poured into petrol, explain what happens and why.
5 marks
Answer
(a) Materials less dense than water (1.0 g/cm3): wood, ice, polythene [1] — all three have densities below 1.0 [1].

(b) Materials less dense than petrol (0.75 g/cm3): none [1]. Wood has the same density as petrol so it would not float (it would be neutrally buoyant). All others are denser.

(c) The water would sink to the bottom [1] because water (1.0 g/cm3) is more dense than petrol (0.75 g/cm3) [1].
Float or sink is always decided by comparing densities. Less dense = floats, more dense = sinks. For part (c), you must state what happens AND explain why using density values.

10
A student wants to find the density of an irregularly shaped stone. Describe a method they could use. You should include: (a) the equipment needed, (b) the measurements to take, (c) how to calculate the density. [6]
6 marks
Answer
(a) Equipment: A balance (to measure mass) [1] and a measuring cylinder containing water (to measure volume by displacement) [1].

(b) Measurements:
• Measure the mass of the stone using the balance [1].
• Record the initial water level in the measuring cylinder [1].
• Lower the stone into the water and record the new water level. Volume of stone = new level − original level [1].

(c) Calculation: density = mass / volume [1].
Six marks, six points. The examiner is looking for: balance, measuring cylinder, measure mass, read initial level, read final level and subtract, then the formula. If the stone is too big for a measuring cylinder, a displacement can is also acceptable — describe filling it to the spout and collecting the overflow.
