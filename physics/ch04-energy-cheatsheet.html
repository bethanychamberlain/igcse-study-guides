All the Formulas — One Place

Name Formula Units What Each Letter Means
Work done W = F x d J W = work done (J), F = force (N), d = distance (m)
GPE GPE = mgh J m = mass (kg), g = gravity (10 N/kg), h = height (m)

Work: What Force Does → W = Fd
GPE: Mountain Goats climb High → GPE = mgh
KE: Half My Velocity Squared → KE = ½mv2
Efficiency: Useful over Total, times 100
Power: Work per Time → P = W/t

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Work done Energy transferred when a force moves an object through a distance. Force (push/pull, not energy)
Energy The ability to do work. Measured in joules (J). Can change form but is never created or destroyed. Power (energy per second, not total energy)
GPE Energy stored because of an object's height above the ground. KE (GPE is about height, KE is about speed)
KE Energy an object has because it is moving. GPE (KE is about speed, GPE is about height)
Efficiency The percentage of input energy that is usefully transferred. Always less than 100%. Power (efficiency is about waste, power is about speed)
Power How fast energy is transferred. Measured in watts (W). 1 W = 1 J per second. Energy (power is the rate, energy is the total amount)
Conservation of energy Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred between forms. "Energy is used up" (WRONG — it just becomes heat)
Joule (J) The unit of energy and work done. Also the unit of GPE and KE. Newton (force) or Watt (power)
Watt (W) The unit of power. 1 watt = 1 joule per second. W for work done — same letter, different meaning!

Worked Examples — Step by Step

Every calculation follows the same three steps: (1) Write the formula. (2) Substitute the numbers. (3) Calculate + write units.

Example 1: Work Done

A child pulls a toy with a force of 8 N over 3 m. How much work is done?

1. W = F x d
2. W = 8 x 3
3. W = 24 J
Example 2: GPE

A 3 kg book is placed on a shelf 2 m high. What is its GPE? (g = 10 N/kg)

1. GPE = m x g x h
2. GPE = 3 x 10 x 2
3. GPE = 60 J
Example 3: KE (watch the squaring step!)

A 4 kg cat runs at 3 m/s. What is its KE?

1. KE = ½ x m x v²
2. KE = ½ x 4 x 3²
3a. Square the speed: 3² = 9
3b. KE = ½ x 4 x 9 = 18 J
Example 4: Efficiency

A motor receives 800 J. It does 200 J of useful work. What is its efficiency?

1. Efficiency = (useful / total) x 100%
2. Efficiency = (200 / 800) x 100%
3a. Divide: 200 / 800 = 0.25
3b. 0.25 x 100% = 25%
Example 5: Power

An engine does 6000 J of work in 10 seconds. What is its power?

1. P = W / t
2. P = 6000 / 10
3. P = 600 W
Exam Trap — Forgetting Units
Always write the unit after your final answer. "60" gets 0 marks. "60 J" gets the mark. The examiner needs to see that you know it's joules, not newtons or watts.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"calculate the work done" W = F x d — look for force (N) and distance (m)
"calculate the gravitational potential energy" GPE = mgh — look for mass (kg), gravity (10), height (m)
"calculate the kinetic energy" KE = ½mv2 — look for mass (kg) and speed (m/s)
"calculate the efficiency" (useful / total) x 100% — useful is the smaller number
"calculate the power" P = W / t — look for energy/work (J) and time (s)
"describe the energy changes" or "energy transfers" Name the forms: "X energy is transferred to Y energy." Always mention heat/thermal as wasted energy
"explain using conservation of energy" Total energy before = total energy after. Energy is transferred, not created or lost
"the ball falls from rest" or "dropped from a height" GPE = KE — set mgh = ½mv2 and solve
"how much energy is wasted?" Wasted = Total input - Useful output
"the object comes to rest at the top" All KE has been converted to GPE. Set KE = GPE

KE vs GPE — Side by Side

KE (Kinetic) GPE (Gravitational Potential)
Depends on Mass and speed Mass, gravity, and height
Formula ½mv2 mgh
Maximum when Object is at the bottom / fastest Object is at the top / highest
Zero when Object is stationary (v = 0) Object is on the ground (h = 0)
Example A ball just before it hits the ground A ball held above your head
Memory Trick — The Roller Coaster
At the top of a roller coaster: maximum GPE, minimum KE (slow).
At the bottom: maximum KE, minimum GPE (fast).
Total energy stays the same the whole ride. GPE converts to KE on the way down, and KE converts to GPE on the way up.

Memory Tricks for Formulas

KE — "Half My Velocity Squared"
KE = ½ m v2. Say it out loud: "half em vee squared." The ½ is the sneaky part — don't forget it. And always square the speed before multiplying.
GPE — "Mountains Got Height"
GPE = m g h. Three letters, all multiplied. Mass times gravity times height. Easy — the hardest part is remembering g = 10 (but they give you that).
Efficiency — "Useful is LESS"
The useful energy always goes on top and is always the smaller number. If you get more than 100%, flip your fraction — you put the numbers the wrong way round.
Power — "Work Takes Time"
P = W / t. Power equals work divided by time. Think: a powerful person finishes the same work in less time. More power = same work, less time.
Work Done — "Force the Distance"
W = F x d. You need both a force AND a distance for work to be done. If nothing moves, no work is done — even if you push really hard. Holding a heavy bag still = 0 work done.
