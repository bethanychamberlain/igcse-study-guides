Measurements and Units

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Physical quantity A number + a unit (e.g. 10 m). You can treat the unit like an algebra symbol. —
SI units The standard system of units used by all scientists worldwide (Système International). —
Mass The quantity of matter in an object, measured in kilograms (kg). Stays the same everywhere. Weight (that's a force, measured in newtons, changes with gravity)
Density (ρ) Mass per unit volume: how much mass is packed into each m3. Formula: ρ = m / V. Mass (density depends on volume too, not just how heavy)
Volume The amount of space an object takes up. SI unit: m3. Also cm3 and litres. Area (2D) vs Volume (3D)
Period The time for one complete swing of a pendulum (or one full oscillation), in seconds. Total time for many swings (always divide to get one)
Zero error When a measuring instrument doesn't read exactly zero before use. Subtract it from readings. —
Scientific notation Writing numbers using powers of 10. E.g. 3.2 × 105 = 320 000. —

SI Prefixes — Side by Side

Prefix Symbol Meaning Power of 10 Example
kilo k 1 000 103 1 km = 1000 m
centi c 1/100 10-2 1 cm = 0.01 m
milli m 1/1 000 10-3 1 mm = 0.001 m
micro μ 1/1 000 000 10-6 1 μm = 0.000001 m
Exam Trap — Milli vs Micro
Milli = thousandth (10-3). Micro = millionth (10-6). Students often think milli means millionth — it does NOT.

The Three Base SI Units You Must Know

Quantity SI Base Unit Symbol
Mass kilogram kg
Length metre m
Time second s
Exam Trap — SI Unit of Mass
The SI base unit of mass is the kilogram (kg), NOT the gram. It is the only base unit with a prefix built in. If the exam asks "What is the SI unit of mass?" the answer is kg.

Key Formula — Density

Formula What Each Letter Means Units
ρ = m / V ρ = density, m = mass, V = volume kg/m3 or g/cm3
m = ρ × V Rearranged to find mass kg or g
V = m / ρ Rearranged to find volume m3 or cm3
Exam Trap — Units Must Match
If mass is in grams, volume must be in cm3 → density in g/cm3. If mass is in kg, volume must be in m3 → density in kg/m3. Never mix them. Conversion: 1 g/cm3 = 1000 kg/m3.

Say It in One Sentence

Physical quantity Every measurement has two parts: a number and a unit — together they are a physical quantity.
SI units The worldwide standard: kg for mass, m for length, s for time — always convert to SI before calculating.
Density How much mass is crammed into each cubic metre — high density means heavy for its size.
Zero error If the instrument doesn't read zero before you start, subtract the offset from every reading.
Pendulum period Time many swings and divide — this reduces the error from your reaction time.
Irregular volume Drop it in water, measure how much the level rises — that rise is the object's volume.
Converting units Big to small = multiply, small to big = divide — write this rule on a sticky note.

Memory Tricks

The Three-Step Method (Use EVERY Time)
Step 1: Write the formula. Step 2: Substitute the numbers. Step 3: Calculate + write the units. Even if you can do it in your head, write all three steps. Skip to the answer and get it wrong = 0 marks. Show working and make a small error = 2 out of 3 marks.
Density Triangle
Draw a triangle: m on top, ρ and V on the bottom. Cover what you want to find: cover ρ and you see m/V. Cover m and you see ρ × V. Cover V and you see m/ρ.
Converting Units — Big/Small Rule
Going from a big unit to a small unit? Multiply (2.5 km = 2500 m). Going from small to big? Divide (4500 g = 4.5 kg). Think: you need MORE of a smaller unit.
Measuring Thin Things
Can't measure one sheet of paper? Measure 500 sheets, then divide by 500. Same trick works for anything too thin to measure individually.
Water Density = 1
Water is the easy one: exactly 1 g/cm3 = 1000 kg/m3. If something floats on water, its density is less than 1 g/cm3. If it sinks, its density is greater.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"What is the SI unit of mass?" Kilogram (kg) — NOT gram
"Find the density of an object with mass 240 g and volume 80 cm3" Step 1: ρ = m/V. Step 2: ρ = 240/80. Step 3: ρ = 3 g/cm3
"Find the mass of 2 m3 of steel (density 7800 kg/m3)" Step 1: m = ρ × V. Step 2: m = 7800 × 2. Step 3: m = 15 600 kg
"How would you measure the density of an irregular solid?" Measure mass with a balance. Find volume by displacement (water rises in a measuring cylinder). Calculate ρ = m/V.
"20 swings of a pendulum take 46 seconds. What is the period?" 46 / 20 = 2.3 s. Period = time for one complete swing.
"How could the student improve accuracy?" Time more swings and divide — this reduces the effect of reaction time error.
"Convert 3500 mm to metres" Small to big = divide: 3500 / 1000 = 3.5 m
"What is a zero error?" When the instrument does not read zero before measuring. Subtract the zero error from every reading.
