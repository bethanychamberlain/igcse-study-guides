Foundation — Can You Recall It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Define the terms mean, median, mode, and range.
4 marks
Answer
Mean: the sum of all values divided by the number of values [1].
Median: the middle value when data is arranged in order [1].
Mode: the value that occurs most often [1].
Range: the difference between the largest and smallest values [1].
One mark per correct definition. "Arranged in order" is essential for the median definition.

2
A pie chart represents 200 students. One sector has an angle of 90°. How many students does this sector represent?
2 marks
Answer
90 ÷ 360 × 200 [1] = 50 students [1].
One mark for the correct method (fraction of 360), one for the answer. Always show the working.

3
State whether each of the following is discrete or continuous data:
(a) number of pets in a household
(b) height of students in a class
(c) shoe sizes
3 marks
Answer
(a) Discrete — you can only have whole numbers of pets [1].
(b) Continuous — height can take any value within a range [1].
(c) Discrete — shoe sizes come in specific values (e.g. 6, 6.5, 7) [1].
Discrete = only certain values possible. Continuous = can be any value, usually from measuring.

4
Describe what is meant by positive correlation and negative correlation on a scatter graph.
2 marks
Answer
Positive correlation: as one variable increases, the other variable also increases [1].
Negative correlation: as one variable increases, the other variable decreases [1].
Don't just say "they go together" — you must describe the direction (both increase, or one increases while the other decreases).

Intermediate — Can You Apply It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to show clear working and apply the methods to data.
5
Find the mean, median, and mode of the following data set:
3, 7, 7, 2, 9, 4, 7, 5, 8, 3
4 marks
Answer
Ordered: 2, 3, 3, 4, 5, 7, 7, 7, 8, 9
Mean: (2+3+3+4+5+7+7+7+8+9) ÷ 10 = 55 ÷ 10 = 5.5 [1]
Median: 10 values → average of 5th and 6th = (5+7) ÷ 2 = 6 [1]
Mode: 7 (appears 3 times) [1]
Range: 9 − 2 = 7 [1]
Always write the data in order first. The examiner can then see your median working is correct. One mark each for mean, median, mode, and range.

6
A school has 360 students. The pie chart angles for their favourite sports are: Football 120°, Tennis 80°, Swimming 60°, Other 100°.
(a) How many students chose swimming?
(b) What fraction of students chose football? Give your answer in its simplest form.
4 marks
Answer
(a) 60 ÷ 360 × 360 [1] = 60 students [1].
(b) 120 ÷ 360 = 12/36 = 1/3 [1 for fraction, 1 for simplifying].
Check: do the angles add to 360°? 120+80+60+100 = 360. Good. Always simplify fractions — don't leave it as 120/360.

7
The grouped frequency table below shows the masses of 30 apples.

60 ≤ m < 80: frequency 5
80 ≤ m < 100: frequency 12
100 ≤ m < 120: frequency 9
120 ≤ m < 140: frequency 4

Calculate the midpoint of each class and use them to estimate the mean mass of the apples.
4 marks
Answer
Midpoints: 70, 90, 110, 130 [1]
fx column: 70×5=350, 90×12=1080, 110×9=990, 130×4=520 [1]
Total fx: 350+1080+990+520 = 2940 [1]
Estimated mean: 2940 ÷ 30 = 98 grams [1]
This is an "estimated" mean because we don't know the exact values — we use midpoints as approximations. Show the midpoints, the multiplications, the total, and the division.

Advanced — Can You Analyse It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need interpretation, comparison, and clear reasoning.
8
The salaries of 5 employees at a small company are: $22 500, $22 500, $17 900, $21 400, and $155 300.
(a) Calculate the mean and the median salary.
(b) Which average better represents the typical salary? Explain your answer.
5 marks
Answer
(a) Mean: (22500+22500+17900+21400+155300) ÷ 5 = 239600 ÷ 5 = $47 920 [1]
Ordered: 17900, 21400, 22500, 22500, 155300
Median: 3rd value = $22 500 [1]

(b) The median ($22 500) is a better representation [1] because the mean ($47 920) is heavily distorted by the one very high salary ($155 300) [1]. Four of the five employees earn less than the mean, so it doesn't represent a "typical" salary [1].
This is a classic exam question. The key phrase: "the mean is affected/distorted by the extreme value (outlier)." Always name WHICH average is better and say WHY.

9
A teacher records the marks of 9 students in French and German:

French: 15, 22, 23, 27, 36, 36, 40, 43, 43
German: 6, 9, 18, 28, 28, 35, 37, 41, 45

(a) Describe the type of correlation you would expect between French and German marks.
(b) A student scored 30 in French but was absent for the German test. Using the data, estimate a reasonable German mark for this student. Explain your reasoning.
5 marks
Answer
(a) Positive correlation [1] — students who score highly in French tend to score highly in German as language skills are related [1].

(b) Looking at students with French marks near 30: the student with 27 scored 28 in German, and the student with 36 scored 28 or 35. A reasonable estimate would be approximately 28–30 for German [1]. This could be found more precisely by plotting a scatter graph and drawing a line of best fit [1], then reading off the German mark where French = 30 [1].
The exam wants you to describe the correlation type (positive) AND justify it. For the estimate, explain that you would use a line of best fit. An answer "between 25 and 32" is acceptable as it's an estimate.

10
A survey of 240 students records how they travel to school. The results are to be shown on a pie chart.

Walk: 80   Bus: 60   Car: 70   Cycle: 30

(a) Calculate the pie chart angle for each category.
(b) Verify that your angles are correct.
(c) Another school surveys 180 students and also finds that 80 walk. Can you say that "the same proportion of students walk" at both schools? Show working to justify your answer.
6 marks
Answer
(a) Walk: 80/240 × 360 = 120° [1]
Bus: 60/240 × 360 = 90° [1]
Car: 70/240 × 360 = 105°
Cycle: 30/240 × 360 = 45° [1]

(b) Check: 120 + 90 + 105 + 45 = 360°. Correct. [1]

(c) School 1: 80/240 = 1/3 = 33.3%. School 2: 80/180 = 4/9 = 44.4% [1]. The proportions are not the same — a higher proportion walks at School 2, even though the actual number (80) is the same [1].
Part (c) is the hardest — the exam is testing whether you understand that the SAME number doesn't mean the SAME proportion when the totals are different. Convert to fractions or percentages to compare.
