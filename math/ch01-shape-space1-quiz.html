Foundation — Can You Recall It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. These test whether you know the basic facts and formulae.
1
Angles on a straight line add up to ____°. Angles at a point add up to ____°. Angles in a triangle add up to ____°.
2 marks
Answer
Angles on a straight line = 180° [1]. Angles at a point = 360°. Angles in a triangle = 180° [1].
These three facts must be memorised cold. They appear in almost every angle question as the starting point.

2
Write down the formula for the area of a trapezium.
1 mark
Answer
A = ½(a + b) × h [1], where a and b are the two parallel sides and h is the perpendicular height.
You must know this from memory. It's the one area formula students most often forget.

3
A regular polygon has an exterior angle of 40°. How many sides does it have?
2 marks
Answer
Exterior angle = 360° ÷ n [1], so n = 360° ÷ 40° = 9 sides [1].
Write the formula first for the method mark. Even if you make an arithmetic slip, you still get 1 mark.
Rate your answer:
4
State the order of rotational symmetry of a regular hexagon.
1 mark
Answer
6 [1]. A regular hexagon looks the same 6 times during a full rotation.
For any regular polygon, the order of rotational symmetry equals the number of sides.

Intermediate — Can You Use the Formula?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to choose the right formula and apply it correctly.
5
Calculate the area of a circle with diameter 12 cm. Give your answer to 1 decimal place.
3 marks
Answer
Radius = 12 ÷ 2 = 6 cm [1].
Area = π × 6² = π × 36 [1] = 113.1 cm² [1].
The question gives the DIAMETER, not the radius. You must halve it. This catches lots of students — always check.
Rate your answer:
6
A cylinder has radius 4 cm and height 10 cm. Calculate its volume. Give your answer to 3 significant figures.
3 marks
Answer
V = πr²h [1]
V = π × 4² × 10 = π × 16 × 10 = 160π [1]
V = 503 cm³ [1].
Write the formula first (1 mark), substitute correctly (1 mark), get the answer right to the correct accuracy (1 mark). Always state units: cm³ for volume.
Rate your answer:
7
Find the arc length of a sector with radius 10 cm and angle 72°. Give your answer to 1 decimal place.
3 marks
Answer
Arc length = (θ / 360) × πd [1]
= (72 / 360) × π × 20 [1]
= (1/5) × 20π = 4π
= 12.6 cm [1].
Remember d = 2r. The fraction 72/360 = 1/5, so you're finding one-fifth of the full circumference.

Advanced — Can You Solve the Problem?

Worth 4-6 marks each. Multi-step problems that combine several ideas. Show all working.
8
A triangular prism has a cross-section that is a right-angled triangle with base 6 cm and height 8 cm. The prism is 15 cm long. Calculate (a) the volume and (b) the total surface area of the prism.
5 marks
Answer
(a) Volume:
Cross-section area = ½ × 6 × 8 = 24 cm² [1].
Volume = 24 × 15 = 360 cm³ [1].

(b) Surface area:
Hypotenuse = √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 cm [1].
Three rectangles: (6 × 15) + (8 × 15) + (10 × 15) = 90 + 120 + 150 = 360 cm² [1].
Two triangles: 2 × 24 = 48 cm².
Total SA = 360 + 48 = 408 cm² [1].
For surface area, don't forget the hypotenuse! Use Pythagoras (3-4-5 triangle scaled by 2 gives 6-8-10). List every face systematically.

9
Two parallel lines are crossed by a transversal. Angle a = 3x + 10° and angle b = 5x − 30°, where a and b are alternate angles. Find the value of x and hence find the size of each angle.
4 marks
Answer
Alternate angles are equal [1], so:
3x + 10 = 5x − 30 [1]
10 + 30 = 5x − 3x
40 = 2x
x = 20 [1]
Each angle = 3(20) + 10 = 70° [1].
Check: 5(20) − 30 = 100 − 30 = 70°. Correct!
State the angle fact first ("alternate angles are equal") for 1 mark. Then set up and solve the equation. Always check by substituting back into BOTH expressions.

10
A map has a scale of 1 : 25 000. A lake on the map has an area of 3.2 cm². Calculate the actual area of the lake in m².
4 marks
Answer
Scale for length: 1 cm represents 25 000 cm [1].
Scale for area: 1 cm² represents 25 000² cm² = 625 000 000 cm² [1].
Actual area = 3.2 × 625 000 000 = 2 000 000 000 cm² [1].
Convert to m²: 2 000 000 000 ÷ 10 000 = 200 000 m² [1].
For area, you must SQUARE the scale factor (because area is two-dimensional). This is the key step most students miss. Also: 1 m² = 10 000 cm² (because 100 cm × 100 cm).
