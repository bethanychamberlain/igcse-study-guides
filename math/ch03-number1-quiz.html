Foundation — Quick Recall

Worth 1-2 marks each. These should be automatic for you. If not, review the notes.
1
Calculate: 5 + 3 × 4 − 2
1 mark
Answer
Multiplication first: 3 × 4 = 12.
Then left to right: 5 + 12 − 2 = 15 [1].
NOT (5 + 3) × 4 − 2. BIDMAS: multiplication before addition.

Intermediate — Apply the Method

Worth 3-4 marks each. Show your working clearly — method marks matter.
5
Find the HCF and LCM of 36 and 48.
4 marks
Answer
36 = 2² × 3² [1]
48 = 2&sup4; × 3 [1]
HCF = lowest power of each shared prime: 2² × 3 = 12 [1].
LCM = highest power of each prime: 2&sup4; × 3² = 16 × 9 = 144 [1].
Draw a factor tree for each number to get the prime factorisation. Then: HCF = smallest shared powers, LCM = biggest powers of everything.

6
Find 15% of 360. Then increase 360 by 15%.
3 marks
Answer
15% of 360: 360 ÷ 100 = 3.6.   3.6 × 15 = 54 [1].
Increase by 15%: 360 + 54 = 414 [1].
Or using multiplier: 360 × 1.15 = 414 [1].
Both methods are valid. The multiplier method (1.15) is faster and reduces errors. Practice both so you can use whichever feels natural.

Without a calculator, work out 3.6 × 0.04. Show your method.
3 marks
Answer
Ignore decimals: 36 × 4 = 144 [1].
Count decimal places: 3.6 has 1, 0.04 has 2. Total = 3 decimal places [1].
Place the point: 0.144 [1].
Method: multiply as whole numbers, then count total decimal places in the question and put them back in the answer. Quick estimate: 4 × 0.04 = 0.16. Answer 0.144 is close. Good.

Advanced — Exam-Style Challenges

Worth 4-5 marks each. These combine multiple skills and test your exam technique.
8
After a 25% discount, a laptop costs £450. What was the original price?
4 marks
Answer
25% discount means the price is 75% of the original [1].
Multiplier for −25% = 0.75 [1].
Original × 0.75 = 450 [1]
Original = 450 ÷ 0.75 = £600 [1].
This is a reverse percentage. Do NOT do 450 + 25% of 450 (that gives £562.50, which is WRONG). You must divide by the multiplier. Sanity check: the original should be MORE than £450 (because it was discounted). 600 > 450 — makes sense.

9
A train leaves at 08:47 and arrives at 11:23. The journey is 195 km. (a) How long is the journey in hours and minutes? (b) Calculate the average speed in km/h. Give your answer to 1 d.p.
5 marks
Answer
(a) 08:47 to 09:00 = 13 min. 09:00 to 11:00 = 2 hours. 11:00 to 11:23 = 23 min.
Total = 2 hours 36 minutes [1][1].

(b) Convert time to hours: 36 min ÷ 60 = 0.6 hours. Total = 2.6 hours [1].
Speed = distance ÷ time = 195 ÷ 2.6 [1] = 75.0 km/h [1].
Use the "bridge" method for time (go to the next full hour, count the hours, add the remainder). For speed, time MUST be in hours (not minutes). 36 minutes = 36/60 = 0.6 hours, NOT 0.36.

10
By writing each number to 1 significant figure, estimate the value of (3.87 × 19.2) ÷ 0.048. Show your working.
4 marks
Answer
Round to 1 s.f.: 3.87 ≈ 4,   19.2 ≈ 20,   0.048 ≈ 0.05 [1].
Estimate = (4 × 20) ÷ 0.05 [1]
= 80 ÷ 0.05 [1]
= 1600 [1].
When the question says "estimate," it means round to 1 s.f. first, then calculate. Dividing by 0.05 is the same as multiplying by 20 (since 1 ÷ 0.05 = 20). Exact answer is 1548, so 1600 is a good estimate.
