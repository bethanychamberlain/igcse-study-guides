Intermediate — Can You Apply the Method?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to follow a multi-step method. Show all working.
5
Find the nth term of the sequence: 7, 11, 15, 19, ...
3 marks
Answer
Common difference = 11 − 7 = 4 [1].
Formula starts with 4n [1].
When n = 1: 4(1) = 4, but first term is 7. Adjustment = 7 − 4 = +3.
nth term = 4n + 3 [1].
Check: n=1: 7. n=2: 11. n=3: 15. Correct!
Three marks, three steps: (1) find the common difference, (2) write dn, (3) find the adjustment. Always check by plugging in.

Solve: 3x + 7 = 22
3 marks
Answer
3x + 7 = 22 [given]
3x = 22 − 7 [1] (subtract 7 from both sides)
3x = 15
x = 15 ÷ 3 [1] (divide both sides by 3)
x = 5 [1]
Check: 3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22. Correct!
Two-step equation: undo addition/subtraction first, then undo multiplication. One line per step.

A line passes through the points (1, 5) and (3, 11). Calculate the gradient of the line.
3 marks
Answer
Gradient = (y2 − y1) ÷ (x2 − x1) [1]
= (11 − 5) ÷ (3 − 1) [1]
= 6 ÷ 2
= 3 [1]
Write the formula first, then substitute. Rise (change in y) over run (change in x). Positive answer = uphill line. Makes sense: the y values are increasing.

Advanced — Can You Reason It Out?

Worth 4-5 marks each. Multi-step problems that combine ideas. Show every line of working.
8
Solve: 4(2x − 5) = 3x + 10
4 marks
Answer
Expand the bracket: 4 × 2x = 8x,   4 × (−5) = −20 [1]
8x − 20 = 3x + 10
Collect x terms: 8x − 3x = 10 + 20 [1]
5x = 30
Divide: x = 30 ÷ 5 [1]
x = 6 [1]
Check: Left: 4(12 − 5) = 4(7) = 28. Right: 3(6) + 10 = 28. Correct!
Step 1: expand brackets. Step 2: get all x terms on one side. Step 3: get all number terms on the other side. Step 4: divide. Always check!

9
The nth term of a sequence is 5n − 2. (a) Find the 20th term. (b) Is 83 a term in this sequence? Show your working.
4 marks
Answer
(a) 20th term = 5(20) − 2 = 100 − 2 = 98 [1].

(b) Set 5n − 2 = 83 [1]
5n = 85
n = 17 [1]
Since n = 17 is a whole number, yes, 83 is the 17th term in the sequence [1].
Part (b) is the tricky one: set the nth term equal to the number and solve for n. If n comes out as a whole number, the number IS in the sequence. If n is a decimal, it's NOT.

A line passes through (2, 7) and (5, 16). Find the equation of the line in the form y = mx + c.
5 marks
Answer
Find gradient:
m = (16 − 7) ÷ (5 − 2) = 9 ÷ 3 = 3 [1][1]

Find y-intercept: Use y = mx + c with one point, say (2, 7) [1]:
7 = 3(2) + c
7 = 6 + c
c = 7 − 6 = 1 [1]

y = 3x + 1 [1]
Check with (5, 16): 3(5) + 1 = 16. Correct!
Two-part problem: find m first (gradient formula), then find c by substituting a point into y = mx + c. Always check with the OTHER point.
