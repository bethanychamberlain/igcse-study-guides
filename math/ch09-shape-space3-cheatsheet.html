Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Similar Same shape, different size. All angles equal, all sides in the same ratio (scale factor). Congruent (same shape AND same size)
Congruent Exact same shape and size. Can be rotated, reflected, or translated but measurements are identical. Similar (similar can be different sizes)
Scale factor The multiplier that converts one similar shape to another. Found by dividing corresponding sides. —
Hypotenuse (H) The longest side of a right-angled triangle, opposite the right angle. Adjacent or Opposite (these depend on which angle you pick)
Opposite (O) The side directly across from the angle you're working with. Adjacent (next to the angle, not across from it)
Adjacent (A) The side next to the angle you're working with (that isn't the hypotenuse). Opposite (across from the angle)
Angle of elevation The angle you look UP from the horizontal to see something above you. Angle of depression (looking DOWN)
Angle of depression The angle you look DOWN from the horizontal to see something below you. Angle of elevation (looking UP)
Bearing Direction measured clockwise from North, always written as 3 digits (e.g. 070°, 215°). An ordinary angle (bearings are always FROM North, always 3 digits)

Similar vs Congruent — Side by Side

Similar Congruent
Shape Same Same
Size Different (one is enlarged) Identical
Angles All equal All equal
Sides In the same ratio All exactly equal
Scale factor Any number Exactly 1
Exam Trap — Rectangles
Two rectangles with the same angles (all 90°) are NOT necessarily similar. You must also check that the side ratios match. E.g. a 2×4 rectangle and a 3×5 rectangle both have 90° angles but are NOT similar (2/3 ≠ 4/5).

SOHCAHTOA — The Three Trig Ratios

Ratio Formula Stands For
sin x O / H Sin = Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse
cos x A / H Cos = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse
tan x O / A Tan = Opposite ÷ Adjacent
Which Ratio to Use
Look at the two sides involved (what you have + what you want). O and H → sin. A and H → cos. O and A → tan.

Exam Trap — Calculator Mode
Make sure your calculator is in DEGREES mode, not radians. If you get tiny answers like 0.36 instead of 20.6°, you're in the wrong mode.

Say It in One Sentence

Similar shapes Same shape, different size — find the scale factor from a pair of corresponding sides, then use it for the rest.
Congruent shapes Exact same shape and size, even if one is rotated, reflected, or slid across the page.
Labelling H, O, A H is always opposite the right angle; O is opposite YOUR angle; A is the remaining side next to YOUR angle.
SOHCAHTOA sin = O/H, cos = A/H, tan = O/A — pick the one that uses the two sides you care about.
Inverse trig When you know two sides and want the angle, use sin−1, cos−1, or tan−1 on your calculator.
Word problems Draw the triangle first, label it, find the right angle, then SOHCAHTOA as normal.
Bearings Always measured clockwise from North, always written as three digits (e.g. 070°, not 70°).

Memory Tricks

SOHCAHTOA — The Sentence
Silly Old Harry Caught A Herring Trawling Off Afghanistan. Or just say "SOH-CAH-TOA" out loud until it sticks.
Multiply or Divide? — "Top = Times, Bottom = Bother"
If the unknown is on top of the fraction, multiply (easy). If it's on the bottom, you have to divide (a bit more bother). E.g. x/10 = cos 32° → x = 10 × cos 32°. But 11/x = sin 36° → x = 11 ÷ sin 36°.
Similar Shapes — Match the Extremes
The longest side of one shape matches the longest side of the other. The shortest matches the shortest. If they're drawn at weird angles, find the longest sides first.
Angle Sense-Check — "Both Add to 90"
In a right-angled triangle, the two non-right angles must add up to 90°. If you find one is 35°, the other must be 55°. Quick check for trig answers.
Drawing Word Problems
Tower problems: draw a vertical line (tower), horizontal line (ground), and an angle at the ground = angle of elevation. The right angle is where the tower meets the ground.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"These triangles are similar. Find x." Find scale factor from two known corresponding sides, then multiply or divide to find x.
"Which shapes are congruent?" Find shapes that are the same size. They may be rotated, reflected, or translated.
"Find the length of side x" (right-angled triangle with an angle given) Label H, O, A. Pick sin/cos/tan. If x is on top, multiply. If x is on bottom, divide.
"Find angle x" (two sides given in a right-angled triangle) Label H, O, A. Calculate the fraction. Use inverse sin/cos/tan on the calculator.
"Angle of elevation to the top of a tower..." Draw vertical tower, horizontal ground. Angle is at the ground. Use tan (usually O and A).
"Find the bearing of B from A" Start at A, face North, measure clockwise to the direction of B. Write as 3 digits.
"Are these rectangles similar?" Check side ratios. Same angles (all 90°) is NOT enough. Side ratios must match too.
"The hypotenuse is 15 cm and angle A is 40°. Find the opposite." sin 40° = O/15. So O = 15 × sin 40° = 9.6 cm
