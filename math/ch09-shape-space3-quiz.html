Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Two triangles have angles of 40°, 60°, and 80°. One triangle has sides 3 cm, 4 cm, and 5 cm. The other has sides 6 cm, 8 cm, and 10 cm. Are these triangles similar, congruent, or neither? Explain your answer.
2 marks
Answer
The triangles are similar [1]. They have the same angles, and their corresponding sides are in the same ratio (scale factor = 2) [1]. They are NOT congruent because they are different sizes.
Key word: "similar." Don't just say "the same shape" — use the word "similar." Mentioning the scale factor earns the second mark.

2
In a right-angled triangle, the angle is 50°. The side opposite the 50° angle is 7 cm and the hypotenuse is 10 cm. Write down the values of sin 50° and cos 50° as fractions using these sides.
2 marks
Answer
sin 50° = opposite / hypotenuse = 7/10 [1]
cos 50° = adjacent / hypotenuse. The adjacent side = √(10² − 7²) = √51 ≈ 7.14. So cos 50° = √51 / 10 [1].
If the question says "write as a fraction using these sides," you just need O/H and A/H. You might not need to calculate the adjacent — if it's given, use it directly.

3
Two shapes are similar. Shape A has a side of length 4 cm and the corresponding side of Shape B is 10 cm. What is the scale factor of the enlargement from A to B?
1 mark
Answer
Scale factor = 10 ÷ 4 = 2.5 [1].
Scale factor = new / original. Make sure you divide the right way round — from A to B means B ÷ A.

4
What does SOHCAHTOA stand for? Write out what each part means.
2 marks
Answer
SOH: sin = Opposite / Hypotenuse [1]
CAH: cos = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
TOA: tan = Opposite / Adjacent [1]
All three correct = 2 marks. Two correct = 1 mark. You MUST know this by heart — it's the foundation of every trig question.

Intermediate — Can You Calculate It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to set up equations and use your calculator.
5
In a right-angled triangle, one angle is 37° and the hypotenuse is 5 cm. Calculate the length of the side opposite the 37° angle. Give your answer correct to 1 decimal place.
3 marks
Answer
Have: angle (37°) and H (5). Want: O. Use sin [1].
sin 37° = O / 5 [1]
O = 5 × sin 37° = 5 × 0.6018... = 3.0 cm [1]
Three marks: choosing sin (1), setting up the equation (1), correct answer to 1 d.p. (1). Show all three steps.

6
In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 6 cm and the adjacent side is 8 cm. Calculate the angle between the adjacent side and the hypotenuse. Give your answer correct to 1 decimal place.
3 marks
Answer
Have: O (6) and A (8). Want: angle. Use tan [1].
tan x = 6 / 8 = 0.75 [1]
x = tan−1(0.75) = 36.9° [1]
Three marks: choosing tan (1), correct fraction (1), correct angle (1). Don't forget to use the inverse (shift) button.

7
Two triangles are similar. The smaller triangle has sides 1.8 cm and 2.4 cm. The side corresponding to 1.8 cm on the larger triangle is 3.6 cm. Find the length of the side corresponding to 2.4 cm on the larger triangle.
3 marks
Answer
Scale factor = 3.6 ÷ 1.8 = 2 [1].
Corresponding side = 2.4 × 2 [1] = 4.8 cm [1].
Find the scale factor first, then multiply. Always check which sides correspond to each other.

Advanced — Can You Apply It?

Worth 4-6 marks each. Multi-step problems in real-world contexts. Show all working.
8
A ladder of length 4 m rests against a vertical wall so that the base of the ladder is 1.5 m from the wall. (a) Calculate the angle between the ladder and the ground. [3] (b) How far up the wall does the ladder reach? Give your answer to 1 d.p. [2]
5 marks
Answer
(a) The ladder is the hypotenuse (4 m), the ground is the adjacent (1.5 m) [1].
cos x = 1.5 / 4 = 0.375 [1]
x = cos−1(0.375) = 68.0° [1]

(b) The wall height is the opposite. sin 68.0° = O / 4 [1]
O = 4 × sin 68.0° = 4 × 0.9272 = 3.7 m [1]
Or use Pythagoras: √(4² − 1.5²) = √(16 − 2.25) = √13.75 = 3.7 m
Draw the diagram: wall vertical, ground horizontal, ladder = hypotenuse. Part (b) can use trig or Pythagoras — both get full marks.

9
A plane flies 80 km from A to B on a bearing of 070°. Calculate how far east B is from A. Give your answer to 1 decimal place.
3 marks
Answer
Draw the diagram: North is up, bearing 070° means 70° clockwise from North [1].
The "east" distance is the side opposite the 70° angle (measuring from the North line). The 80 km is the hypotenuse.
sin 70° = east / 80 [1]
east = 80 × sin 70° = 80 × 0.9397 = 75.2 km [1]
In bearing problems: the angle from North is used, distance east = hyp × sin(bearing), distance north = hyp × cos(bearing). Draw the North line first, then measure the angle clockwise.

10
In the diagram, ABD is a straight line. Angle BAC = 90°, AB = 4 m, and AC = 6 m. (a) Use trigonometry to calculate angle ABC. [2] (b) Find angle CBD. [1] (c) Calculate the length of BC. [2]
5 marks
Answer
(a) In triangle ABC: angle A = 90°, AB = 4 (adjacent to angle B), AC = 6 (opposite to angle B).
tan B = 6 / 4 = 1.5 [1]
angle ABC = tan−1(1.5) = 56.3° [1]

(b) ABD is a straight line, so angles ABC + CBD = 180°.
angle CBD = 180° − 56.3° = 123.7° [1]

(c) Using Pythagoras (or trig): BC = √(4² + 6²) = √(16 + 36) = √52 [1] = 7.2 m (1 d.p.) [1]
This is a classic multi-part exam question. Part (a) is trig, part (b) is angles on a straight line, part (c) can use Pythagoras or trig. Show all working for maximum marks.
