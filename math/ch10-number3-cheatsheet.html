Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Index / Power The small number that tells you how many times to multiply. In 34, the index is 4. The base (that's the big number — the 3)
Square number A number multiplied by itself. E.g. 52 = 25. Square root (that's the reverse: √25 = 5)
Cube number A number multiplied by itself three times. E.g. 23 = 8. Cube root (∛8 = 2)
Standard form Writing a number as a × 10n, where 1 ≤ a < 10 and n is an integer. 32 × 105 is NOT standard form (32 is not between 1 and 10)
Reciprocal 1 divided by the number. The reciprocal of 5 is 1/5. The reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2. Negative (the reciprocal of 5 is 1/5, not −5)
Proper fraction Top number is smaller than the bottom. E.g. 3/5. Improper fraction (top ≥ bottom, e.g. 9/4)
Mixed number A whole number plus a fraction. E.g. 2¼. Improper fraction (same value, different form: 2¼ = 9/4)
Substitution Replacing letters in a formula with given numbers, then calculating the result. —

Rules of Indices — The Complete Set

Rule What It Means Example
xa × xb = xa+b Multiplying: ADD the powers 32 × 34 = 36
xa ÷ xb = xa−b Dividing: SUBTRACT the powers 56 ÷ 52 = 54
(xa)b = xa×b Power of a power: MULTIPLY (23)2 = 26
x0 = 1 Anything to the power 0 = 1 70 = 1
x−n = 1/xn Negative power = reciprocal 2−3 = 1/8
Exam Trap — Same Base Only
These rules ONLY work when the base is the same. 32 × 34 = 36 works. But 32 × 54 CANNOT be simplified this way.

Negative Number Sign Rules

Signs Result Example
positive × positive positive +7 × +3 = +21
negative × negative positive −6 × −4 = +24
positive × negative negative +7 × −5 = −35
negative × positive negative −8 × +4 = −32
Same = Positive, Different = Negative
This rule works for multiplying, dividing, AND when two signs sit next to each other. + + = +   − − = +   + − = −   − + = −. E.g. 3 − (−6) = 3 + 6 = 9.

Fraction Operations — Quick Reference

Operation Method Example
Adding Common denominator, add tops 1/4 + 2/5 = 5/20 + 8/20 = 13/20
Subtracting Common denominator, subtract tops 3/4 − 1/3 = 9/12 − 4/12 = 5/12
Multiplying Top × top, bottom × bottom 2/3 × 4/5 = 8/15
Dividing Keep, Flip, Change (KFC) 2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6
Common Denominator — Only for +/−
You ONLY need a common denominator for adding and subtracting. For multiplying and dividing, just go straight across (after flipping for division).

Standard Form — Quick Reference

Original Number Standard Form Power Is
2000 2 × 103 Positive (big number)
56 000 000 5.6 × 107 Positive (big number)
0.0004 4 × 10−4 Negative (small number)
0.00031 3.1 × 10−4 Negative (small number)
Big Number = Positive Power, Small Number = Negative Power
Move the decimal so the number is between 1 and 10. Count how many places you moved. If the original was big (moved left), power is positive. If small (moved right), power is negative.

Say It in One Sentence

Indices Multiply → add powers. Divide → subtract powers. Power of power → multiply powers. Zero power = 1.
Negative power x−n means "1 over xn" — it's a reciprocal, NOT a negative number.
Standard form Write as a × 10n where a is between 1 and 10 — positive power for big numbers, negative for small.
Adding fractions Find a common denominator, convert both fractions, add the tops, keep the bottom.
Dividing fractions Keep the first, Flip the second, Change to multiply — KFC.
Negative signs Same signs give positive, different signs give negative — works for multiplying, dividing, and double signs.
Substitution Replace letters with numbers, use brackets for negatives, then follow BIDMAS carefully.

Memory Tricks

Index Rules — "MAD-ZEN"
Multiply = Add powers. Divide = subtract. Zero power = 1. Exponent on exponent = multiply. Negative power = reciprocal.
Brackets Matter! — (−3)2 vs −32
(−3)2 = 9 (the whole thing is squared). −32 = −9 (only the 3 is squared, then the minus is applied). Brackets change the answer completely.
KFC for Dividing Fractions
Keep the first fraction. Flip the second fraction. Change the ÷ to ×. Then multiply straight across.
Standard Form Check
Before writing your answer, ask: "Is my number between 1 and 10?" If it's 32 × 105, fix it: 3.2 × 106. If it's 0.5 × 103, fix it: 5 × 102.
Substitution — "Brackets Around Negatives"
Always write negative values in brackets when substituting. v = u + at with a = −3 becomes v = u + (−3)t. This prevents sign errors every time.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Simplify 43 × 45" Add the powers: 48
"Evaluate 2−3" 1/23 = 1/8 (not −8)
"Write 0.00056 in standard form" 5.6 × 10−4 (moved decimal 4 places right)
"Calculate (3 × 104) × (2 × 105)" 3 × 2 = 6. 104 × 105 = 109. Answer: 6 × 109
"Work out 2/3 ÷ 4/5" KFC: 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6
"What is −5 − (−3)?" Two negatives = positive: −5 + 3 = −2
"If v = u + at, find v when u = 5, a = −2, t = 4" v = 5 + (−2)(4) = 5 − 8 = −3. Do multiplication before addition (BIDMAS).
"Evaluate 50" 1. Anything to the power of 0 equals 1.
