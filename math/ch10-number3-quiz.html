What is the value of: (a) 50   (b) 2−3   (c) (−3)2
3 marks
Answer
(a) 50 = 1 [1] — any number to the power 0 is 1.
(b) 2−3 = 1/23 = 1/8 (or 0.125) [1]
(c) (−3)2 = (−3) × (−3) = 9 [1] — negative times negative = positive.
Watch out: −32 = −9 (only the 3 is squared), but (−3)2 = 9 (the whole thing is squared). Brackets matter!

Intermediate — Can You Apply It?

Worth 2-3 marks each. Apply the rules to calculations and formulae.
5
Simplify these, leaving your answer in index form:   (a) 53 × 54   (b) 87 ÷ 82   (c) (32)4
3 marks
Answer
(a) 53 × 54 = 57 [1] — add the powers.
(b) 87 ÷ 82 = 85 [1] — subtract the powers.
(c) (32)4 = 38 [1] — multiply the powers.
The base stays the same in all three rules. Only the powers change.

Work out and simplify:   3/4 + 2/5
2 marks
Answer
Common denominator = 20 [1]
3/4 = 15/20,   2/5 = 8/20
15/20 + 8/20 = 23/20 = 1 3/20 [1]
Find the LCD, convert both fractions, add the tops. Leave as a mixed number or improper fraction — both accepted.

A formula states that F = ma. Find the value of F when m = 12 and a = 3. Then find the value of I if I = mu − mv, when m = 6, u = 8, and v = 5.
3 marks
Answer
F = ma = 12 × 3 = 36 [1]
I = mu − mv = (6 × 8) − (6 × 5) = 48 − 30 = 18 [2]
Substitute the values in carefully. For I, multiply first (BIDMAS), then subtract.

9
If x = 3 and y = −4, work out the value of:   (a) 2x + y   (b) x2 − y   (c) xy + y2
4 marks
Answer
(a) 2(3) + (−4) = 6 − 4 = 2 [1]
(b) (3)2 − (−4) = 9 + 4 = 13 [1] — subtracting −4 = adding 4
(c) (3)(−4) + (−4)2 = −12 + 16 = 4 [2]
Part (c) is the trickiest: (−4)2 = 16 (positive!), and 3 × (−4) = −12. Then −12 + 16 = 4. Use brackets around negative numbers to avoid sign errors.

A rubber ball is dropped from a height of 300 cm. After each bounce, it rises to 4/5 of its previous height. (a) How high does it rise after the first bounce? [1] (b) How high does it rise after the fourth bounce? Give your answer to the nearest cm. [3]
4 marks
Answer
(a) First bounce: 300 × 4/5 = 240 cm [1]

(b) Second bounce: 240 × 4/5 = 192 cm [1]
Third bounce: 192 × 4/5 = 153.6 cm
Fourth bounce: 153.6 × 4/5 = 122.88 cm [1]
= 123 cm (nearest cm) [1]
Or use: 300 × (4/5)4 = 300 × 256/625 = 122.88 = 123 cm
You can calculate step by step (multiply by 4/5 each time) or use the power method. Both work. Show your working for partial marks.
