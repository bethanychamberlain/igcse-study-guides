Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Reflection Flips a shape over a mirror line. Image is the same size but reversed. Rotation (that's a turn, not a flip)
Rotation Turns a shape around a fixed point (the centre of rotation). Reflection (turn vs. flip — different transformations)
Translation Slides a shape without turning or flipping it. Described by a column vector. Enlargement (which changes size)
Enlargement Changes the size of a shape. All lengths multiplied by the scale factor. Scale factor < 1 makes it SMALLER (but it's still called an "enlargement")
Scale factor The number you multiply each length by in an enlargement. > 1 = bigger. Between 0 and 1 = smaller.
Column vector Two numbers: top = right (+) or left (−), bottom = up (+) or down (−). Coordinates — vectors describe movement, coordinates describe position
Bearing A direction measured clockwise from North, written as three figures (e.g. 045°). An angle — bearings always start from North, always go clockwise
Hypotenuse The longest side of a right-angled triangle, opposite the right angle. The other two sides (which are shorter)
Pythagoras' theorem a² + b² = c² where c is the hypotenuse. Only works for right-angled triangles. Trigonometry (that uses sin, cos, tan — different topic)

The Four Transformations — What You Must State

Transformation What It Does You MUST Give
Reflection Flips over a line The mirror line equation (e.g. y = 1, x = −2, y = x)
Rotation Turns around a point Centre + angle + direction (CW or ACW)
Translation Slides without turning The column vector
Enlargement Changes size Scale factor + centre of enlargement
Exam Trap — "Describe Fully"
"Describe fully the single transformation" means give ALL the details listed above. Saying "rotation 90°" without the centre and direction loses marks. Every missing detail = lost marks.

Quadrilateral Properties

Shape Sides Angles Symmetry
Square 4 equal All 90° 4 lines, rot. order 4
Rectangle 2 pairs equal All 90° 2 lines, rot. order 2
Rhombus 4 equal Opp. angles equal 2 lines, rot. order 2
Parallelogram Opp. sides equal & parallel Opp. angles equal No lines, rot. order 2
Trapezium 1 pair parallel Varies None (in general)
Kite 2 pairs adjacent equal 1 pair opp. equal 1 line, no rotation

Remember: Angles in ANY quadrilateral sum to 360°.

Method Cards — Step-by-Step

1. Pythagoras — Finding the Hypotenuse
1 Identify the two shorter sides (a and b) and the hypotenuse (c = longest, opposite the right angle).
2 Square both shorter sides: a² and b².
3 ADD them: a² + b².
4 Square root the result: c = √(a² + b²).
Sides 6 and 8: c² = 36 + 64 = 100 → c = √100 = 10 cm
2. Pythagoras — Finding a Shorter Side
1 Identify the hypotenuse (c) and the known shorter side (b). Find the missing side (a).
2 Rearrange: a² = c² − b².
3 SUBTRACT the squares.
4 Square root the result.
Hypotenuse 7, one side 4: a² = 49 − 16 = 33 → a = √33 = 5.74 cm (3 s.f.)
3. Distance Between Two Points
1 Find the horizontal distance: x2 − x1.
2 Find the vertical distance: y2 − y1.
3 Use Pythagoras: distance = √[(x2−x1)² + (y2−y1)²].
A(1,2) to B(6,4): √(5² + 2²) = √(25+4) = √29 = 5.39 (3 s.f.)
4. Back Bearing
1 If original bearing is less than 180°: back bearing = bearing + 180°.
2 If original bearing is 180° or more: back bearing = bearing − 180°.
Bearing 110°: back bearing = 110 + 180 = 290°

Say It in One Sentence

Reflection Every point is the same distance from the mirror line, but on the other side — like looking in a mirror.
Rotation The shape turns around a fixed point — you need centre, angle, and direction to describe it fully.
Translation The shape slides without turning or flipping — described by a column vector (across, up/down).
Enlargement Every length is multiplied by the scale factor, measured from the centre of enlargement.
Bearing From North, clockwise, three digits — stand at the "from" point and face North, then turn clockwise.
Pythagoras a² + b² = c² for right-angled triangles only — ADD squares for hypotenuse, SUBTRACT for a shorter side.
Rhombus A "squashed square" — all sides equal, but angles are NOT 90° (unless it's actually a square).

Memory Tricks

Transformation Checklist — "RR-TE"
Reflection = mirror line. Rotation = centre + angle + direction. Translation = vector. Enlargement = scale factor + centre. Before you write your answer, check you've given every required piece.
Pythagoras — "Hypotenuse = ADD, Short Side = SUBTRACT"
Finding the longest side? ADD the squares, then root. Finding a shorter side? SUBTRACT the squares, then root. Ask yourself first: "Am I finding the biggest or a smaller one?" That tells you the operation.
The 3-4-5 Triangle
3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5². This is the most famous Pythagorean triple. Multiples also work: 6-8-10, 9-12-15. Recognising these saves time and helps you check answers quickly.
Bearings — "From = Where You Stand"
"The bearing of B from A" means you're standing at A, facing North, turning clockwise toward B. Put your finger on the "from" point. Draw the North line there. Measure clockwise.
Column Vector — "x Goes Across, y Goes Up"
Top number = horizontal (positive = right, negative = left). Bottom number = vertical (positive = up, negative = down). Think of it as (right/left, up/down). Same as coordinate axes.
Quadrilateral Hierarchy
A square is a special rectangle AND a special rhombus. A rectangle and a rhombus are both special parallelograms. A parallelogram is a special trapezium (it has one pair of parallel sides — actually two). Understanding this "family tree" helps you remember properties.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Describe fully the single transformation" Name it + give ALL details: mirror line / centre+angle+direction / vector / scale factor+centre.
"Reflect shape A in the line y = 1" Draw y = 1. For each vertex, count squares to the line, then the same distance on the other side.
"Rotate shape B 90° clockwise about (0, 0)" Use tracing paper. Trace, pin at (0,0), turn 90° clockwise.
"Translate shape C by vector (3, −2)" Move every point 3 right and 2 down.
"Enlarge shape D by scale factor 2, centre (1, 1)" Measure each vertex's distance from (1,1), double it, plot new points.
"Find the bearing of B from A" Stand at A. Draw North. Measure clockwise. Write as 3 digits.
"Calculate the length of AC" (right-angled triangle) Use Pythagoras. Identify the hypotenuse. ADD squares or SUBTRACT squares, then root.
"Find the distance between (3, 1) and (7, 4)" Pythagoras on coordinates: √[(7−3)² + (4−1)²] = √(16+9) = √25 = 5.
"Name this quadrilateral and give a reason" State the name AND quote a specific property: "Rhombus, because all sides are equal but angles are not 90°."
Exam Trap — Pythagoras: Add or Subtract?
If your "shorter side" comes out longer than the hypotenuse, you added when you should have subtracted. The hypotenuse is always the longest side. Quick check: does c > a and c > b? If not, redo it.
