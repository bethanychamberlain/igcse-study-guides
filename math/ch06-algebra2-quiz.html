Foundation — Can You Do the Method?

Worth 1-3 marks each. These test the basic steps. Get these right before moving on.
1
Factorise completely:   6x + 15
1 mark
Full Working
1. Find HCF of 6 and 15: 3
2. 6x ÷ 3 = 2x.   15 ÷ 3 = 5.
3. Answer: 3(2x + 5)
Check: 3 × 2x = 6x.   3 × 5 = 15.   6x + 15. Correct.
1 mark for the correct factorised form. If you wrote 3(2x + 5), you got it.

2
Factorise completely:   8a2 − 12a
2 marks
Full Working
1. Find HCF of numbers: HCF of 8 and 12 = 4
2. Find HCF of letters: both have at least one a
3. Overall HCF = 4a
4. 8a2 ÷ 4a = 2a.   12a ÷ 4a = 3.
5. Answer: 4a(2a − 3)
Check: 4a × 2a = 8a2.   4a × (−3) = −12a.   8a2 − 12a. Correct.
1 mark for taking out a common factor (e.g. 4 or 2a). 2 marks for the FULLY factorised form 4a(2a − 3). "Completely" means maximum HCF.

Full Working

Intermediate — Can You Apply the Method?

Worth 3-4 marks each. These need multiple steps and careful working.
5
Solve these simultaneous equations:
2x + y = 7
x − y = 2
4 marks
Full Working
1. Label: (1) 2x + y = 7   (2) x − y = 2
2. Look at y: (1) has +y, (2) has −y. Different signs → ADD.
3. (1) + (2):   2x + x + y + (−y) = 7 + 2
  3x = 9
4. x = 9 ÷ 3 = 3 [1 mark]
5. Substitute x = 3 into (2):   3 − y = 2  →  y = 3 − 2 = 1 [1 mark]
Check: Put into (1): 2(3) + 1 = 6 + 1 = 7. Correct!
Answer: x = 3, y = 1
Marks: 1 for correct method (adding/subtracting), 1 for eliminating a variable, 1 for x = 3, 1 for y = 1. Show all steps.

6
For the sequence 2,   9,   16,   23,   30, ...
(a) Find the nth term.   (b) What is the 40th term?
4 marks
Full Working
(a)
1. Common difference: 9 − 2 = 7 [1 mark]
2. Starts with 7n.
3. When n = 1: 7 × 1 = 7. But first term is 2. Adjustment: 2 − 7 = −5
4. nth term = 7n − 5 [1 mark]
Check: n=1: 7(1)−5 = 2. n=2: 7(2)−5 = 9. n=3: 7(3)−5 = 16. All correct.
(b)
5. 40th term: 7(40) − 5 = 280 − 5 = 275 [1 mark for substitution, 1 mark for answer]
Always verify the nth term formula against the first few terms before using it. Show the substitution for part (b) — don't just write the answer.

The formula for the area of a triangle is:   A = ½ b h
Rearrange to make h the subject.
3 marks
Full Working
1. Start:   A = ½ b h
2. Multiply both sides by 2 (undo the ½):   2A = bh [1 mark]
3. Divide both sides by b (undo the ×b):   2A / b = h [1 mark]
4. Rewrite:   h = 2A / b [1 mark]
Check: If A = 12, b = 4: h = 2(12)/4 = 6.   Check: ½ × 4 × 6 = 12. Correct.
Each rearrangement step earns a mark. Write a new line for every operation.

Advanced — Can You Solve Multi-Step Problems?

Worth 4-6 marks each. These combine skills and require very careful working. This is where exam grades are won.
8
A shop sells apples and pears. 3 apples and 2 pears cost $2.50. 5 apples and 2 pears cost $3.50.
Find the cost of one apple and one pear.
5 marks
Full Working
1. Let a = cost of one apple, p = cost of one pear. [1 mark for setting up]
2. Write equations:   (1) 3a + 2p = 2.50   (2) 5a + 2p = 3.50
3. Both have +2p (same sign) → subtract.
4. (2) − (1):   5a − 3a + 2p − 2p = 3.50 − 2.50
  2a = 1.00 [1 mark]
5. a = 1.00 ÷ 2 = $0.50 [1 mark]
6. Substitute into (1):   3(0.50) + 2p = 2.50  →  1.50 + 2p = 2.50  →  2p = 1.00  →  p = $0.50 [1 mark]
Check: (2): 5(0.50) + 2(0.50) = 2.50 + 1.00 = 3.50. Correct! [1 mark for check]
Answer: One apple costs $0.50, one pear costs $0.50.
Word problems: ALWAYS start by defining your variables ("Let a = ..."). Then write the equations. Then solve normally. The marks are: define variables, form equations, correct method, both answers, check.

9
The formula for kinetic energy is:   E = ½ m v2
Rearrange to make v the subject.
4 marks
Full Working
1. Start:   E = ½ m v2
2. Multiply both sides by 2:   2E = mv2 [1 mark]
3. Divide both sides by m:   2E / m = v2 [1 mark]
4. Square root both sides:   v = √(2E / m) [1 mark]
Check: If E = 100, m = 8: v = √(200/8) = √25 = 5.   Check: ½ × 8 × 25 = 100. Correct! [1 mark]
Reverse BIDMAS: undo the ½ first (×2), then undo ×m (÷m), then undo the square (√). Three clear steps = three method marks.

10
Simplify:   2x/3  +  (x + 4)/6
4 marks
Full Working
1. Find LCD of 3 and 6: LCD = 6 [1 mark]
2. First fraction: multiply top and bottom by 2:   2x/3 = 4x/6
3. Second fraction already has denominator 6:   (x + 4)/6
4. Add the tops:   [4x + (x + 4)] / 6 [1 mark]
5. Simplify the top:   4x + x + 4 = 5x + 4 [1 mark]
6. Answer: (5x + 4) / 6 [1 mark]
Check: Try x = 2:   2(2)/3 + (2+4)/6 = 4/3 + 6/6 = 4/3 + 1 = 7/3.   Formula: (5(2)+4)/6 = 14/6 = 7/3. Correct!
Key steps: find LCD, convert both fractions, combine tops carefully, simplify. If it was subtraction, you'd need brackets around the second top.
