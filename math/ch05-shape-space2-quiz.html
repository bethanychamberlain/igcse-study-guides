Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
State the four types of transformation and, for each one, list the information you must give to describe it fully.
4 marks
Answer
Reflection — equation of the mirror line [1].
Rotation — centre, angle, and direction (clockwise/anticlockwise) [1].
Translation — the column vector [1].
Enlargement — scale factor and centre of enlargement [1].
This is pure recall. If you can list these four with their details from memory, you'll never lose marks on "describe fully" questions.

State Pythagoras' theorem. Which side is the hypotenuse?
2 marks
Answer
a2 + b2 = c2 [1], where c is the hypotenuse — the longest side, which is opposite the right angle [1].
One mark for the formula, one for identifying the hypotenuse correctly.

3
State the three rules you must follow when writing a bearing.
3 marks
Answer
1. Bearings are measured from North [1].
2. Bearings are measured clockwise [1].
3. Bearings are written as three figures (e.g. 045° not 45°) [1].
All three rules needed for full marks. The three-figure rule is the one students forget most often.

4
Name a quadrilateral that has:
(a) four equal sides but angles that are NOT all 90°
(b) exactly one pair of parallel sides
(c) two pairs of adjacent equal sides and one line of symmetry
3 marks
Answer
(a) Rhombus [1]
(b) Trapezium [1]
(c) Kite [1]
Knowing the properties of each quadrilateral is key. A rhombus has equal sides but only opposite angles equal. A square also has four equal sides but all 90° — so part (a) is specifically the rhombus.

Intermediate — Can You Apply It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to show clear working and apply the methods.
5
A right-angled triangle has shorter sides of 5 cm and 12 cm. Find the length of the hypotenuse.
3 marks
Answer
c2 = 52 + 122 [1]
c2 = 25 + 144 = 169 [1]
c = √169 = 13 cm [1]
5-12-13 is a Pythagorean triple. If you recognised it, great! But still show the working for full marks.

6
The bearing of town B from town A is 065°.
(a) What is the bearing of A from B?
(b) Town C is due South of A. What is the bearing of C from A?
3 marks
Answer
(a) Back bearing = 065 + 180 = 245° [2 — 1 for method, 1 for answer]
(b) Due South = 180° [1]
For back bearings: if the original is less than 180, add 180. If 180 or more, subtract 180. Due South is always 180°.

7
Triangle P has vertices at (1, 2), (3, 2), and (3, 5). It is translated by the vector (4, −3) to give triangle Q.
(a) Write down the coordinates of the vertices of Q.
(b) What vector would translate Q back to P?
4 marks
Answer
(a) Add (4, −3) to each vertex:
(1+4, 2−3) = (5, −1)
(3+4, 2−3) = (7, −1)
(3+4, 5−3) = (7, 2) [2 — 1 for method, 1 for all three correct]

(b) The reverse vector is (−4, 3) [2 — reverse the signs of both components].
The reverse translation always has the opposite signs. If you went (4, −3) forward, you go (−4, 3) back.

Advanced — Can You Solve Multi-Step Problems?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need multiple steps, clear reasoning, and careful working.
8
A rectangle has length 9 cm and width 4 cm.
(a) Calculate the length of the diagonal.
(b) A square has the same diagonal length. Find the side length of the square. Give your answer to 3 significant figures.
5 marks
Answer
(a) d2 = 92 + 42 [1] = 81 + 16 = 97 [1]
d = √97 = 9.85 cm (3 s.f.) [1]

(b) For a square with diagonal d, both sides are equal (call them s):
s2 + s2 = 97  →  2s2 = 97 [1]
s2 = 48.5  →  s = √48.5 = 6.96 cm (3 s.f.) [1]
Part (b) is harder because you need to realise that in a square, the diagonal creates TWO equal shorter sides. So a2 + a2 = d2 becomes 2a2 = d2.

9
In a parallelogram ABCD, angle ABC = 72° and angle BAC = 28°.
(a) Find angle ADC.
(b) Find angle ACD.
(c) Find angle ACB.
5 marks
Answer
(a) In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal. But adjacent angles are supplementary (add to 180°).
Angle ADC = 180° − 72° = 108° [2 — 1 for knowing co-interior angles sum to 180°, 1 for answer].

(b) In triangle ACD: angle ADC = 108°. Since AB is parallel to DC, angle ACD = angle BAC = 28° (alternate angles) [1]. So angle ACD = 28°.

(c) Angle ACB = angle ABC − angle BAC... wait, angle ACB is part of triangle ABC.
In triangle ABC: angles sum to 180°.
Angle ACB = 180° − 72° − 28° = 80° [2 — 1 for angles in a triangle, 1 for answer].
Draw the parallelogram and label all the angles. Remember: opposite angles are equal, co-interior (same-side) angles sum to 180°, alternate angles between parallel lines are equal.

10
A ship sails 20 km due North from port P and then 35 km due East to reach point Q.
(a) Calculate the direct distance from P to Q.
(b) Calculate the bearing of Q from P. Give your answer to the nearest degree.
6 marks
Answer
(a) The journey forms a right-angled triangle with legs 20 km (North) and 35 km (East).
PQ2 = 202 + 352 [1]
PQ2 = 400 + 1225 = 1625 [1]
PQ = √1625 = 40.3 km (3 s.f.) [1]

(b) The angle at P (between North and PQ):
tan(angle) = opposite ÷ adjacent = 35 ÷ 20 = 1.75 [1]
angle = tan−1(1.75) = 60.3° [1]
Bearing = 060° (to nearest degree) [1]
Part (b) combines Pythagoras with bearings and basic trigonometry. The angle is measured from North (the 20 km leg), so the "opposite" is the 35 km East leg. The bearing is the angle itself, written as three figures. If you haven't covered trig yet, you could solve this by accurate scale drawing instead.
