Foundation — Can You Calculate It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
The price of a laptop increases from $800 to $920. Calculate the percentage increase.
2 marks
Answer
Actual increase = $920 − $800 = $120 [1]
Percentage increase = (120 ÷ 800) × 100 = 15% [1]
Remember: always divide by the ORIGINAL value ($800), not the new value ($920).

2
Convert the following: (a) Change 3/8 to a decimal. (b) Change 0.45 to a fraction in its simplest form. (c) Change 7/20 to a percentage.
3 marks
Answer
(a) 3 ÷ 8 = 0.375 [1]
(b) 0.45 = 45/100 = 9/20 [1]
(c) (7/20) × 100 = 35% [1]
For (b), you must simplify. 45/100 alone would not get the mark. Divide both by 5.

3
A length is measured as 56 cm, correct to the nearest centimetre. Write down (a) the lower bound and (b) the upper bound for this length.
2 marks
Answer
(a) Lower bound = 55.5 cm [1]
(b) Upper bound = 56.5 cm [1]
"Nearest cm" means the error is ±0.5 cm. Lower = 56 − 0.5 = 55.5. Upper = 56 + 0.5 = 56.5. In inequality form: 55.5 ≤ length < 56.5.

4
Work out, without a calculator: 5 + (28 + 5) ÷ 3
1 mark
Answer
Brackets first: 28 + 5 = 33.
Then divide: 33 ÷ 3 = 11.
Then add: 5 + 11 = 16 [1]
BIDMAS: do the brackets first, then division, then addition. A common wrong answer is 38/3 if you add 5 + 28 first.

Intermediate — Can You Apply It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to show working and apply methods to word problems.
5
Juan buys a car for $4000 and sells it for $4600. (a) Calculate his actual profit. (b) Calculate his percentage profit.
3 marks
Answer
(a) Profit = $4600 − $4000 = $600 [1]
(b) Percentage profit = (600 ÷ 4000) × 100 [1] = 15% [1]
For percentage profit, divide by the COST price ($4000), not the selling price. Show the formula for method marks.

6
Estimate the answer to (81.4 × 98.2) by rounding each number to one significant figure. Show your working.
3 marks
Answer
81.4 ≈ 80 [1]
98.2 ≈ 100 [1]
80 × 100 = 8000 [1]
You MUST show the rounded values to get method marks. Just writing "about 8000" without showing the rounding loses a mark. The actual answer is 7993.48, so 8000 is an excellent estimate.

7
Arrange in order of size, smallest first:   1/2,   45%,   0.6
3 marks
Answer
Convert all to decimals [1]:
1/2 = 0.5,   45% = 0.45,   0.6 = 0.6 [1]
Order: 45%,   1/2,   0.6 (i.e. 0.45, 0.5, 0.6) [1]
Show your conversions. If you just write the final order without showing how you converted, you may lose a method mark if the order is wrong.

Advanced — Can You Problem-Solve?

Worth 4-6 marks each. Multi-step problems that combine skills. This is where exam grades are won or lost.
8
A house was built in 2015 at a total cost of: wages $35,000, materials $18,000, overheads $4,500. In 2016, wages increased by 10%, materials increased by 5%, and overheads stayed the same. (a) Find the total cost in 2016. (b) Calculate the percentage increase in total cost from 2015 to 2016.
5 marks
Answer
Total cost 2015 = $35,000 + $18,000 + $4,500 = $57,500 [1]

2016 wages: $35,000 × 1.10 = $38,500 [1]
2016 materials: $18,000 × 1.05 = $18,900 [1]
2016 overheads: $4,500 (unchanged)
Total 2016 = $38,500 + $18,900 + $4,500 = $61,900 [1]

Percentage increase = (($61,900 − $57,500) ÷ $57,500) × 100 = (4400 ÷ 57500) × 100 = 7.7% (to 1 d.p.) [1]
Common mistake: applying 10% and 5% to the TOTAL instead of to each component separately. Each percentage applies only to its own item.

9
The length and width of a rectangle are measured to the nearest 0.1 cm as 8.4 cm and 4.3 cm. (a) Write down the upper bound for the length. (b) Write down the lower bound for the width. (c) Explain why the actual area of the rectangle could be different from 8.4 × 4.3 = 36.12 cm2.
4 marks
Answer
(a) Upper bound for length = 8.4 + 0.05 = 8.45 cm [1]
(b) Lower bound for width = 4.3 − 0.05 = 4.25 cm [1]
(c) Both measurements are rounded [1]. The actual length could be anywhere from 8.35 to 8.45 cm, and the actual width from 4.25 to 4.35 cm, so the actual area could range from 8.35 × 4.25 = 35.4875 cm2 to 8.45 × 4.35 = 36.7575 cm2 [1].
"Nearest 0.1 cm" means the error is ±0.05 cm. For part (c), you need to explain that both measurements are approximations, so multiplying them gives an approximate area.

10
A student uses a calculator to work out (6.1 × 32.6) / 19.3 and gets the answer 10.3 (to 3 s.f.). Without using a calculator, show by estimating that 10.3 is a reasonable answer.
4 marks
Answer
Round each number to 1 significant figure [1]:
6.1 ≈ 6,   32.6 ≈ 30,   19.3 ≈ 20 [1]
Estimate = (6 × 30) / 20 = 180 / 20 = 9 [1]
9 is close to 10.3, so the answer is reasonable [1].
Show every step: the rounded values, the estimated calculation, and a clear concluding statement that the estimate is close to 10.3. All four are needed for full marks.
