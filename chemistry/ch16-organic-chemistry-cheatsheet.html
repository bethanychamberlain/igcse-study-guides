Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Hydrocarbon A compound containing ONLY carbon and hydrogen atoms (e.g. methane CH₄). Organic compound (organic compounds may also contain O, N, etc. — not just C and H)
Saturated All bonds are single bonds. No C=C double bond. Cannot react by addition. Unsaturated (has a C=C double bond — can react by addition)
Unsaturated Contains at least one C=C double bond. Can decolourise bromine water. Saturated (single bonds only — no reaction with bromine water)
Homologous series A family of organic compounds with the same functional group and general formula, differing by CH₂. Isomers (same formula, different structure — not the same as a series)
Functional group The atom or group that determines how a compound reacts. E.g. C=C, –OH, –COOH. —
Cracking Breaking large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller, more useful ones using heat and a catalyst. Fractional distillation (that separates but does not break molecules)
Structural isomers Compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural formulae. Members of the same homologous series (those have different formulae, differing by CH₂)
Ester Made from carboxylic acid + alcohol. Has a pleasant smell. Used in perfumes and flavourings. Carboxylic acid (the acid is sour; the ester is sweet-smelling)

Exam Trap — Alkane vs Alkene
One letter changes everything! Alkane = saturated (single bonds, unreactive). Alkene = unsaturated (double bond, reactive). If you write the wrong one, you lose all the marks.

Say It in One Sentence

Petroleum A mixture of hundreds of hydrocarbons, separated into useful fractions by fractional distillation.
Fractional distillation Heat petroleum, vapours rise up a tower, each fraction condenses at its own boiling point height.
Cracking Breaks long-chain hydrocarbons into shorter alkanes + alkenes using heat and a catalyst (zeolite).
Bromine water test Orange bromine water goes colourless with an alkene (unsaturated) but stays orange with an alkane (saturated).
Combustion Complete (plenty of O₂) = CO₂ + H₂O. Incomplete (limited O₂) = CO + soot + H₂O.
Four-family chain Alkanes → (crack) → Alkenes → (add steam) → Alcohols → (oxidise) → Carboxylic acids.
Ester Carboxylic acid + alcohol → ester + water. Pleasant smell. Used in perfumes and food flavourings.

Memory Tricks

The Four Families Chain
Picture a factory production line: Alk-A-nes (raw material from oil) get cracked into Alk-E-nes (reactive), which get steamed into Alc-OH-ols, which get oxidised into Acids. Each step adds something or breaks something.
Naming Prefixes — "Met Ethel, Proper Butcher"
Meth = 1 carbon. Ethel = 2 carbons. Proper = 3 carbons. Butcher = 4 carbons. Then add the ending: -ane, -ene, -ol, or -oic acid.
Alkane = A = All single bonds
Alkane → All single bonds → sAturated. Alkene → has a doublE bond → unsaturatEd. The vowel tells you the bond type.
Trends Going Down the Tower
As chains get Longer: boiling point goes Up, viscosity goes Up, flammability goes Down. Remember "LUU-D" — Longer = Up, Up, Down.
Substitution vs Addition
Alkanes do substitution (swap). Alkenes do addition (add across the double bond). "A does S, E does A" — or think: alkenes have a "door" (double bond) that opens to let atoms ADD on.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"What is a hydrocarbon?" A compound containing only carbon and hydrogen
"How would you test for an alkene?" Add bromine water — it turns from orange to colourless (decolourises)
"Give two reasons for cracking" 1. To produce shorter-chain fuels (more demand). 2. To produce alkenes (for plastics)
"Why is petrol more flammable than diesel?" Petrol has shorter chains, a lower boiling point, and is more volatile
"What is the functional group of alcohols?" –OH (hydroxyl group)
"Compare fermentation and hydration for making ethanol" Fermentation: renewable, slow, impure. Hydration: non-renewable, fast, pure. Give two differences for full marks
"What are structural isomers?" Compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural formulae
"What is an ester? How is it made?" Sweet-smelling compound made from carboxylic acid + alcohol (with acid catalyst). Water is also produced
"Write the equation for complete combustion of methane" CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
