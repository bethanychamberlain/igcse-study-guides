Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Eutrophication A chain of events where fertilisers cause algae blooms, oxygen depletion, and fish death in waterways. Pollution (eutrophication is a specific type of water pollution, not all pollution)
Greenhouse effect CO₂ and CH₄ in the atmosphere trap heat re-emitted by Earth. Natural and necessary for life. Global warming (that's the ENHANCED greenhouse effect caused by extra CO₂/CH₄)
Global warming The increase in Earth's average temperature due to rising levels of greenhouse gases from human activity. Greenhouse effect (natural = good; enhanced = bad)
Acid rain Rain made acidic by dissolved SO₂ and nitrogen oxides from burning fossil fuels. Damages buildings and kills trees. Normal rain (even clean rain is slightly acidic from dissolved CO₂, pH ~5.6)
Carbon monoxide CO — colourless, odourless, poisonous gas from incomplete combustion. Binds to haemoglobin, blocking oxygen transport. Carbon dioxide (CO₂ causes global warming; CO is the poison)
Particulates Tiny solid particles (soot) from incomplete combustion. Cause respiratory problems and cancer. Gas pollutants (particulates are solid, not gas)
Flue gas desulfurisation Treating power station exhaust with calcium oxide to remove SO₂ before it causes acid rain. Catalytic converter (that's in cars; FGD is in power stations)
Aquifer Underground water stored in porous rock. A clean source for drinking water. Reservoir (that's above-ground water storage)

The Five Air Pollutants — Side by Side

Pollutant Source Harm
CO₂ Complete combustion of fuels Greenhouse gas → global warming
CO Incomplete combustion Binds to haemoglobin → death
Particulates Incomplete combustion (diesel) Lung damage, cancer
SO₂ Burning fuels with sulfur Acid rain
NO / NO₂ N₂ + O₂ in hot engines Acid rain, smog
Exam Trap — Where Do Nitrogen Oxides Come From?
NOT from the fuel! Nitrogen oxides form when nitrogen and oxygen in the air react at the high temperatures inside engines and furnaces. The exam loves this distinction.

CO₂ vs Methane as Greenhouse Gases

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) Methane (CH₄)
Sources Burning fossil fuels, deforestation Livestock, rice paddies, natural gas leaks
Amount in air Much more Much less
Trapping power 1× (baseline) ~28× more powerful per molecule
How to reduce Renewables, plant trees, carbon capture Rear fewer cattle, eat less meat

Say It in One Sentence

Air composition 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, ~1% argon (plus traces of CO₂ and noble gases).
Eutrophication Fertilisers → algae bloom → blocks light → plants die → bacteria decompose → O₂ used up → fish die.
Greenhouse effect Sun heats Earth, Earth re-emits heat, CO₂/CH₄ trap that heat in the atmosphere — this is natural and keeps us warm.
Water test White anhydrous copper(II) sulfate turns blue; blue cobalt(II) chloride paper turns pink.
Pure water test Boils at exactly 100 °C and freezes at exactly 0 °C.
Catalytic converter Converts CO to CO₂ and nitrogen oxides to harmless N₂ in car exhausts.

Memory Tricks

Eutrophication Domino Chain
Picture dominos falling: Fertiliser → Algae → Blocks light → Plants die → Bacteria decompose → Oxygen gone → Fish die. Each step causes the next. Memorise the chain — each step = 1 exam mark.
CO vs CO₂
CO = Colourless Odourless killer (poison). CO₂ = Climate Overheater (greenhouse gas). One "O" = one problem (poison). Two "O"s = two problems (warming + climate change).
Water Treatment — Five Steps in Order
Source → Solids out (filter/settle/floc) → Dissolved stuff out (carbon) → Kill microbes (chlorine) → Store. "Some Students Don't Keep Studying" = Source, Solids, Dissolved, Kill, Store.
Water Test Colours
Copper sulfate turns Blue (C before B in the alphabet). Cobalt chloride turns Pink (think: "Co-Pink" = cobalt = pink).

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Name a pollutant that causes acid rain" Sulfur dioxide (or nitrogen oxides)
"Explain how carbon monoxide is harmful" It binds to haemoglobin in red blood cells, preventing them from carrying oxygen — can cause death
"Explain how fertilisers harm a river" (4-5 marks) Fertilisers → algae grow → block light → plants die → bacteria use O₂ to decompose them → fish suffocate
"Suggest two ways to reduce CO₂" 1. Use renewable energy (wind/solar) instead of fossil fuels. 2. Plant more trees (absorb CO₂ by photosynthesis)
"What is the difference between the greenhouse effect and global warming?" Greenhouse effect is natural (keeps Earth warm). Global warming is the enhanced effect from extra human-produced CO₂/CH₄
"How would you test that a liquid contains water?" Add to white anhydrous copper(II) sulfate — turns blue
"How would you show that water is pure?" Measure boiling point (100 °C) and freezing point (0 °C) — must be sharp and exact
"Name the composition of clean dry air" 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, ~1% argon
