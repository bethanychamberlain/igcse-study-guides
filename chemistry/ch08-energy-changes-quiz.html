Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Define exothermic reaction and state how it affects the temperature of the surroundings.
2 marks
Answer
An exothermic reaction is one that gives out thermal energy to the surroundings [1]. The temperature of the surroundings rises / increases [1].
The key phrase is "gives out energy to the surroundings." Don't just say "it gets hot" — say where the energy goes.

2
State whether each of the following is exothermic or endothermic: (a) burning methane, (b) thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate, (c) neutralisation of an acid with an alkali.
3 marks
Answer
(a) Burning methane (combustion) is exothermic [1].
(b) Thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate is endothermic [1].
(c) Neutralisation is exothermic [1].
Combustion and neutralisation are always exothermic. Thermal decomposition is always endothermic (the clue is in the name — it needs thermal energy).

3
In an exothermic reaction, which have more energy — the reactants or the products?
1 mark
Answer
The reactants have more energy [1]. In an exothermic reaction, energy has been given out, so the products end up with less energy.
Think of the energy level diagram: reactants are drawn high, products are drawn low. The difference is the energy given out.

4
Define activation energy and give its symbol.
2 marks
Answer
Activation energy is the minimum energy that colliding particles must have in order to react [1]. Its symbol is Ea [1].
"Minimum energy" is the key phrase. Don't just say "the energy needed to start a reaction" — it's about the particles colliding with enough energy.

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY using energy concepts, not just name things.
5
Water at 25°C is used to dissolve two compounds. The temperature of the ammonium nitrate solution is 21°C and the temperature of the calcium chloride solution is 45°C. Which compound dissolved exothermically? Explain your reasoning.
3 marks
Answer
Calcium chloride dissolved exothermically [1]. The temperature of the solution increased from 25°C to 45°C [1], which means thermal energy was given out to the surroundings (the water), so the process was exothermic [1]. Ammonium nitrate dissolved endothermically because the temperature fell to 21°C — it took in energy from the water.
Three ideas: (1) name the compound, (2) state the temperature change as evidence, (3) link temperature rise to energy being given out = exothermic.

6
In every reaction, the line on a reaction pathway diagram curves upwards first, even for exothermic reactions. Explain why, using the ideas of bond breaking and bond making.
3 marks
Answer
Before new bonds can form, the bonds in the reactant molecules must first be broken [1]. Bond breaking is an endothermic process — it requires energy to be taken in [1]. This initial energy input (the activation energy) causes the energy level to rise, so the line curves upwards first [1]. Only after bonds are broken can new bonds form and release energy, causing the line to come back down.
The chain: bonds must break first → breaking bonds takes in energy → energy level rises (line goes up). Then new bonds form, energy is released, line goes down.

7
A reaction has ΔH = −184 kJ/mol. (a) What does the symbol ΔH stand for? (b) What does the negative sign tell you? (c) Is this reaction exothermic or endothermic?
3 marks
Answer
(a) ΔH stands for the enthalpy change of the reaction [1].
(b) The negative sign tells you that energy is given out / released to the surroundings [1].
(c) The reaction is exothermic [1].
Negative ΔH = exothermic (energy out). Positive ΔH = endothermic (energy in). If you remember one, you know the other.

Advanced — Can You Calculate and Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need calculations, full sentences, and clear reasoning.
8
Hydrogen reacts with chlorine to form hydrogen chloride:
H2 + Cl2 → 2HCl
Using the bond energies H—H = 436 kJ/mol, Cl—Cl = 242 kJ/mol, H—Cl = 431 kJ/mol, calculate the enthalpy change for this reaction. State whether it is exothermic or endothermic.
5 marks
Answer
Energy in (bonds broken):
1 × H—H = 436 kJ [1]
1 × Cl—Cl = 242 kJ
Total energy in = 436 + 242 = 678 kJ [1]

Energy out (bonds formed):
2 × H—Cl = 2 × 431 = 862 kJ [1]

ΔH = 678 − 862 = −184 kJ/mol [1]

The reaction is exothermic (because ΔH is negative) [1].
Show every step clearly. Even if you make an arithmetic mistake, you get marks for the method: listing bonds broken, listing bonds formed, using the formula, and interpreting the sign.

9
The decomposition of ammonia is: 2NH3 → N2 + 3H2
Using the bond energies N—H = 391 kJ/mol, N≡N = 946 kJ/mol, H—H = 436 kJ/mol, calculate ΔH and explain what the sign of your answer tells you about this reaction.
6 marks
Answer
Energy in (bonds broken in 2NH3):
Each NH3 has 3 N—H bonds, so 2 molecules = 6 N—H bonds
6 × 391 = 2346 kJ [1]

Energy out (bonds formed in N2 + 3H2):
1 × N≡N = 946 kJ [1]
3 × H—H = 3 × 436 = 1308 kJ [1]
Total energy out = 946 + 1308 = 2254 kJ

ΔH = 2346 − 2254 = +92 kJ/mol [1]

The positive sign means the reaction is endothermic [1] — more energy is taken in to break bonds than is released when new bonds form, so overall energy is absorbed from the surroundings [1].
6 marks, 6 ideas: count bonds broken, calculate energy in, count bonds formed, calculate energy out, apply the formula, interpret the positive sign. Show all working.

10
A hydrogen-oxygen fuel cell is used to power a car. (a) State the overall reaction in the fuel cell. (b) What is the only chemical product? (c) Give two advantages and two disadvantages of using a hydrogen fuel cell compared with a petrol engine.
6 marks
Answer
(a) 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O (hydrogen and oxygen react to produce water and electrical energy) [1]

(b) The only chemical product is water [1].

(c) Advantages (any two):
• The only product is water, which is harmless / non-polluting, whereas petrol produces CO2 (climate change), CO, and NOx [1].
• Hydrogen can be made from water by electrolysis using renewable energy [1].

Disadvantages (any two):
• Hydrogen must be stored as a gas under high pressure, which is difficult and expensive [1].
• Hydrogen filling stations are not yet widely available [1].
Also accepted: most hydrogen is currently obtained from fossil fuels; fuel cell cars cost more than petrol cars.
The exam wants specific, contrasting points. "Better for the environment" alone is too vague — say WHY (only produces water, not CO2). For disadvantages, give practical reasons (storage, infrastructure), not just "it's expensive."
