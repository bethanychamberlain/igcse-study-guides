Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
State the approximate percentage composition of clean dry air.
2 marks
Answer
Nitrogen: 78%, oxygen: 21% [1]. The remaining ~1% is mainly argon, with small amounts of carbon dioxide and traces of other noble gases [1].
78-21-1 is one of the most recalled facts in chemistry. The 1% is mostly argon, NOT carbon dioxide (CO₂ is only about 0.04%).

Name the two main greenhouse gases and give one source for each.
2 marks
Answer
Carbon dioxide (CO₂) — from burning fossil fuels (or deforestation) [1].
Methane (CH₄) — from livestock/grazing animals (or rice paddy fields, or natural gas leaks) [1].
Any valid source gets the mark. Just make sure you match the right source to the right gas.

3
What is a fertiliser? Name the three elements that an NPK fertiliser provides.
2 marks
Answer
A fertiliser is a substance added to soil to make it more fertile / to replace nutrients [1]. An NPK fertiliser provides nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K) [1].
NPK literally stands for nitrogen-phosphorus-potassium. Make sure you say phosphorus, not phosphate (phosphorus is the element).

4
Describe two tests you could use to show that a liquid contains water.
2 marks
Answer
1. Add the liquid to white anhydrous copper(II) sulfate — it turns blue if water is present [1].
2. Add the liquid to blue cobalt(II) chloride paper — it turns pink if water is present [1].
These tests prove the liquid CONTAINS water, not that it IS pure water. To prove purity, check boiling point = 100°C and freezing point = 0°C.

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY, not just name things.
5
Describe the steps involved in treating river water to make it safe to drink.
4 marks
Answer
1. Choose a clean water source (river or aquifer) [1].
2. Remove solid particles by filtration (through sand), sedimentation (letting particles settle), or flocculation (adding a coagulant to clump particles together) [1].
3. Filter through carbon (activated charcoal) to remove dissolved compounds, bad tastes, smells, and some toxic metals [1].
4. Add chlorine to kill microbes (bacteria and viruses) [1].
5. Store in a clean covered reservoir.
The examiner typically awards 1 mark per distinct step. Mentioning chlorine specifically (not just "a chemical") is important. Some mark schemes also credit adding fluoride to prevent tooth decay.

6
Explain how fertilisers can cause fish in a river to die. Use the term eutrophication in your answer.
4 marks
Answer
Fertilisers (nitrates and phosphates) are washed from farmland into the river [1]. They promote rapid growth of algae on the water surface [1]. When the algae die, bacteria feed on the dead algae and decompose them, using up the dissolved oxygen in the water [1]. Fish suffocate (die from lack of oxygen). This process is called eutrophication [1].
The chain: fertilisers → algae bloom → bacteria decompose dead algae → oxygen used up → fish die. Each link is a mark. Using the word "eutrophication" is required when the question asks for it.

7
Carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide are both produced by burning fossil fuels. Explain how each is formed, and describe the harm each one causes.
4 marks
Answer
Carbon dioxide (CO₂): formed by complete combustion of carbon-containing fuels (when there is plenty of air/oxygen) [1]. It is a greenhouse gas that contributes to global warming and climate change [1].

Carbon monoxide (CO): formed by incomplete combustion of carbon-containing fuels (when there is a limited supply of air/oxygen, e.g. in car engines) [1]. It is poisonous — it binds to haemoglobin in blood, preventing it from carrying oxygen, which can cause death [1].
The key distinction: complete (plenty of air) vs incomplete (limited air). CO₂ causes global warming. CO is a direct poison. Don't mix them up.

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning.
8
Explain how the greenhouse effect works. Then explain why increasing levels of greenhouse gases are a problem, and give three predicted impacts of climate change.
6 marks
Answer
The greenhouse effect: The Sun's energy reaches Earth as light [1]. Earth absorbs this and re-emits it as heat (infrared/thermal energy). Greenhouse gases (CO₂ and CH₄) in the atmosphere absorb this heat and re-radiate it back, keeping Earth warm [1].

The problem: Human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation, farming livestock) are increasing the levels of CO₂ and CH₄. This means more heat is trapped, causing Earth's average temperature to rise (global warming) [1].

Three impacts of climate change (any three):
• Melting polar ice/glaciers → sea level rise → flooding of low-lying areas [1]
• More frequent and severe storms, droughts, heatwaves, and wildfires [1]
• Species extinction / spread of tropical diseases to new areas / crop failures / climate refugees [1]
The greenhouse effect itself is natural and good. The problem is the ENHANCED greenhouse effect from extra gases. Three distinct impacts needed — don't repeat the same idea in different words.

9
Describe three different ways to reduce acid rain. For each, explain which pollutant it targets and how it works.
6 marks
Answer
1. Use low-sulfur fuels: Targets sulfur dioxide (SO₂). Sulfur is removed from coal, gas, and petroleum fuels before they are burned, so less SO₂ is produced [1+1].

2. Flue gas desulfurisation: Targets SO₂. Waste gases from power stations are treated with a slurry of calcium oxide (lime). The SO₂ reacts with calcium oxide to form calcium sulfite (CaSO₃), removing the SO₂ before it reaches the atmosphere [1+1].

3. Catalytic converters: Targets nitrogen oxides (NO/NO₂) and also removes carbon monoxide. Fitted in car exhaust systems, they use platinum/rhodium/palladium catalysts to convert nitrogen oxides to harmless nitrogen gas, and CO to CO₂ [1+1].
For each method: name it, say which pollutant it removes, and explain how it works. That's 2 marks per method for full marks.

10
Suggest and explain four different ways that humans could reduce the levels of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
6 marks
Answer
1. Use renewable energy (wind, solar, hydroelectric, tidal) instead of burning fossil fuels to generate electricity. This produces no CO₂ [1+1].

2. Plant more trees (and stop deforestation). Trees absorb CO₂ during photosynthesis: carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen [1+1].

3. Use electric vehicles or hydrogen fuel cells for transport instead of petrol/diesel engines. Electric vehicles produce no exhaust gases; hydrogen fuel cells produce only water [1].

4. Reduce methane by eating less meat from grazing animals (cattle, sheep). Rear fewer livestock, eat more plant-based food, fish, or chicken instead [1].
Give four DIFFERENT strategies. The examiner wants variety — don't give two types of renewable energy as separate answers. Link each to the specific gas it targets (CO₂ or CH₄). Including the photosynthesis equation shows top-level understanding.
