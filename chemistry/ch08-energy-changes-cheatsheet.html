Energy Changes in Reactions

Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Exothermic A reaction that gives out thermal energy to the surroundings. Surroundings get hotter. Endothermic (opposite — takes in energy, surroundings cool down)
Endothermic A reaction that takes in thermal energy from the surroundings. Surroundings get colder. Exothermic (opposite — gives out energy, surroundings heat up)
Activation energy (Eₐ) The minimum energy colliding particles need in order to react. Every reaction needs it. Enthalpy change (Eₐ is the hump on the diagram; ΔH is the overall drop or rise)
Enthalpy change (ΔH) The overall energy change of a reaction. Negative = exothermic, positive = endothermic. Activation energy (activation energy is always positive; ΔH can be negative or positive)
Bond energy Energy needed to break one mole of a bond (or released when the same bond forms). In kJ/mol. —
Fuel cell A device that converts chemical energy directly into electrical energy. The H₂-O₂ fuel cell produces only water. Electrolysis (electrolysis puts electricity IN to force a reaction; a fuel cell gets electricity OUT from a reaction)
Catalyst Lowers the activation energy — gives an alternative pathway. Unchanged at the end. A catalyst does NOT change ΔH or the total amount of product — only makes it faster

Exothermic vs Endothermic — Side by Side

Exothermic Endothermic
Energy direction Given OUT to surroundings Taken IN from surroundings
Surroundings temp Goes UP (feels hotter) Goes DOWN (feels colder)
ΔH sign Negative (−) Positive (+)
Energy diagram Products LOWER than reactants Products HIGHER than reactants
Bond energy maths Energy out > energy in Energy in > energy out
Examples Combustion, neutralisation, Na + water Thermal decomposition, sherbet, photosynthesis
Exam Trap — Temperature Trick
Students think "endothermic = getting hotter." Wrong. Endothermic takes energy away from the surroundings, so the surroundings cool down. Endo = IN (energy goes into the reaction). Exo = OUT (energy exits the reaction).

Exam Trap — Formula Order
ΔH = energy in (break) − energy out (make). NOT the other way around. If you flip it, your sign is wrong and exothermic becomes endothermic.

Say It in One Sentence

Exothermic The reaction gives out heat and the surroundings warm up — think combustion, neutralisation.
Endothermic The reaction absorbs heat and the surroundings cool down — think thermal decomposition, sherbet.
Activation energy The minimum energy particles need to collide successfully — the "hump" on a reaction pathway diagram.
ΔH formula Energy in (bonds broken) minus energy out (bonds made) — negative means exothermic.
Bond energy calc Draw all bonds, add up energy in and energy out separately, then subtract — three steps, every time.
Fuel cell Hydrogen + oxygen make water and electricity — clean but hydrogen storage and supply are the problems.

Memory Tricks

Exo = Exit
Exothermic = energy exits the reaction (given out). Endothermic = energy enters the reaction (taken in). If you remember "exit," you'll never mix them up.
Diagram Direction
Exo = products go down (energy dropped out). Endo = products go up (energy was absorbed). The line always humps UP first for activation energy — then goes down (exo) or stays up (endo).
Three-Step Bond Calculation
1. Draw every bond. 2. Add up IN and OUT separately. 3. Subtract: ΔH = IN − OUT. Negative = exothermic. Positive = endothermic. Same method every time. Show all three steps for full marks.
Fuel Cell = "Clean But Not Easy"
Only product is water (clean!). But: hydrogen must be stored at high pressure (hard), filling stations are rare (inconvenient), and most hydrogen currently comes from fossil fuels (not truly green yet).

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"The beaker gets warmer / temperature rises" Exothermic — the reaction gives out energy to the surroundings
"The temperature drops / surroundings get colder" Endothermic — the reaction takes in energy from the surroundings
"Is bond breaking exothermic or endothermic?" Bond breaking is always endothermic (takes in energy). Bond making is always exothermic.
"Calculate the enthalpy change" ΔH = energy in (break) − energy out (make). Show every bond, every multiplication, every total.
"Draw a reaction pathway diagram for an exothermic reaction" Reactants HIGH, products LOW. Hump above reactants = Eₐ. Arrow down = ΔH (negative). Label axes: "Energy" and "Progress of reaction."
"Draw a reaction pathway diagram for an endothermic reaction" Reactants LOW, products HIGH. Hump above both = Eₐ. Arrow up = ΔH (positive). Label axes.
"Why is the temperature constant during a change of state?" Energy is used to overcome forces between particles, not to increase kinetic energy
"Give one advantage of hydrogen fuel cells over petrol" The only product is water, which is harmless, whereas petrol produces CO₂ which causes climate change
"Why isn't hydrogen widely used as a fuel?" Hydrogen must be stored under high pressure, filling stations are rare, and most hydrogen is made from fossil fuels
"What is the difference between a fuel cell and electrolysis?" A fuel cell produces electricity from a chemical reaction. Electrolysis uses electricity to force a reaction. They are opposites.
