Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
What does "rate of reaction" measure? State how you can find the rate of a reaction.
2 marks
Answer
Rate of reaction measures how fast a reaction takes place / how fast reactants are used up or products are formed [1]. You find it by measuring the amount of a reactant used up per unit of time, or the amount of a product formed per unit of time [1].
The key phrase is "per unit of time." Don't just say "how much product is made" — you must include the time element.

2
State four factors that can increase the rate of a chemical reaction.
2 marks
Answer
Any four of: (1) increase the concentration of a reactant [½], (2) increase the temperature [½], (3) increase the surface area of a solid reactant [½], (4) increase the pressure of a gaseous reactant [½], (5) use a catalyst [½]. [4 correct = 2 marks, 2-3 correct = 1 mark]
Giving all five is a safe bet. Remember: pressure only applies to gas reactions.

3
Define catalyst. Define enzyme.
2 marks
Answer
A catalyst is a substance that increases the rate of a reaction and is unchanged at the end of the reaction [1].
An enzyme is a biological catalyst / a protein made by living cells that acts as a catalyst [1].
"Unchanged at the end" is essential for the catalyst definition. Don't forget it. For enzyme, you must link it to "biological" or "made by cells."

4
Some magnesium and an excess of dilute hydrochloric acid are reacted together. 40 cm³ of hydrogen is produced in 5 minutes. Calculate the average rate of the reaction.
2 marks
Answer
Average rate = total volume of product ÷ total time [1]
= 40 cm³ ÷ 5 minutes = 8 cm³ per minute [1].
Show the formula first, then substitute the numbers. Always include the unit (cm³ per minute) — you'll lose a mark without it.

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY using collision theory, not just name things.
5
A student monitors a reaction that produces a gas. The graph below shows the volume of gas collected over time. The curve is steep at first, then becomes less steep, and finally goes flat. Explain what happens to the rate during the reaction and why the curve eventually goes flat.
3 marks
Answer
The rate is fastest at the start (steep curve) because the concentration of reactants is highest [1]. As the reaction proceeds, reactants are used up so their concentration decreases, and the rate slows down (curve becomes less steep) [1]. The curve goes flat when the reaction is complete / all of the limiting reactant has been used up, so no more product is formed [1].
Three marks, three ideas: (1) fast at start because high concentration, (2) slows because reactants used up, (3) flat because reaction is over. Use the word "concentration" — don't just say "less stuff left."

6
Marble chips (calcium carbonate) react with dilute hydrochloric acid. In experiment 1, large chips are used. In experiment 2, the same mass of marble is crushed into a powder. Explain why the reaction in experiment 2 is faster, even though the same total amount of carbon dioxide is produced.
3 marks
Answer
Crushing the marble into a powder increases its surface area [1]. More particles of calcium carbonate are exposed to the acid, so there are more frequent collisions between acid particles and the marble [1]. The same total amount of CO₂ is produced because the same mass of marble was used in both experiments — it all reacts eventually [1].
Key: say "surface area," say "more particles exposed," and explain the same total product by referencing the same mass. Many students forget the last point.

7
Hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) decomposes very slowly into water and oxygen. When manganese(IV) oxide is added, the reaction becomes much faster. (a) What role does manganese(IV) oxide play? (b) What would you observe if you tested the gas produced with a glowing splint? (c) What would you find if you weighed the manganese(IV) oxide before and after the reaction?
3 marks
Answer
(a) Manganese(IV) oxide acts as a catalyst — it speeds up the reaction without being used up [1].
(b) The glowing splint would relight, because the gas produced is oxygen [1].
(c) The mass would be the same as before, because a catalyst is unchanged at the end of the reaction [1].
Part (c) is the classic trick question. Students who know the definition of a catalyst get it instantly. If you say "less mass" you've confused catalyst with reactant.

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning. This is where exam grades are won or lost.
8
Using collision theory, explain why increasing the temperature increases the rate of a reaction. You should give two separate reasons.
5 marks
Answer
For a reaction to occur, particles must collide with enough energy (the activation energy) [1].

Reason 1: At a higher temperature, the particles have more kinetic energy and move faster [1]. They collide more frequently (more collisions per unit of time) [1].

Reason 2: Because the particles have more energy, a greater proportion of the collisions have energy equal to or greater than the activation energy [1]. So more collisions are successful, and the rate increases [1].
This is the most important 5-mark question in the chapter. TWO reasons: (1) more frequent collisions, (2) more collisions exceed the activation energy. If you only give one reason, you can't get more than 3 marks. Practice writing both until it's automatic.

9
Ethene reacts with steam at high pressure to form ethanol:
C₂H₄(g) + H₂O(g) → C₂H₅OH(l)
Using collision theory, explain why increasing the pressure leads to a higher rate for this reaction. Then explain why increasing the pressure would have no effect on the rate of reaction between two solids.
5 marks
Answer
Why pressure increases rate for gases:
Increasing the pressure forces the gas molecules into a smaller volume [1]. This increases the concentration of the gas (more molecules per unit volume) [1]. The molecules are closer together, so they collide more frequently [1]. More frequent collisions means more successful collisions per unit of time, so the rate increases [1].

Why pressure has no effect on solids:
In a reaction between two solids, the particles are already in fixed positions and cannot be pushed closer together by increasing pressure. Pressure only affects the spacing of gas particles [1].
The chain for gases: more pressure → smaller volume → higher concentration → more frequent collisions → faster rate. For solids: particles are already tightly packed and can't be compressed. The exam often asks both sides of this — be ready.

10
A catalyst speeds up a reaction. (a) In terms of activation energy, explain how a catalyst increases the rate of a reaction. (b) Sketch a reaction pathway diagram showing the energy profile with and without a catalyst. Label: reactants, products, activation energy without catalyst, activation energy with catalyst. (c) Explain why catalysts are important in industry.
6 marks
Answer
(a) A catalyst provides an alternative reaction pathway with a lower activation energy [1]. Because the activation energy is lower, more particles now have enough energy for a successful collision [1], so the frequency of successful collisions increases and the rate increases.

(b) Diagram should show [1 for correct shape, 1 for labels]:
• x-axis: progress of reaction, y-axis: energy
• Reactants at one energy level on the left, products at a different level on the right
• A large "hump" above the reactant level labelled "Eₐ without catalyst"
• A smaller "hump" on the same diagram labelled "Eₐ with catalyst"

(c) Catalysts are important in industry because they speed up reactions, saving time [1]. They also allow reactions to occur at lower temperatures, which reduces energy/fuel costs [1]. Since catalysts are not used up, they can be reused, saving money.
This question combines three skills: the activation energy explanation, the diagram, and the industrial importance. Practice each separately, then put them together. The diagram alone is worth 2 marks and takes 30 seconds to draw — don't skip it.
