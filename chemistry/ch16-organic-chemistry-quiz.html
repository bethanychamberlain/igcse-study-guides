Foundation — Can You Name It?

Worth 1-2 marks each on the exam. Get these right and you're building a solid base.
1
Define the term hydrocarbon.
1 mark
Answer
A hydrocarbon is a compound that contains only carbon and hydrogen [1].
Both elements must be named AND the word "only" is essential. Without "only" you could be describing an alcohol or acid.

3
Give the molecular formula for: (a) propane, (b) ethene, (c) ethanol.
3 marks
Answer
(a) C₃H₈ [1] — propane: 3 carbons, alkane so 2(3)+2 = 8 hydrogens.
(b) C₂H₄ [1] — ethene: 2 carbons, alkene so 2(2) = 4 hydrogens.
(c) C₂H₅OH [1] — ethanol: 2 carbons, alcohol (show the OH separately).
Use the general formulae: alkanes CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, alkenes CₙH₂ₙ, alcohols CₙH₂ₙ₊₁OH. Plug in n = number of carbons.

What is the test for an unsaturated compound? State the reagent used and the result you would see.
2 marks
Answer
Add bromine water [1]. If the compound is unsaturated, the bromine water changes from orange to colourless (it is decolourised) [1].
You must say both the reagent AND the colour change. "Turns clear" is not enough — say "decolourises" or "changes from orange to colourless."

Intermediate — Can You Explain It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to explain WHY, not just name things.
5
Petroleum is separated into fractions by fractional distillation. Describe how this process works and explain why different fractions are collected at different heights in the tower.
4 marks
Answer
Petroleum is heated in a furnace until it vaporises [1]. The vapour enters a tall fractionating tower which is hot at the bottom and cool at the top [1]. As the vapour rises, it cools, and fractions condense at different heights depending on their boiling points [1]. Fractions with shorter chains have lower boiling points, so they rise higher before condensing and are collected near the top [1].
Four marks, four ideas: (1) petroleum is heated/vaporised, (2) tower has a temperature gradient, (3) fractions condense at different heights, (4) shorter chains = lower boiling point = higher up. Chain each idea logically.

6
Define cracking. Give two reasons why cracking is important in the petroleum industry.
3 marks
Answer
Cracking is the breaking down of large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller, more useful molecules [1].

Reason 1: It helps match supply to demand — for example, converting excess long-chain fractions (like naphtha) into shorter-chain, more useful fractions (like petrol) [1].
Reason 2: It produces alkenes, which are reactive and used as starting materials for making plastics, alcohols, and other useful chemicals [1].
The definition must include "large molecules broken into smaller ones." For the reasons, one must be about supply/demand and the other about producing alkenes.

7
Ethanol can be made by two different methods. Name both methods and give one advantage of each.
4 marks
Answer
Method 1: Reaction of ethene with steam (catalytic addition) [1].
Advantage: The reaction is fast and continuous, and produces pure ethanol [1].

Method 2: Fermentation of glucose using yeast [1].
Advantage: It uses renewable resources (plant sugars like sugarcane or maize) and takes place at room temperature, using less energy [1].
You must name the method AND give a distinct advantage for each. "Cheap" alone is not specific enough — say WHY it is cheap (renewable resource, low temperature, etc.).

Advanced — Can You Discuss It?

Worth 5-6 marks each. These need full sentences, multiple points, and clear reasoning. This is where exam grades are won or lost.
8
Ethanoic acid is a member of a homologous series. (a) Define homologous series. (b) Give three characteristics of a homologous series. (c) Ethanoic acid reacts with sodium carbonate. Write a balanced equation and state what you would observe.
6 marks
Answer
(a) A homologous series is a family of similar compounds that have the same functional group and so have similar chemical properties [1].

(b) Three characteristics:
• All members fit the same general formula [1]
• Each member differs from the next by a CH₂ unit [1]
• Members show a gradual trend in physical properties (e.g. boiling points increase with chain length) [1]

(c) 2CH₃COOH + Na₂CO₃ → 2CH₃COONa + H₂O + CO₂ [1]
Observation: fizzing/effervescence (bubbles of carbon dioxide gas are produced) [1].
For the equation, make sure it is balanced. For the observation, "fizzing" or "bubbles" is the key word. You could also say the solid dissolves.

9
Ethene is described as an unsaturated hydrocarbon. (a) Explain what this means. (b) Describe two different addition reactions of ethene, naming the reagent and product in each case. (c) State the type of reaction and explain why alkanes cannot undergo this type of reaction.
6 marks
Answer
(a) Unsaturated means it contains a carbon-carbon double bond (C=C) [1]. Hydrocarbon means it contains only carbon and hydrogen.

(b) Reaction 1: Ethene + hydrogen (with nickel catalyst, heat/pressure) → ethane [1].
Reaction 2: Ethene + steam (with acid catalyst, heat/pressure) → ethanol [1].

(c) These are addition reactions [1]. In an addition reaction, the C=C double bond breaks open and atoms add across it, giving only one product [1]. Alkanes cannot undergo addition reactions because they have only single bonds (they are saturated) — there is no double bond to open up [1].
For each addition reaction, you need: reagent + product + conditions. For part (c), you must explain the link between double bonds and addition. Don't just say "alkanes are unreactive" — explain WHY.

10
Describe the journey from petroleum to a plastic bottle. In your answer, mention: fractional distillation, cracking, the monomer, and addition polymerisation. Name the plastic produced.
5 marks
Answer
Petroleum is separated into fractions by fractional distillation [1]. The naphtha fraction is then broken into smaller molecules by cracking, which produces the alkene ethene [1]. Ethene molecules are the monomers (small repeating units) [1]. Thousands of ethene molecules join together in a reaction called addition polymerisation — the C=C double bonds break and the molecules add on to each other to form a long chain [1]. The plastic produced is called poly(ethene), commonly known as polythene [1].
This question tests whether you can link the whole chapter together. The chain is: petroleum → fractional distillation → naphtha → cracking → ethene → addition polymerisation → poly(ethene). Each step is a mark.
