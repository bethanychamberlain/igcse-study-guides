Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Mole (mol) The chemist's counting unit. 1 mol = 6.02 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro constant). Grams (moles and grams are DIFFERENT — use Mᵣ to convert)
Molar mass The mass of 1 mole of a substance in grams = its Aᵣ or Mᵣ in grams. —
Molar gas volume Volume of 1 mol of any gas at rtp = 24 dm³ = 24,000 cm³. —
Concentration Amount of solute per unit volume of solution, usually mol/dm³ or g/dm³. —
Empirical formula The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms in a compound. E.g. CH₃. Molecular formula (the actual number of atoms — could be C₂H₆)
Molecular formula The actual number of each atom in one molecule. A whole-number multiple of the empirical formula. Empirical formula (the simplest ratio)
% yield (Actual mass ÷ theoretical mass) × 100. How much product you really got vs what you expected. % purity (that's pure substance vs impure sample)
% purity (Mass of pure substance ÷ mass of impure sample) × 100. % yield (that's actual vs theoretical)

All the Key Formulas — One Place

moles = mass (g) ÷ Mᵣ
mass (g) = moles × Mᵣ
volume of gas (dm³) = moles × 24
moles of gas = volume (dm³) ÷ 24
concentration (mol/dm³) = moles ÷ volume (dm³)
moles = concentration × volume (dm³)
% yield = (actual mass ÷ theoretical mass) × 100
% purity = (mass of pure ÷ mass of impure sample) × 100
n = Mᵣ (given) ÷ empirical formula mass
molecular formula = empirical formula × n
Unit Trap — cm³ vs dm³
1 dm³ = 1000 cm³. All the formulas above use dm³. If the question gives you cm³, divide by 1000 first! This is the #1 error in moles calculations. Write the conversion as a visible step every time.

Say It in One Sentence

The mole 1 mol = 6.02 × 10²³ particles — the bridge between atoms (tiny) and grams (real world).
Molar mass 1 mol of any substance weighs exactly its Aᵣ or Mᵣ in grams.
Equation coefficients The big numbers in a balanced equation tell you the mole ratio, NOT the gram ratio.
Avogadro's Law 1 mol of ANY gas = 24 dm³ at room temperature and pressure. Doesn't matter what gas.
Concentration How many moles of solute are dissolved in each dm³ of solution.
Empirical formula Divide each element's mass by its Aᵣ, then divide all by the smallest — that gives the ratio.
Molecular formula Empirical formula multiplied by n, where n = Mᵣ ÷ empirical formula mass.

Memory Tricks

The Triangle (Mass / Moles / Mᵣ)
Draw a triangle: mass on top, moles × Mᵣ on the bottom. Cover what you want to find — what's left tells you what to do. Want moles? mass ÷ Mᵣ. Want mass? moles × Mᵣ.
Coefficients = Moles, NOT Grams
In 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O, the "2" means 2 moles of hydrogen, not 2 grams. You must multiply by Mᵣ to convert to grams. This is where most students lose marks.
4-Step Method for Mass Calculations
1. Write the balanced equation. 2. Write Mᵣ for each substance you need. 3. Convert moles in the equation to grams. 4. Scale up or down to match the actual mass in the question. Show ALL steps.
Empirical Formula — 5-Step Table
1. Write masses (or assume 100 g from %). 2. Divide by Aᵣ = moles. 3. Divide all by the smallest. 4. If not whole numbers, multiply (e.g. 1:1.5 → 2:3). 5. Write the formula. Use a table — it prevents errors every time.
Atoms vs Molecules — Check the Question
"0.5 mol of bromine molecules (Br₂)" is different from "0.5 mol of bromine atoms (Br)." The molecule mass is double the atom mass. Always check which one the question asks for.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"How many moles in 44 g of CO₂?" Mᵣ of CO₂ = 44. Moles = 44 ÷ 44 = 1 mol
"What mass is 0.5 mol of H₂O?" Mᵣ of H₂O = 18. Mass = 0.5 × 18 = 9 g
"What volume does 2 mol of gas occupy at rtp?" Volume = 2 × 24 = 48 dm³
"6000 cm³ of gas — how many moles?" Convert first: 6000 ÷ 1000 = 6 dm³. Moles = 6 ÷ 24 = 0.25 mol
"25 cm³ of solution contains 0.05 mol. What is the concentration?" Convert: 25 ÷ 1000 = 0.025 dm³. Concentration = 0.05 ÷ 0.025 = 2 mol/dm³
"Find the empirical formula from 80% C and 20% H" Assume 100 g: 80 g C, 20 g H. Moles: C = 80/12 = 6.67, H = 20/1 = 20. Ratio: 6.67/6.67 = 1, 20/6.67 = 3. Formula = CH₃
"Empirical formula CH₃, Mᵣ = 30. Find molecular formula." Empirical mass = 12 + 3 = 15. n = 30 ÷ 15 = 2. Molecular formula = C₂H₆
"Theoretical mass 130.5 g, actual mass 121.2 g. % yield?" (121.2 ÷ 130.5) × 100 = 92.9%. If answer > 100%, you made an error
"10 g impure sample, 9.5 g is pure. % purity?" (9.5 ÷ 10) × 100 = 95%
