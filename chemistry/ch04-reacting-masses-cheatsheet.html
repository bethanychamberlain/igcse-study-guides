Key Vocabulary

Word What It Means Don't Confuse With
Valency The number of electrons an atom loses, gains, or shares when it forms a compound. Linked to group number. Charge (charge is for ions; valency is used to build formulae)
Reactants Substances on the left side of the arrow — what you start with. Products (right side — what you end up with)
Products Substances on the right side of the arrow — what is formed. Reactants (left side)
Aᵣ Relative atomic mass — average mass of an element's atoms compared to 1/12 of C-12. Mᵣ (that's for whole molecules/compounds, not single atoms)
Mᵣ Relative molecular / formula mass — add up all Aᵣ values of every atom in the formula. Aᵣ (that's just one element)
Conservation of mass Total mass of reactants = total mass of products. No atoms appear or disappear. —
% composition What percentage of a compound's mass comes from a particular element. % purity (that's about impurities, not elements)
% purity (Mass of pure substance ÷ total mass of sample) × 100. % composition (that's about elements in a formula)

State Symbols — Know All Four

Symbol Meaning Example
(s) Solid Mg(s), CaCO₃(s)
(l) Liquid H₂O(l)
(g) Gas O₂(g), CO₂(g)
(aq) Aqueous (dissolved in water) NaCl(aq), HCl(aq)

Valency by Group — The Pattern

Group I II III IV V VI VII
Valency 1 2 3 4 3 2 1
The Pattern
Valency goes up 1, 2, 3, 4 then comes back down 3, 2, 1. It mirrors! Group IV is the peak at 4.

Say It in One Sentence

Naming rule Metal first, then non-metal with ending changed to -ide (e.g. sodium chloride, magnesium oxide).
Criss-cross method Swap the valencies: each element's valency becomes the subscript of the other element.
Balancing equations Same number of each atom on both sides — only add big numbers in front, NEVER change the subscripts.
Conservation of mass Nothing is created or destroyed — atoms just rearrange, so total mass stays the same.
Mᵣ calculation Add up all the Aᵣ values of every atom in the formula — multiply by subscripts first!
% composition (Mass of element in formula ÷ total Mᵣ) × 100.
Brackets in formulae The subscript outside the bracket multiplies EVERYTHING inside: Mg(NO₃)₂ = 1 Mg, 2 N, 6 O.

Memory Tricks

Balancing Order
Balance metals first, then non-metals, leave oxygen and hydrogen for last. They're usually in multiple compounds so they're trickier. Check every element at the end.
Never Change the Small Numbers
When balancing, you can only put big numbers in front (like 2MgO). Changing the subscript (like MgO₂) creates a completely different substance. Big numbers = how many. Small numbers = the formula itself.
Mᵣ With Brackets — Expand First
For Ca(OH)₂: expand the bracket first → Ca + 2O + 2H. Then add: 40 + (2×16) + (2×1) = 40 + 32 + 2 = 74. Always expand brackets before you start adding.
Formulae You Must Just Know
Water = H₂O. Ammonia = NH₃. Methane = CH₄. You can't work these out from the name — memorise them. The exam assumes you know them.
Aᵣ Values to Memorise
H=1, C=12, N=14, O=16, Na=23, Mg=24, Al=27, S=32, Cl=35.5, Ca=40, Fe=56, Cu=64, Zn=65. The exam usually gives them, but knowing them saves time.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
"Write the formula for aluminium oxide" Al = valency 3, O = valency 2. Criss-cross: Al₂O₃
"Balance this equation: Mg + O₂ → MgO" 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO. Check: 2 Mg each side, 2 O each side
"What are the state symbols?" (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) dissolved in water
"Calculate the Mᵣ of Ca(OH)₂" 40 + (2 × 16) + (2 × 1) = 40 + 32 + 2 = 74
"Calculate the Mᵣ of Mg(NO₃)₂" 24 + (2 × 14) + (6 × 16) = 24 + 28 + 96 = 148. Don't forget: 2 × 3 = 6 oxygen atoms
"Find the percentage of oxygen in SO₂" Mᵣ = 32 + 32 = 64. Mass of O = 32. % = (32 ÷ 64) × 100 = 50%
"Why doesn't total mass change in a reaction?" Conservation of mass: atoms are rearranged but no atoms are created or destroyed, so total mass stays the same
"What is the percentage purity?" (Mass of pure substance ÷ total mass of impure sample) × 100
"A reacting mass question gives you X grams..." Write the balanced equation → find Mᵣ of each substance → scale up/down by the ratio to match the given mass. Show every step.
