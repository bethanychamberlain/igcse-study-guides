Exercise 2: Flat to Vivid 5 min
Rewrite each flat sentence using specific, vivid details.
"The food at the market was good."
The market was packed like sardines as the spicy food was tingling my mouth as if there were little people dancing on my tongue.
"The building was really big and impressive."
The colossal building was standing over me like a giant giraffe
"It was hot and we walked around the city all day."
It felt like lava was being poured onto us while we were walkng through the city

Tutor Notes

  • He's using similes naturally! All three rewrites contain similes ("packed like sardines," "like a giant giraffe," "like lava"). This is a real strength.
  • Sentence 1 is the strongest — "little people dancing on my tongue" is creative and vivid. Two similes in one sentence, plus taste sense.
  • Sentence 2 is weaker — "giant giraffe" is an unusual comparison for a building. "Standing over me" is good (personification), but could be more specific about the building itself.
  • Sentence 3 is good but generic — "lava being poured onto us" captures the heat well but doesn't add any city-specific detail.
  • Missing: No full stops at the end of sentences 2 and 3. Spelling: "walkng" → walking.
  • Overall pattern: He reaches for figurative language instinctively — this is a strong foundation. The next step is combining similes with specific sensory details (what he actually saw/heard/felt).

~170 words (target: 80–120) 3+ senses used 4 similes

Tutor Notes

  • SIMILES everywhere — "like a lion hunting for food," "like there country just won the World Cup," "like we were in an interrogation room," "like a fan on full blast," "felt like a dream." He's using figurative language confidently. This is his strongest writing skill.
  • 3 senses present — Sound (announcer screaming, crowd chanting), Touch (cold air), Sight (bright orange sun, green pitch, stadium lights). Target met.
  • Overlong — 170 words vs 80–120 target. Wanders from Toulouse football match to Barcelona beach. The task asked for ONE moment, not a trip summary.
  • Run-on sentences — The whole paragraph is essentially 3–4 sentences connected by commas and "and." Same pattern as the Easter test Q11.
  • Scope creep — Starts with the football match (good!) then drifts to Barcelona beach and a general trip reflection. Needs to stay focused on the one moment.
  • Spelling issues: exiting → exciting, toulouse → Toulouse, crown → crowd, there → their, staduim → stadium, interigation → interrogation, infront → in front, looks → look, didnt → didn't, beggining → beginning.
  • "Me and some friends" → "Some friends and I" (grammar).
