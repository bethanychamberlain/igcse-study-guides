Q11 15 marks
Write a narrative beginning: "The morning started like any other, but nothing about this day would be ordinary."
The morning started like any other, but nothing about this day would be ordinary. I woke up to the beautiful orange sun beaming through my curtains signaling for me to get out of bed. I put my hoodie on and headed to the kitchen to make a bowl of cereal, which I quietly brought to my bedroom. As I'm eating my breakfast and watching TV I hear my mom get up and go to the kitchen. As I finish my food my mom says "Today we are going to airport to pick up your brother." I quickly reply while getting ready. I meet my mom in the car and we started playing our favorite songs on the way to the airport. When we arrive I see my younger brother with his suitcase and backpack, and he sees me and runs towards me shouting "JOJO!" In excitement, my mom comes over to us and hugs him tight. After we get in the car we all get an Amber Alert on our phones saying "If you are in Brevard County or in a 30 mile radius from Brevard County, evacuate NOW, this is not a drill. Foreign military aircrafts and missiles have been located towards Brevard County. Please evacuate. Now this is not a drill." We all look at each other in panic asking each other if this is a prank from hackers as we slowly speed up on the highway. Then to our right we see a big explosion in the forest as flames erupt in the sky. We all scream and my mom speeds up to the max as our radio randomly starts playing the president telling us the U.S. was attacked and we're in full out war. We all panic hoping we can make it out the area alive. I open my phone to text family but I see there is no service any more, and as I drop my phone due to another explosion behind us now. Eventually we make it out the county but we still keep driving until we started running low on fuel. We end up at a military base finding all aircrafts and ground vehicles are being deployed as well as anti-air missiles. A high rank soldier comes up to us telling us to get inside for protection. A few hours later in the bunker we hear an extremely loud alarm system go off as a speaker tells us "If you are hearing this message, Nuclear Warfare has begun. Pray for forgiveness now and kiss your loved ones and hug them tight — everything went black as two nuclear bombs killed everyone in a 200 mile radius, including us.
8/15 Content/Structure: 6/10 | Style/Accuracy: 2/5
Strengths: Matches prompt perfectly (ordinary → extraordinary). Personal and specific (Brevard County, "JoJo," airport). Clear narrative arc with genuine emotional resonance and ambitious storytelling. ~350–400 words.
Weaknesses: Almost entirely "telling" (action/plot) rather than "showing" (sensory detail). Run-on sentences throughout. Limited vocabulary variety. Escalation from Amber Alert to nuclear war too compressed. Ending abrupt (no reflection/resolution). Dialogue punctuation inconsistent. Spelling: "layed" → "laid."
