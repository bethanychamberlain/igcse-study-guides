How to Decode Any Word on the Exam
  1. Find the root — the core meaning in the middle of the word
  2. Check the prefix — what comes before changes the direction (un-, anti-, de-)
  3. Check the suffix — what comes after tells you the category (-ase = enzyme, -tion = process)
  4. Combine them — photo (light) + synthesis (putting together) = putting together using light

The Endo/Exo Pair — Know This Cold
ENDO = energy goes IN (endo sounds like "in-do"). Feels cold. EXO = energy comes OUT (exo sounds like "exit"). Feels hot. Works for: endothermic/exothermic (chemistry), endocrine (biology, secretes internally), ectothermic (gets heat from outside).
The Hyper/Hypo Pair
HYPER = too much / above (think: hyperactive kid bouncing off walls). HYPO = too little / below (think: hypothermia = too cold = below normal temp). If the solution is hypertonic, there's MORE solute outside the cell.

The Chemistry Naming Rule
-ide = just two elements (sodium chloride = Na + Cl). -ate = contains oxygen too (sodium sulfate = Na + S + O). If you see -ate on the exam, there's oxygen hiding in there.
The Biology Enzyme Rule
If a word ends in -ase, it's ALWAYS an enzyme. The first part tells you what it breaks down: lipase breaks lipids, protease breaks proteins, amylase breaks amylose (starch). Free marks on the exam!
The Sugar Rule
If a word ends in -ose, it's a sugar. Monosaccharide = one sugar (glucose). Disaccharide = two sugars joined (sucrose). Polysaccharide = many sugars chained (starch, glycogen).

False Friends — Words That Look the Same but Aren't!

How to Drill These

Method 1 — Cover and Guess
Cover the "Means" column with your hand. Read the prefix/suffix/root and say the meaning out loud. Check. Move on. Come back to the ones you missed.
Method 2 — Reverse Drill
Cover the "Prefix" column. Read the meaning and try to say the word part. This is harder but builds the connection both ways.
Method 3 — Random Word Decode
Open any science textbook page. Point at a random bold word. Break it into parts using these tables. If you can decode 3 in a row without looking, you've got it.
Method 4 — Teach It
Explain one word root to someone else (your aunt, uncle, a friend). If you can teach it, you know it. This is the strongest way to memorise anything.
