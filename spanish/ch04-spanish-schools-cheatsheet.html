Giving Opinions — The Exam Formula

Opinion + porque + Reason
Me gusta la biología porque es interesante
No me gustan las matemáticas porque son difíciles
Me encanta la música porque el profesor es divertido
Odio la física porque es muy difícil
Prefiero el inglés porque es útil
Gusta vs Gustan
Me gusta + singular: “Me gusta la historia.”
Me gustan + plural: “Me gustan las ciencias.”
The verb agrees with the THING you like, not with you. “Me gusta las matemáticas” is WRONG.

Telling Time — Key Patterns

Time Spanish Note
1:00 a la una Singular “la” — ONLY for one o’clock
2:00 - 12:00 a las dos / tres / ocho... Plural “las” — for every other hour
:15 a las ocho y cuarto Quarter past
:30 a las ocho y media Half past
:45 a las nueve menos cuarto Quarter TO the next hour
The One O’Clock Rule
“A la una” = singular (one hour). Everything else = “A las dos/tres/cuatro...” = plural (multiple hours). This is the only exception.

The Imperfect Tense — “Used To” / “Was”

Person -AR (hablar) -ER / -IR (comer)
yo habl-aba com-ía
tú habl-abas com-ías
él / ella habl-aba com-ía
nosotros habl-ábamos com-íamos
vosotros habl-abais com-íais
ellos / ellas habl-aban com-ían

Three Irregular Imperfects — Only These Three

Person SER (was) IR (used to go) VER (used to see)
yo era iba veía
tú eras ibas veías
él / ella era iba veía
nosotros éramos íbamos veíamos
vosotros erais ibais veíais
ellos / ellas eran iban veían
Good News
Only three verbs are irregular in the imperfect. Every other verb in Spanish follows the regular -aba/-ía pattern. Compare that to the preterite which has loads of irregulars. The imperfect is actually the easier past tense to learn.

Imperfect vs Preterite — Which Past Tense?

Imperfect Preterite
Means “used to” / “was doing” “did” (once, completed)
Duration Ongoing, repeated, no clear end One-time, finished action
Example “Estudiaba cada día”
(I used to study every day)		 “Estudié ayer”
(I studied yesterday)
Think “Was this a habit or background?” “Did this happen once and end?”
Quick Decision Test
Repeated / habitual? → Imperfect (-aba / -ía).
Happened once and finished? → Preterite (-é / -ó).

Say It in One Sentence

Opinion formula Me gusta + subject + porque + reason = higher marks. Always add the reason.
Gusta vs gustan Singular thing = gusta. Plural thing = gustan. It agrees with the THING, not with you.
Imperfect -AR All -AR imperfects have -aba in them: hablaba, estudiaba, jugaba. Hear the “aba-aba” rhythm.
Imperfect -ER/-IR All -ER/-IR imperfects have -ía in them: comía, vivía, tenía. Same endings for both.
Two tenses = marks Present + imperfect in one paragraph shows tense range: “Mi colegio en Dinamarca era pequeño. Mi colegio en Florida es grande.”
Porque vs por qué One word, no accent = because. Two words, accent = why? “¿Por qué?” “Porque es fácil.”

Memory Tricks

The “ABA” Chant for -AR Imperfect
Chant it like a rhythm: -aba, -abas, -aba, -ábamos, -abais, -aban. Notice: yo and él/ella are IDENTICAL (both -aba). You only need to learn five endings, not six.
The “ÍA” Pattern for -ER/-IR Imperfect
Every form has -ía: comía, comías, comía, comíamos, comíais, comían. The “ía” never disappears. If it sounds like “ee-ah,” you are doing it right.
Only THREE Irregular Imperfects in ALL of Spanish
SER, IR, VER. That is it. Every other verb follows the regular pattern. Compare that to the preterite which has dozens of irregulars. The imperfect is the friendly past tense.
Compare Your Two Schools
You have a built-in essay: Danish school (imperfect) vs Florida school (present). “En Dinamarca, mi colegio era pequeño. Los profesores eran simpáticos. Estudiaba muchas asignaturas. Ahora, mi colegio es grande.” Two tenses, personal content, top marks.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
“¿Qué asignaturas estudias?” (What subjects do you study?) “Estudio inglés, matemáticas, biología y química.”
“¿Cuál es tu asignatura favorita?” (What is your favourite subject?) Opinion + reason: “Me encanta la biología porque es interesante.”
“Describe un día típico” (Describe a typical day) Use times: “Me levanto a las siete. Las clases empiezan a las ocho y media.”
“¿Cómo era tu colegio anterior?” (What was your old school like?) Imperfect: “Era pequeño. Los profesores eran simpáticos. Estudiaba muchas asignaturas.”
“¿Qué hacías...?” (What did you use to do?) Imperfect: “Jugaba al fútbol. Comía en el comedor. Estudiaba mucho.”
Blank needs “used to” Imperfect (-aba for -AR, -ía for -ER/-IR): “estudiaba, comía, vivía”
