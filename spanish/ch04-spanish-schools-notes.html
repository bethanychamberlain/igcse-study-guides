Section 2 Giving Opinions — ¿Qué opinas?
The IGCSE always asks for your opinion. Just naming subjects is foundation level. Adding opinions pushes you into higher marks.
W
Write This Down — Opinion Phrases
SpanishEnglish
Me gusta / Me gustanI like (singular / plural)
Me encanta / Me encantanI love
No me gusta / No me gustanI don’t like
OdioI hate
PrefieroI prefer
Creo que...I think that...
En mi opinión...In my opinion...

“Me gusta la biología porque es interesante.” — I like biology because it is interesting.
“No me gustan las matemáticas porque son difíciles.” — I don’t like maths because it is difficult.
“Prefiero la música porque el profesor es divertido.” — I prefer music because the teacher is fun.

!
Don't Confuse — Gusta vs Gustan

Me gusta + singular noun: “Me gusta la historia.”
Me gustan + plural noun: “Me gustan las ciencias.”

The verb agrees with the THING you like, not with you. “Me gusta las matemáticas” is WRONG because matemáticas is plural.

Why This Matters

The IGCSE mark scheme says: opinion + reason = higher marks. Saying “Me gusta la historia” is okay. Saying “Me gusta la historia porque es interesante y el profesor es divertido” is worth more. Always add porque.

  • I know 5+ opinion phrases in Spanish
  • I know the difference between gusta (singular) and gustan (plural)
  • I always add “porque” + reason after my opinion

Write This Down — School Day Routine

“Me levanto a las siete.” — I get up at seven.
“Las clases empiezan a las ocho y media.” — Classes start at half past eight.
“El recreo es a las once.” — Break is at eleven.
“Como a la una.” — I eat at one.
“Las clases terminan a las tres y media.” — Classes end at half past three.
“Hago los deberes a las cinco.” — I do homework at five.
“Me acuesto a las diez.” — I go to bed at ten.

!
Don't Confuse — “A la una” vs “A las dos”

One o’clock uses a la una (singular “la”). Every other hour uses a las (plural “las”): a las dos, a las tres, a las ocho.

  • I can tell the time in Spanish (full hours, half past, quarter past/to)
  • I can describe my school day in 5+ sentences with times
  • I know “a la una” (singular) vs “a las dos” (plural)
Section 4 The Spanish School System — El sistema educativo
The IGCSE reading exam often includes texts about Spanish schools. You need to know this vocabulary even if you don’t attend one.
W
Write This Down — Key Facts

ESO (Educación Secundaria Obligatoria) = compulsory secondary education, ages 12-16.
Bachillerato = two-year pre-university course (like sixth form / 11th-12th grade). Not compulsory.
Formación profesional = vocational training (alternative to Bachillerato).

“En España, la educación es obligatoria hasta los dieciséis años.”
— In Spain, education is compulsory until age sixteen.

Section 5 The Imperfect Tense — How Things Used to Be
The imperfect tense describes what things were like or what you used to do. On the Easter test you scored 0% on this — you didn’t attempt any imperfect forms.
W
Write This Down — When to Use the Imperfect

1. Describing what things were like: “El colegio era grande.” (The school was big.)
2. Saying what you used to do regularly: “Estudiaba mucho.” (I used to study a lot.)
3. Setting the scene: “Hacía sol y los alumnos jugaban en el patio.” (It was sunny and the students were playing in the yard.)

!
Don't Confuse — Imperfect vs Preterite

Imperfect = used to / was doing (ongoing, repeated, no clear end): “Estudiaba cada día” (I used to study every day).
Preterite = did (one-time, completed action): “Estudié ayer” (I studied yesterday).
If it happened regularly or describes a scene → imperfect. If it happened once and finished → preterite.

Study Tip — Compare Your Schools

Write 3 sentences about your Danish school using the imperfect: “En Dinamarca, mi colegio era pequeño. Estudiaba muchas asignaturas. Los profesores eran simpáticos.” Then write 3 about your Florida school using the present. Using two tenses in one paragraph = higher marks.

  • I know the -AR imperfect endings: -aba, -abas, -aba, -ábamos, -abais, -aban
  • I know the -ER/-IR imperfect endings: -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían
  • I know the three irregular imperfects: ser (era), ir (iba), ver (veía)
  • I can explain the difference between imperfect and preterite
  • I can write 3+ sentences using the imperfect tense
