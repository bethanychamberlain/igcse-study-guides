Foundation — Can You Recall It?

Worth 1-2 marks each. Vocabulary and basic structures.
1
Give the Spanish for: plane, train, suitcase, passport, return ticket.
2 marks
Answer
el avión, el tren, la maleta, el pasaporte, un billete de ida y vuelta [1 for 3+, 2 for all 5].

2
How do you say these weather expressions? (a) It’s sunny (b) It’s cold (c) It’s raining (d) It’s cloudy
2 marks
Answer
(a) Hace sol, (b) Hace frío, (c) Llueve, (d) Está nublado [½ each].
Notice three different constructions: hace (a,b), verb alone (c), está (d). Don’t use “hace” for all of them.
Rate:
3
Put these verbs into the preterite (yo form): visitar, comer, ir, hacer.
2 marks
Answer
visité, comí, fui (irregular), hice (irregular) [½ each].
The first two are regular (-é, -í). The last two are irregular and must be memorised.

4
What is the difference between muy and mucho? Give one example of each.
2 marks
Answer
Muy = very (before adjectives/adverbs): “muy bonito” [1].
Mucho = a lot of (before nouns/after verbs): “mucho sol” or “como mucho” [1].
“Mucho bonito” is always wrong. “Muy sol” is always wrong. Learn which goes where.

Intermediate — Can You Use It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. Sentences combining vocabulary, tenses, and travel context.
5
Translate into Spanish: “I would like a double room with shower for three nights. How much does it cost?”
3 marks
Answer
“Quisiera una habitación doble con ducha para tres noches. ¿Cuánto cuesta?”
[1] quisiera + habitación doble, [1] con ducha para tres noches, [1] ¿Cuánto cuesta?
“Quisiera” is the most polite and exam-appropriate way to ask. “Quiero” works too but is more blunt.
Rate:
6
Translate into Spanish: “Last summer I went to Spain by plane. It was very hot and sunny. I visited a cathedral and ate paella.”
4 marks
Answer
“El verano pasado fui a España en avión. Hacía mucho calor y sol. Visité una catedral y comí paella.”

[1] fui (irregular preterite of ir), [1] hacía (imperfect for weather), [1] visité, comí (regular preterites), [1] overall accuracy and vocabulary.
This uses two tenses: preterite for actions (fui, visité, comí) and imperfect for weather (hacía). Two tenses = higher marks.
Rate:
7
Choose SER or ESTAR for each blank and give the correct preterite/imperfect form:
(a) El hotel _______ (was) muy grande. (b) El hotel _______ (was) cerca de la playa. (c) Las vacaciones _______ (were) increíbles. (d) Yo _______ (was) contento.
3 marks
Answer
(a) era (SER — describing what it was like) [¾]
(b) estaba (ESTAR — location) [¾]
(c) fueron (SER — judging the experience, preterite) [¾]
(d) estaba (ESTAR — temporary feeling) [¾]
This is ser vs estar applied to past tenses. Description/identity = SER. Location/feeling = ESTAR. Same rules, just conjugated differently.

Advanced — Can You Write a Full Description?

Worth 5-6 marks each. Extended writing with multiple tenses, vocabulary, and opinions.
8
Write a paragraph (6-7 sentences) describing a real or imagined holiday. Include: where you went, how you travelled, where you stayed, what the weather was like, what you did, your opinion, and what you would like to do next time. Use at least three different tenses.
6 marks
Model Answer
“El verano pasado, fui a España con mi familia. Viajamos en avión desde Florida. Nos alojamos en un hotel grande que estaba cerca de la playa. Hacía mucho sol todos los días. Visité la catedral de Barcelona y comí tapas en un restaurante. Fue una experiencia increíble. Me gustaría volver a España el año que viene.”

[1] preterite (fui, viajamos, visité, comí), [1] imperfect (estaba, hacía), [1] conditional (gustaría), [1] travel vocabulary, [1] opinion (fue increíble), [1] accuracy and coherence.
Three tenses in one paragraph (preterite + imperfect + conditional) = top band. This exact format works for any “describe a holiday” question on the exam.

9
Translate into Spanish: “We went to a restaurant and my father ordered paella. The food was delicious but quite expensive. After eating, we walked to the beach. It was a very good day. I would like to go back because I loved Spain.”
5 marks
Answer
“Fuimos a un restaurante y mi padre pidió paella. La comida era deliciosa pero bastante cara. Después de comer, fuimos a la playa a pie. Fue un día muy bueno. Me gustaría volver porque me encantó España.”

[1] preterite forms (fuimos, pidió, fue, encantó), [1] imperfect (era deliciosa — description), [1] quantifier (bastante cara), [1] conditional (gustaría), [1] vocabulary and accuracy.
Notice “era deliciosa” (imperfect, describing what the food was like) vs “fue un día bueno” (preterite, judging the completed day). This distinction is what separates C from B grades.
Rate:
10
Write 4 sentences about a bad holiday experience. Include: where you went, what the weather was like (bad), a problem at the hotel or restaurant, and what you would do differently next time. Use preterite, imperfect, and conditional.
5 marks
Model Answer
“Fui a Inglaterra con mi familia. Hacía mal tiempo y llovía todos los días. El hotel era pequeño y la habitación no tenía ducha. La próxima vez, me gustaría ir a un país con sol, como España.”

[1] preterite (fui), [1] imperfect for weather (hacía, llovía) and description (era, tenía), [1] negative (no tenía), [1] conditional (gustaría), [1] overall accuracy.
Describing a bad holiday is actually easier to write because you get to use negatives (no tenía, no había) and contrast (but next time I would...). The examiner loves to see contrast.
