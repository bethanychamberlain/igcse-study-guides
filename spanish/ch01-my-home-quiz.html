Foundation — Can You Recall It?

Worth 1-2 marks each. Vocabulary and basic grammar recall.
1
Translate these room names into Spanish (include the article el or la): kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, garden.
2 marks
Answer
la cocina, el dormitorio, el baño (or el cuarto de baño), el jardín [1 for correct Spanish words, 1 for correct articles].
You must include el/la. Getting the Spanish word right but the article wrong loses you half the marks.

What does “hay” mean in English? Write one sentence in Spanish using hay to describe something in your bedroom.
2 marks
Answer
Hay = there is / there are [1]. Example sentence: “En mi dormitorio hay una cama.” (In my bedroom there is a bed) [1]. Any correct sentence using hay with appropriate vocabulary accepted.
Remember: hay works for both singular AND plural. “Hay tres sillas” is also correct.

Match each preposition to its English meaning: encima de, debajo de, al lado de, detrás de.
2 marks
Answer
encima de = on top of, debajo de = under/below, al lado de = next to, detrás de = behind [1 for any 2 correct, 2 for all 4 correct].
These are the four most common prepositions on the IGCSE. Learn them cold.

Intermediate — Can You Use It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. You need to write correct sentences, not just translate single words.
5
Translate into Spanish: “The lamp is on top of the table. The chair is next to the desk.”
3 marks
Answer
“La lámpara está encima de la mesa.” [1] “La silla está al lado del escritorio.” [1]. Third mark [1] for using del (de + el) correctly instead of “de el.”
Remember: de + el = del. Writing “de el escritorio” is a grammar error. Also notice both sentences use ESTAR because they describe location.

6
Fill in the correct form of the adjective: (a) La casa es muy _______ (bonito). (b) Los dormitorios son _______ (pequeño). (c) La cocina es _______ (moderno).
3 marks
Answer
(a) bonita [1] — feminine singular (la casa).
(b) pequeños [1] — masculine plural (los dormitorios).
(c) moderna [1] — feminine singular (la cocina).
The rule: -o → -a for feminine, add -s for plural. You got adjective agreement perfect on the Easter test, so this should be easy marks.

7
Explain the difference between “hay un libro en la mesa” and “el libro está en la mesa.” When would you use each one?
3 marks
Answer
“Hay un libro” = “there is a book” — introduces something for the first time / says it exists [1].
“El libro está” = “the book is” — describes the location of a specific, known book [1].
Use hay + un/una (indefinite) when mentioning something new. Use está + el/la (definite) when referring to something already known [1].
This distinction is tested on the IGCSE. The keyword is: hay = existence, está = location of a known thing.

Advanced — Can You Describe and Explain?

Worth 5-6 marks each. Extended writing using multiple grammar points together. This is where exam grades are won or lost.
8
Write 4-5 sentences in Spanish describing your bedroom. Include: what furniture is in it (hay), where at least two items are (prepositions + está), and at least two adjectives that agree correctly.
5 marks
Model Answer
“Mi dormitorio es pequeño pero bonito. Hay una cama grande y un escritorio moderno. La cama está al lado de la ventana. El escritorio está delante de la estantería. En la pared hay un espejo grande.”

[1] correct use of hay, [1] correct use of está + preposition, [1] adjective agreement (e.g. pequeño with dormitorio, grande with cama), [1] variety of vocabulary, [1] overall accuracy and coherence.
This is the kind of paragraph the exam wants. Notice it uses hay (existence), está (location), prepositions (al lado de, delante de), and adjectives that agree. Stack these together for maximum marks.

9
Conjugate the verb vivir (to live) in all 6 present tense forms. Then write two sentences: one saying where you live, and one saying what your family does at home using a different -AR or -ER verb.
5 marks
Answer
vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven [2 — 1 mark for 3+ correct, 2 marks for all 6].

Example sentences:
“Vivo en una casa grande en Palm Bay.” [1]
“Mi familia come en el comedor.” or “Mi madre cocina en la cocina.” [1]
[1] for overall accuracy (correct verb endings, agreement, sentence structure).
The yo form is always -o for all three verb types. For the IGCSE, you mostly need yo, él/ella, and nosotros. Prioritise those three.

10
Translate into Spanish: “I live in a big, modern house. There are four bedrooms and two bathrooms. My bedroom is small but comfortable. There is a bed next to the window and a bookshelf behind the door. There is no garden.”
6 marks
Answer
“Vivo en una casa grande y moderna. Hay cuatro dormitorios y dos baños. Mi dormitorio es pequeño pero cómodo. Hay una cama al lado de la ventana y una estantería detrás de la puerta. No hay jardín.”

[1] Vivo en + correct house description, [1] hay with plural nouns, [1] adjective agreement (grande/moderna with casa, pequeño/cómodo with dormitorio), [1] prepositions (al lado de, detrás de), [1] no hay used correctly, [1] overall accuracy and spelling.
This tests everything from Chapter 1 in one paragraph. If you can do this from memory, you own the chapter. Notice “moderna” not “moderno” because “casa” is feminine.
