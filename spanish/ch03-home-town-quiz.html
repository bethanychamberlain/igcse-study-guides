How do you say “by car,” “by bus,” and “on foot” in Spanish?
2 marks
Answer
en coche, en autobús, a pie [1 for 2 correct, 2 for all 3].
Remember: “en” for vehicles, “a pie” for walking. Never “en pie.”

3
Complete the three comparative patterns:
(a) more ... than = _______ + adjective + _______
(b) less ... than = _______ + adjective + _______
(c) as ... as = _______ + adjective + _______
2 marks
Answer
(a) más + adj + que, (b) menos + adj + que, (c) tan + adj + como [1 for 2 correct patterns, 2 for all 3].
These three patterns are the entire comparative system. If you know these, you can compare anything.

What is the difference between la librería and la biblioteca?
1 mark
Answer
La librería = bookshop (you buy books). La biblioteca = library (you borrow books) [1].
Classic false friend. “Library” looks like “librería” but it’s not.

Intermediate — Can You Use It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. Sentence-level accuracy with comparatives and directions.
5
Translate into Spanish: “Madrid is bigger than Palm Bay. Palm Bay is quieter than Madrid. My town is as pretty as your town.”
3 marks
Answer
“Madrid es más grande que Palm Bay.” [1]
“Palm Bay es más tranquilo que Madrid.” (or “menos ruidoso que”) [1]
“Mi ciudad es tan bonita como tu ciudad.” [1]
Each sentence tests a different pattern: más...que, más/menos...que, tan...como. If you got all three, you own comparatives.

6
Translate into Spanish: “The hospital is opposite the park. The church is between the museum and the square. I go to school by bus.”
3 marks
Answer
“El hospital está enfrente del parque.” [1 — must use está for location and del for de+el]
“La iglesia está entre el museo y la plaza.” [1]
“Voy al colegio en autobús.” [1 — must use al for a+el]
Three contractions tested here: del (de+el), al (a+el). Also: location always uses ESTAR.
Rate your answer:
7
Fill in with the correct irregular comparative: (a) El restaurante italiano es _______ (better) que el restaurante francés. (b) Mi hermano es _______ (older) que yo. (c) El tiempo hoy es _______ (worse) que ayer.
3 marks
Answer
(a) mejor [1], (b) mayor [1], (c) peor [1].
These are irregular — you cannot say “más bueno” or “más malo.” Mejor, peor, mayor, menor must be memorised.

Advanced — Can You Describe and Compare?

Worth 5-6 marks each. Extended writing combining town vocabulary, comparatives, perfect tense, and opinions.
8
Write a paragraph (5-6 sentences) in Spanish describing your town. Include: what places there are (hay), at least one location using está, at least one comparative, and one sentence using the perfect tense.
6 marks
Model Answer
“Vivo en Palm Bay, una ciudad pequeña en Florida. Hay muchos restaurantes y un parque grande. El parque está cerca de mi casa. Palm Bay es más tranquilo que Orlando. He vivido aquí cinco años. Me gusta mi ciudad porque es bonita y segura.”

[1] hay for existence, [1] está for location, [1] comparative (más...que), [1] perfect tense (he vivido), [1] opinion (me gusta), [1] overall accuracy and variety.
This paragraph uses four different grammar points in six sentences. That’s the kind of range the examiner rewards.

9
Form the perfect tense for each: (a) yo / visitar, (b) nosotros / comer, (c) ella / hacer, (d) ellos / ver. Then use two of them in full sentences about your town.
5 marks
Answer
(a) he visitado [½], (b) hemos comido [½], (c) ha hecho [1 — irregular!], (d) han visto [1 — irregular!].

Example sentences:
“He visitado el museo tres veces.” [1] “Hemos comido en el restaurante italiano.” [1]
The irregular ones (hecho, visto) are worth more because they test whether you’ve memorised them. “Ha hacido” or “han veido” are wrong.

10
Translate into Spanish: “My town is smaller than Madrid but it is better because it is quieter. There is a supermarket near my house. The cinema is opposite the restaurant. I have visited many cities but I prefer my town.”
6 marks
Answer
“Mi ciudad es más pequeña que Madrid pero es mejor porque es más tranquila. Hay un supermercado cerca de mi casa. El cine está enfrente del restaurante. He visitado muchas ciudades pero prefiero mi ciudad.”

[1] comparative (más pequeña que), [1] irregular comparative (mejor), [1] hay for existence, [1] está + preposition for location with del contraction, [1] perfect tense (he visitado), [1] overall accuracy and adjective agreement.
This paragraph tests comparatives (regular + irregular), hay, estar, the perfect tense, and contractions. Six grammar points = six marks.
