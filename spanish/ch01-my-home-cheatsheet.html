Key Vocabulary

Spanish What It Means Don’t Confuse With
hay there is / there are (same word for both!) está — “hay” introduces new things; “está” locates known things
la cocina the kitchen cocinar = to cook (verb, not the room)
el dormitorio the bedroom dormir = to sleep (the verb)
encima de on top of debajo de = under (exact opposite)
delante de in front of detrás de = behind (exact opposite)
del of the (de + el combined) de la — does NOT contract (de la cocina, never “dela”)
un piso a flat / apartment el suelo = the floor (the surface you walk on)

The Four Words for “The” — Definite Articles

Singular Plural
Masculine el (el salón) los (los dormitorios)
Feminine la (la cocina) las (las cocinas)
Gender Rule
Words ending in -o are usually masculine (el piso, el dormitorio). Words ending in -a are usually feminine (la casa, la cocina). Learn the exceptions as you go.

Adjective Agreement — Making Descriptions Match

Masc. Singular Fem. Singular Masc. Plural Fem. Plural
-o adjectives pequeño pequeña pequeños pequeñas
-e adjectives grande grande grandes grandes
The Rule
-o/-a adjectives: change ending to match gender AND number.
-e adjectives: only change for number (add -s), gender stays the same.

Regular Present Tense Endings — -AR, -ER, -IR

Person -AR (hablar) -ER (comer) -IR (vivir)
yo habl-o com-o viv-o
tú habl-as com-es viv-es
él / ella habl-a com-e viv-e
nosotros habl-amos com-emos viv-imos
vosotros habl-áis com-éis viv-ís
ellos / ellas habl-an com-en viv-en
Yo Form Shortcut
The yo form is always -o for all three verb types. Drop the ending, add -o. On the exam you mostly write about yourself, so this one form gets you far.

Hay vs Está — The Key Difference

HAY ESTÁ
Means There is / There are It is (located at)
Use when Introducing something new Saying where something specific is
Article hay + un/una (a) el/la + está (the)
Example “Hay un libro en la mesa.” “El libro está en la mesa.”
Quick Test
See “a” (un/una)? Use hay. See “the” (el/la)? Use está.

Say It in One Sentence

Hay One word for both “there is” and “there are” — use it to list what exists in any place.
De + el = del “al lado del salón” — this contraction is mandatory, not optional.
Adjective order In Spanish, adjectives go AFTER the noun: “una casa grande”, not “una grande casa.”
-o → -a Adjectives ending in -o change to -a for feminine nouns: bonito → bonita.
Prepositions All prepositions of place use está (ESTAR for location): “La cama está al lado de la ventana.”
Present -o The “yo” form is always -o for regular verbs: hablo, como, vivo.

Memory Tricks

Preposition Pairs
Learn them as opposites: encima (on top) vs debajo (under). delante (in front) vs detrás (behind). cerca (near) vs lejos (far). Three pairs = six prepositions learned.
Hay = “Hi, I’m New Here”
Hay introduces something for the first time, like someone saying “Hi, I’m new here.” Once you know the thing, use está to say where it is.
The DEL Rule
Think of it as a traffic merge: de and el crash together into del. Only happens with el — de la, de los, de las all stay separate.
Room Genders — Most Are Masculine
Almost every room is el: el salón, el dormitorio, el baño, el garaje, el jardín, el pasillo. The big exception is la cocina (kitchen). If in doubt, guess el.

Quick-Fire Exam Cues

If the Question Says... The Answer Is...
“Describe tu casa” (Describe your house) “Vivo en una casa grande. Hay tres dormitorios, un salón y una cocina.”
“¿Qué hay en tu dormitorio?” (What is in your bedroom?) Use hay + un/una: “Hay una cama, un armario y un escritorio.”
“¿Dónde está...?” (Where is...?) Use está + preposition: “Está al lado de la ventana.”
“¿Cómo es tu casa?” (What is your house like?) Use es (SER) + adjective: “Es grande, moderna y luminosa.”
“¿Dónde vives?” (Where do you live?) “Vivo en una casa en Palm Bay.” (vivir = -IR verb, yo form = vivo)
“¿Qué haces en casa?” (What do you do at home?) Use present tense -o forms: “Cocino, estudio y veo la televisión.”
