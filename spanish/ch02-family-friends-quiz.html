Fill in the correct possessive adjective: (a) ______ madre es médica. (my) (b) ______ hermanos son altos. (your, tú) (c) ______ casa es grande. (his/her)
2 marks
Answer
(a) Mi [½], (b) Tus [1 — must be plural because “hermanos” is plural], (c) Su [½].
The big test here is (b): “tus” not “tu” because hermanos is plural. If you wrote “tu hermanos” that’s wrong. The possessive must match the noun’s number.

4
What is the difference between tu (no accent) and tú (with accent)?
1 mark
Answer
Tu (no accent) = your (possessive adjective) [½]. Tú (with accent) = you (subject pronoun) [½].
“Tu madre” = your mother. “Tú eres alto” = you are tall. The accent makes it a completely different word.

Intermediate — Can You Use It?

Worth 3-4 marks each. Write correct sentences using multiple grammar points.
5
Translate into Spanish: “Our grandmother is very nice and generous.” Be careful with the possessive adjective.
3 marks
Answer
“Nuestra abuela es muy simpática y generosa.”
[1] Nuestra (feminine because abuela is feminine — NOT nuestro), [1] correct use of ser (personality = permanent), [1] adjective agreement (simpática and generosa both feminine).
This is the classic trap. “Nuestro” is wrong here because “abuela” is feminine. It must be “nuestra.” Only nuestro/vuestro change for gender.

6
Write a physical description of a person in Spanish (3 sentences). Use SER for build and TENER for hair and eyes. Include correct adjective agreement.
3 marks
Model Answer
“Mi madre es alta y delgada. Tiene el pelo largo y moreno. Tiene los ojos verdes.”

[1] SER used for build (es alta y delgada), [1] TENER used for hair/eyes, [1] adjective agreement (alta/delgada both feminine to match madre).
Common error: using “es” for hair (“es pelo largo”). Always use “tiene” for hair and eyes.

7
Add the personal “a” where needed: (a) Quiero _____ mi hermano. (b) Veo _____ la televisión. (c) Conozco _____ tu madre. Explain why.
3 marks
Answer
(a) Quiero a mi hermano [1] — hermano is a person.
(b) Veo la televisión [no “a”] [1] — television is NOT a person.
(c) Conozco a tu madre [1] — madre is a person.
The rule is simple: person = add “a”, thing = no “a.” The exam tests this with mix-and-match sentences.

Advanced — Can You Describe in Detail?

Worth 5-6 marks each. Extended writing combining appearance, personality, possessives, and opinion.
8
Write a paragraph (5-6 sentences) in Spanish describing a family member. Include: their name and relationship to you, physical appearance (SER + TENER), personality (SER), and your opinion of them.
6 marks
Model Answer
“Mi hermana se llama María. Es alta y delgada. Tiene el pelo largo y rubio y los ojos azules. Es muy simpática y sociable. A veces es un poco desordenada. Me llevo bien con mi hermana porque es graciosa.”

[1] name + relationship, [1] SER for build, [1] TENER for hair/eyes, [1] SER for personality with agreement, [1] opinion phrase (me llevo bien con), [1] overall accuracy and variety.
This is the full IGCSE pattern: relationship + appearance + personality + opinion. Practice this format and you can use it for any “describe a person” question.

9
Translate into Spanish: “My best friend is a good person. His parents are very hardworking. Their house is near our school. I love my best friend because he is funny and intelligent.”
5 marks
Answer
“Mi mejor amigo es una buena persona. Sus padres son muy trabajadores. Su casa está cerca de nuestro colegio. Quiero a mi mejor amigo porque es gracioso e inteligente.”

[1] multiple possessives (mi, sus, su, nuestro) used correctly, [1] “buena persona” (not “buen” — persona is feminine), [1] está for location, [1] personal “a” before “mi mejor amigo,” [1] y → e before “inteligente.”
This tests five grammar points in one paragraph: possessives, adjective position, ser vs estar, personal a, and the y→e rule. Each one is a mark.

10
Fill in every blank with the correct possessive adjective. Then translate the whole paragraph into English.

“______ (My) familia es pequeña. Vivo con ______ (my) padres y ______ (my) hermano. ______ (Our) casa está en Palm Bay. ______ (His) dormitorio es grande pero ______ (my) dormitorio es pequeño. ______ (Their) abuelos viven en otra ciudad.”
5 marks
Answer
Mi familia, mis padres, mi hermano, Nuestra casa, Su dormitorio, mi dormitorio, Sus abuelos [3 marks for fill-ins — 1 for each pair correct].

Translation: “My family is small. I live with my parents and my brother. Our house is in Palm Bay. His bedroom is big but my bedroom is small. Their grandparents live in another city.” [2 marks for translation].
The traps: “mis padres” (plural!), “nuestra casa” (feminine — not nuestro), “sus abuelos” (plural). Each one tests whether you go beyond just “mi.”
