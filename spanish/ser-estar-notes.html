Don't Confuse — This Is What You Got Wrong on the Easter Test

On your Easter test, you scored 0% on ser vs estar. Not because you left it blank — because you have them completely reversed.

Here is exactly what you did:

• You wrote “está” for identity and profession — WRONG. Identity = SER (es)
• You wrote “es” for location — WRONG. Location = ESTAR (está)

You also rated yourself 5 out of 5 confidence on this topic. That confidence is the problem — it means you won’t catch the mistake on your own. So let’s fix it right now.

W
Write This Down — Tattoo This on Your Brain

SER = What something IS (identity, nature, permanent things)
ESTAR = Where something IS or how it FEELS right now (location, temporary states)

“Él es profesor.” — He IS a teacher. (identity = SER)
“Él está en Madrid.” — He IS in Madrid. (location = ESTAR)

Section 1 SER — “Superman’s Essential Reality”
Use SER for things that are permanent, essential, or defining. Think: if it won’t change tomorrow, use SER.
W
Write This Down — Identity & Nationality

SER tells you WHO or WHAT someone/something is.

“Soy estudiante.” — I am a student.
“Ella es mexicana.” — She is Mexican.
“Somos americanos.” — We are American.

W
Write This Down — Profession

What you DO for a living = SER (it defines you).

“Mi tía es profesora.” — My aunt is a teacher.
“Son médicos.” — They are doctors.

W
Write This Down — Permanent Characteristics

Physical traits and personality that don’t change day to day = SER.

“Es alto.” — He is tall.
“Soy inteligente.” — I am intelligent.
“La casa es grande.” — The house is big.

W
Write This Down — Time, Dates & Origin

Telling the time, saying where something is FROM, and what something is MADE OF = SER.

“Son las tres.” — It is three o’clock.
“Es de México.” — He/She is from Mexico.
“La mesa es de madera.” — The table is made of wood.
“Hoy es lunes.” — Today is Monday.

Why This Matters

On the IGCSE 0530, ser vs estar appears in fill-in-the-blank, translation, and writing tasks. Getting these wrong doesn’t just lose you the grammar mark — it can make your whole sentence mean something different.

  • I know SER is for identity, nationality, and profession
  • I know SER is for permanent characteristics (tall, big, clever)
  • I know SER is for time, dates, origin, and material
Section 2 ESTAR — “Emotional, Temporary And Right-now”
Use ESTAR for things that are temporary, changeable, or about where something is located right now. Think: if it could change tomorrow, use ESTAR.
W
Write This Down — Location (Where Things Are)

ESTAR tells you WHERE someone or something is. Always. Every time.

“Madrid está en España.” — Madrid is in Spain.
“Estoy en casa.” — I am at home.
“¿Dónde está el libro?” — Where is the book?

!
Don't Confuse — Location Is ALWAYS Estar

Even if a city has been in the same place for 1000 years, you still say “Madrid está en España”. Location = ESTAR, no exceptions. You wrote “es” for location on your test. That is always wrong.

W
Write This Down — Temporary States & Feelings

How someone FEELS right now, or a condition that will change = ESTAR.

“Estoy contento.” — I am happy (right now).
“Está cansada.” — She is tired.
“El café está caliente.” — The coffee is hot (right now — it will cool down).
“La puerta está abierta.” — The door is open (someone could close it).

W
Write This Down — Progressive Tenses (-ando / -iendo)

When something is happening RIGHT NOW, use ESTAR + present participle.

“Está lloviendo.” — It is raining.
“Estoy estudiando.” — I am studying.
“Estamos comiendo.” — We are eating.

?
Why This Matters

The IGCSE writing and speaking tasks often ask you to describe what you’re doing on holiday, how you feel, or where you are. Every one of those uses ESTAR. Get this wrong and the examiner knows your grammar foundations are shaky.

  • I know ESTAR is for location — always, even for permanent places
  • I know ESTAR is for feelings and temporary conditions
  • I know ESTAR is used with -ando/-iendo for things happening right now

T
Study Tip — Memory Tricks

SER = “Superman’s Essential Reality”
Think of Superman — he IS Kryptonian (identity), he IS strong (permanent trait), he IS Clark Kent (profession). These things define him. They don’t change.

ESTAR = “Emotional, Temporary And Right-now”
Think of how you feel right now — you’re tired, you’re at school, you’re studying. All of that will change by tonight.

!
Don't Confuse — The One Exception That Trips Everyone Up

“Where you are FROM” uses SER — “Soy de Florida”
“Where you ARE right now” uses ESTAR — “Estoy en Dinamarca”

Origin (where you come from) = SER. Current location (where you are) = ESTAR. Don’t mix these up.

Section 4 Adjectives That Change Meaning with Ser/Estar
Some adjectives mean something completely different depending on whether you use SER or ESTAR. This is an IGCSE favourite.

Adjective With SER (permanent) With ESTAR (right now)

Why This Matters

Imagine writing on the exam: “Mi profesor está aburrido” — you just said your teacher is bored in class. What you meant was “Mi profesor es aburrido” — your teacher is a boring person. The examiner will mark you wrong and wonder what you actually meant.

T
Study Tip — The Quick Test

Ask yourself: “Is this about what the person/thing IS, or how they FEEL right now?”

• “He is boring” = what he IS = SER
• “He is bored” = how he FEELS = ESTAR
• “She is clever” = what she IS = SER
• “She is ready” = her state right NOW = ESTAR

  • I can explain the difference between “es aburrido” and “está aburrido”
  • I know that “está malo” means sick, not bad
  • I know that “está listo” means ready, not clever

T
Study Tip — Notice the Accents

ESTAR has accents on almost every form except “estoy” and “estamos”. If you forget the accent, it’s a spelling error on the exam. Write them out by hand 5 times — your hand will remember.
