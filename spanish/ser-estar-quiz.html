Foundation — Fill in Ser or Estar (Conjugated)

Choose the correct verb AND conjugate it for the subject. Say WHY to yourself before checking.
1
Fill in the blank with the correct form of ser or estar:
“Mi hermano ___ médico.” (My brother ___ a doctor.)
2 marks
Answer
es (SER, third person singular)
WHY: Profession — being a doctor is what he does, it’s his identity. Identity and profession = SER. Always. [1 mark for correct verb, 1 mark for correct conjugation]
This is exactly the type of question you got wrong on the Easter test. You wrote “está” — that would mean “my brother is located a doctor” which makes no sense.

2
Fill in the blank:
“El supermercado ___ en la calle principal.” (The supermarket ___ on the main street.)
2 marks
Answer
está (ESTAR, third person singular)
WHY: Location — we are saying WHERE the supermarket is. Location = ESTAR. Every single time, no exceptions. [1 mark for correct verb, 1 mark for correct conjugation]
On the Easter test, you wrote “es” for location. Remember: “en” (in/on/at) is your signal for ESTAR.

3
Fill in the blank:
“Nosotros ___ muy contentos hoy.” (We ___ very happy today.)
2 marks
Answer
estamos (ESTAR, first person plural)
WHY: Feelings right now — “happy today” is a temporary emotion, not a permanent personality trait. The word “hoy” (today) confirms it’s temporary. Temporary feelings = ESTAR. [1 mark for correct verb, 1 mark for correct conjugation]
Key word: “hoy” (today) tells you it’s temporary → ESTAR.

4
Fill in the blank:
“___ las ocho de la mañana.” ( ___ eight in the morning.)
2 marks
Answer
Son (SER, third person plural)
WHY: Telling the time = SER. Always. The verb is plural (“son”) because “las ocho” (eight o’clock) is plural. Only “la una” (one o’clock) uses “es”. [1 mark for correct verb, 1 mark for correct conjugation]
Time = SER. “Es la una” (1:00) but “Son las dos/tres/cuatro...” (2:00+).

Intermediate — Choose, Explain, and Translate

You need to pick the right verb AND explain your reasoning. Marks for explanation, not just the answer.
5
Choose ser or estar and explain why:
“Carlos ___ de Argentina, pero ahora ___ en Londres.” (Carlos ___ from Argentina, but now he ___ in London.)
4 marks
Answer
“Carlos es de Argentina, pero ahora está en Londres.”

First blank — “es” (SER): “De Argentina” = origin, where he is FROM. Origin is permanent — he will always be from Argentina. Origin = SER. [1 mark verb + 1 mark explanation]

Second blank — “está” (ESTAR): “En Londres” = his current location, where he IS right now. Location = ESTAR. The word “ahora” (now) confirms it’s about right now. [1 mark verb + 1 mark explanation]
This sentence is the classic ser/estar test. “De” (from) = SER. “En” (in) = ESTAR. If you got both right, you understand the core rule.

6
Translate into Spanish. Use the correct form of ser or estar and explain your choice:
“My parents are tall and my sister is tired.”
4 marks
Answer
“Mis padres son altos y mi hermana está cansada.”

“son altos” (SER): Being tall is a permanent physical characteristic. It won’t change tomorrow. Permanent traits = SER. Plural because “padres” = they. [1 mark verb + 1 mark explanation]

“está cansada” (ESTAR): Being tired is a temporary state — she’ll feel different after sleeping. Temporary feelings = ESTAR. Note “cansada” (feminine) because it’s “hermana”. [1 mark verb + 1 mark explanation]
Bonus: notice “altos” (masculine plural, agrees with “padres”) and “cansada” (feminine singular, agrees with “hermana”). Adjective agreement matters too.

7
What is the difference in meaning between these two sentences? Explain in English.
a) “María es aburrida.”
b) “María está aburrida.”
4 marks
Answer
a) “María es aburrida” = María IS a boring person. [1 mark meaning] SER + aburrida describes her permanent personality — she is always dull and uninteresting. [1 mark explanation]

b) “María está aburrida” = María IS bored (right now). [1 mark meaning] ESTAR + aburrida describes her temporary feeling — she feels bored at this moment, but that will change. [1 mark explanation]
This is the exam’s favourite ser/estar question. SER + adjective = what the person IS like. ESTAR + adjective = how the person FEELS now.

8
Write a short paragraph (4-5 sentences) in Spanish describing yourself: who you are, where you are from, where you are right now, how you feel, and what you are doing. Use both ser and estar at least twice each.
6 marks
Example Answer
“Soy estudiante y soy de Florida. Soy alto y amable. Ahora estoy en Dinamarca. Estoy un poco cansado pero contento. Estoy estudiando español.”

SER used for: profession (estudiante), origin (de Florida), permanent traits (alto y amable) [1 mark each correct use]
ESTAR used for: location (en Dinamarca), temporary feelings (cansado, contento), progressive tense (estudiando) [1 mark each correct use]
Your answer doesn’t need to match this exactly. You get marks for: correct choice of ser/estar [2], correct conjugation [2], making sense [1], and using accents properly [1].

9
Explain the difference in meaning for each pair. Write the English meaning and say whether it’s SER (permanent) or ESTAR (right now).
a) “Pedro es listo” vs “Pedro está listo”
b) “La fruta es verde” vs “La fruta está verde”
c) “Juan es malo” vs “Juan está malo”
6 marks
Answer
a) “Pedro es listo” = Pedro is clever/smart (SER — permanent trait, he’s always clever). [1 mark]
“Pedro está listo” = Pedro is ready (ESTAR — temporary state, he’s ready right now). [1 mark]

b) “La fruta es verde” = The fruit is green in colour (SER — permanent characteristic, it’s a green type of fruit). [1 mark]
“La fruta está verde” = The fruit is unripe (ESTAR — temporary condition, it will ripen). [1 mark]

c) “Juan es malo” = Juan is a bad person (SER — that’s his character). [1 mark]
“Juan está malo” = Juan is sick/ill (ESTAR — temporary health condition, he’ll get better). [1 mark]
1 mark per correct meaning. The examiner wants the English translation AND the reason (permanent vs temporary). Don’t just write the meaning — say why.

10
Each sentence below has an error with ser or estar. Find the error, correct it, and explain why the original is wrong.
a) “Mi casa está grande y bonita.”
b) “Los niños son en el parque.”
c) “Ella es muy cansada después del partido.”
6 marks
Answer
a) Error: “está” should be “es”.
Corrected: “Mi casa es grande y bonita.”
WHY: Being big and pretty are permanent characteristics of the house. They describe what the house IS like, not a temporary condition. Permanent traits = SER. [1 mark correction + 1 mark explanation]

b) Error: “son” should be “están”.
Corrected: “Los niños están en el parque.”
WHY: “En el parque” = location. We are saying WHERE the children are. Location = ESTAR. Always. The word “en” is the giveaway. [1 mark correction + 1 mark explanation]

c) Error: “es” should be “está”.
Corrected: “Ella está muy cansada después del partido.”
WHY: Being tired after a match is a temporary state — she won’t be tired forever. “Después del partido” (after the match) confirms it’s temporary. Temporary feelings = ESTAR. [1 mark correction + 1 mark explanation]
Error-correction questions appear on every IGCSE paper. The technique: (1) identify the wrong verb, (2) write the correct one, (3) explain WHY using the ser/estar rules. All three parts are needed for full marks.
